What is the license for the content on here (help.openstreetmap.org) ?

4

There's some great answers here on help.openstreetmap.org and I think it's a great idea to add some of this information to the OSM wiki (especially the FAQ page). But:

(All openstreetmap.org wiki contents (including images) are released with the same license as the rest of our data; OpenStreetMap License (cc-by-sa-2), except on a few pages where we have stated otherwise)

Would I (or anyone else) be able (legally) to add and modify portions of content from here (help.openstreetmap.org) to http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/ ?

(I am aware that the pending ODbL does not affect the content of wiki or this website).

In the footer on every page on help.openstreetmap.org and wiki.openstreetmap.org you see . That means that you are free to copy any content betwean theese sites as long as you attribute the source. Just add it to the editing comment or as a comment.

ODbL is not considered for anything but the main database and the FAQ needs to be changed during the licence change.

Just a side-note to @Gnonthgol's ccbysa 2.0 summary ("That means that you are free to copy any content betwean theese sites as long as you attribute the source."): this is not correct. Gnonthgol's comment is right: he said "between these sites", referring to the wiki and help.osm.org. You can of course copy material between two CC-BY-SA-licensed sites with impunity; the licence is compatible with itself.
