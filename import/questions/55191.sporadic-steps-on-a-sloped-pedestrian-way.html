Sporadic steps on a sloped pedestrian way

1

What could be the best approach for tagging steps that go on a sloped pedestrian way but that only show up ocassionally?

  • alternate highway=pedestrian and highway=steps for each respective section?
  • tag the entire way as highway=pedestrian regardless of the steps?
  • tag the entire way as highway=steps regardless of the walk sections?
  • draw a highway=pedestrian + area=yes, and then draw a highway=footway alternating with highway=steps for each section?
  • tag it highway=footway and alternate with highway=steps, even though the way is not just a "footway" but a wider street where 3 or maybe 4 cars fit well (if parked side to side and excluding the wheelchair ramps) but it's not meant for motorized vehicles?

I might be overthinking a little.

On the pedestrian ways I've tagged (width less than 2 meters and usually closer to 1 meter) I usually put short sections of "highway=steps" along with "step_count=*" (if I've counted them) where the steps are and "highway=footway" or "highway=path" for the rest (depending the general character with urban/suburban more likely to be footway and mountain hiking ways more likely to be path).

But you are describing something wide enough for four cars to fit. I've never tagged such a wide pedestrian way. I suppose one could simply tag as a way with a "width=*" value instead of tagging as an area. Not sure how I'd tag an area with steps as I don't see a way to indicate which is up versus down while there is a convention for that on a way.
