I want to see, is there any effect of changing EIGRP k values on packet travel time from one host to another..

In your environment, the packet will take 3 hops, no matter how you route it, so I don't see a reason why it should take longer in one of the scenarios, unless you add artificial delay on one of the links.

Furthermore: within a simulated environment, the routing will be too fast to measure any real delay, unless you add artificial delay on one of the links.

So, how would you measure the delay, if there was one:

  • synchronize the time on your hosts
  • capture in parallel on both hosts
  • send the test packet
  • stop capturing
  • compare the time stamps in both capture files
  • don't overly trust a very small delta of the time stamps, as you will never be able to synchronize the hosts to the micro second!!
