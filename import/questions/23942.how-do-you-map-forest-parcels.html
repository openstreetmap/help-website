This is quite a common problem, but has not been fully resolved for tagging.

I would recommend reading the following two wiki pages (and their discussion pages) to get some ideas: man_made=cutline and place=section. A cutline will typically be used to mark parcel boundaries, whereas there is no common way of marking the actual parcel numbers (except that there is widespread agreement that they should be placed in a ref tag). In many European countries the parcel numbers are displayed prominently at the corners of the parcel and are frequently shown on maps (e.g., IGN Top25 Foret de Fontainebleau, Białowieża Primeval Forest MAP). In large homogeneous forest stands they are an important navigation aid.

The general idea is that the parcels are delineated by natural or artificial features (roads, cutlines, rivers) and that a relation is created to mark the polygon which is the forest parcel. What we have not yet agreed is a common way to tag these polygons (the purpose of the proposed section tag).
