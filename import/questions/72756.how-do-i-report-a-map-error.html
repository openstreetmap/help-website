One other thing that's probably worth mentioning, as https://www.openstreetmap.org/about says, is that OpenStreetMap is created by local people with local knowledge, so if you know that something's wrong, you're probably the person who is best placed to fix it. Some things might be tricky to fix, but some of the street name issues you mention in your notes might be easier. If you hit the "edit" button at the top of the screen you'll be taken through an "introduction to editing"; there's also help here and here.

I suspect that https://nominatim.openstreetmap.org/search.php?q=springdale+oregon&polygon_geojson=1&viewbox= and https://nominatim.openstreetmap.org/search.php?q=troutdale&polygon_geojson=1&viewbox= explain what's happening - Troutdale is defined (perhaps incorrectly) as an area, and Springdale as a node. Because your address isn't within the Troutdale that OSM has, it picks the next nearest, which is Springdale.
