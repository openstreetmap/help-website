While reading more about Notes I found the answer on its wiki page.

You can subscribe to an RSS feed of all the note activity in your area. This allows you to keep an overview of the things reported in your area you map in. The URL for the RSS feed is

http://api.openstreetmap.org/api/0.6/notes/feed?bbox=smaller_longitude,smaller_latitude,larger_longitude,larger_latitude

You can do that very easy with the quality assurance tool.
http://tyrasd.github.io/osm-qa-feeds/ Select your region, and choose the tool you want to have an RSS feed for.

a) OSM Notes (User generated bug reports from osm.org.)
b) WhoDid It (Lists changesets that affect the watched region.)
c) Newest OSM (Lists new contributors.)
d) Keep Right! (Reports a large variety of automatically detected errors.)
e) Like OSM-notes, but deprecated
f) ITO OSM Mapper (Displays mapping activity (free sign up required))
