You'll have to try for yourself - it is hard for us to know what your requirements are. Traditionally, QGis comes from the Geodata processing side, offering all sorts of analytical functionality, whereas Maperitive specialised on rendering. You are unlikely to get an aesthetically pleasing result from either; both programs can be used to do the grunt work of making a map from the data but you will very likely want to post-process that map into something nice by applying manual work. For this, many people seem to use the Maperitive-SVG-Illustrator toolchain.
