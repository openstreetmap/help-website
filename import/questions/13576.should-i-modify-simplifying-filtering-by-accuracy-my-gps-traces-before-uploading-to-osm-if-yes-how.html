Should I modify (Simplifying, filtering by accuracy) my GPS traces before uploading to OSM ? If yes, how ?

(For example, here's one trace - http://www.openstreetmap.org/user/skorasaurus/traces/1253111 where in some areas the trace appears to be very 'bunched together').

Since we only use GPS traces as source material (and evidence), you don't need to tidy up your traces before uploading. People who are familiar with the area will be able to spot when the traces are wildly inaccurate, so a certain amount of inaccuracy won't cause a problem. Equally, when multiple traces of the same roads and paths are uploaded, it's easy to see where they coincide and where they don't. Providing yours isn't the only trace in the area, and the only form of suitable source material, and someone unfamiliar with the area doesn't choose to use it to armchair map, it'll be fine.

That said, if you have the time and inclination to remove points with a high Dilution of Precision, it's not going to do any harm, for much the same reasons as above.

You may want to cut traces before they enter private property, your home, friend's homes or other areas that are not open access. I do not see any need to upload data that is not needed in OSM and only takes up storage space. The "bunched together bits" can some times be good to pinpoint a park bench or other place you stopped at and can see on bing and use check alignment.
