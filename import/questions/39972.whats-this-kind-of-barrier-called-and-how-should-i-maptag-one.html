I'd artificially split the path so that parallel paths go through each barrier, and I'd map the right-hand one as a horse_stile. It looks like it's combined with a gate, but I personally wouldn't map the "gate-ness" of it unless the gate could be opened to allow wide motor vehicle access.

For the left-hand part I'm not sure that cycle_barrier is "wrong", but it's arguably not as "correct" as motorcycle_barrier - I suspect that there's useful discussion to be had about that. I'm not a cyclist myself so it'd be useful to get input from people who are (or who use or create cycle routers) as to at what point a cycle barrier stops being one and becomes a motorcycle barrier.

*Hi, Thank you for your email. Please see the link below, this should provide all the information you require about barriers: https://www.sustrans.org.uk/sites/default/files/file_content_type/access_control_guide_jan_2012.pdf

Also for reference, please see below a link to our position on access controls on paths. As organisation we believe accessibility for all is of upmost importance. There should be a presumption for paths to be open to all legitimate users with minimal or no access controls. If some form of access control is necessary, a single row of bollards (or other features, such as rocks or planters) leaving 1.5m gaps and with clear visibility of other users can be effective in many locations. https://www.sustrans.org.uk/our-position/access-controls-paths

Many thanks NNN
