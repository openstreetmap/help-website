Bus Routes or Bus Services?

3

I have just started using OSM in Amersfoort, Netherlands, and the bus route information is completely out of date - it seems to have been taken from an old map. It has bus stops in my road (which are not there) serving routes which no longer exist.

OSM seems to use the actual route of the bus to hold most of the information, not the stops themselves. Although this might be interesting for bus companies, I don't really care which ways the buses drive down, I only want to know where to catch a bus, and where it is going. Unfortunately this is not shown against bus stops on the transport layer, although some of the information is held in the relation.

I can find the current routes and link them to the appropriate bus stops but I am not prepared to spend several weeks riding the buses to find out which roads they actually go down.

I have three questions:

  1. Is it worth setting up relations for the new routes if I only attach them to bus stops, or should I leave it to someone more dedicated to do the whole job?
  2. How should I note an expiry date on the routes: the current timetables are apparently valid until the end of 2014.
  3. Finally, is it OK to delete all the non-existent bus routes and bus stops or are they there for some historical reason?

Thanks

Mark

In reverse order:

  • Yes, feel free to delete non-existent or otherwise outdated information.
  • I have no suggestions on this, but if you add the information make it clear that it's valid until at least that date, and not that it should be removed on that date.
  • It's common to do partial work in OSM, and encouraged if it is useful and doesn't make anyone else's life harder. So I'd suggest adding the bits that you know of the routes, and other people can extend them. Waiting on one person doing an entire route might be a long wait.
