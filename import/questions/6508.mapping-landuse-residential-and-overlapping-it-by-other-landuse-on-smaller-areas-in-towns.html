Think of landuse=residential as "this is a residential area". A residential area may comprise a whole city quarter; a residential area can contain parks, playgrounds, trees, and roads of course; even the occasional shop doesn't turn a residential area into a retail or commercial area.

This means that one would not normally use landuse=residential to denote only the area where a house and garden are; it will usually cover whole blocks. Also, a landuse=residential will not end on one side of the road and then begin again on the other; it will contain the road - unless perhaps the road is a 8-lane motorway.

Personally I don't see a problem with a smaller area (e.g. leisure=playground or landuse=grass) being drawn "on top" of the landuse=residential area, but some mappers would also in these cases create a multipolygon and make the smaller area a "hole" in the landuse area. That's basically a matter of personal taste.

I don't see any problems with mapping in this way. Whether to snap landuse polygons to highways is up to you, most mapping near me does not snap the polygons to highways.

In the UK there are quite a lot of places where some overlap is used with a big residential area and then smaller places like grass, cemetery, recreation_ground are done with overlapping landuse. Having a partial overlap is bad though, the smaller landuse areas are basically like implicit multipolygons.

So your mapping looks great, I would continue in the same way.
