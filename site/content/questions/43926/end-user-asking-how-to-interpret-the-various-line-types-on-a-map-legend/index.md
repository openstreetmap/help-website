<p>If you're zoomed in far enough you can also use the "?" button and then click on a feature in the map. This will yield a list of things that are present at that location - could be just the thing you clicked on, or a couple more things in the vicinity. Hovering over items in the list will highlight them. Click on the item that represents the map feature you are interested in, and you will see the its "tags" - the attributes that describe what sort of thing it is. Not all of them will immediately make sense to you (for example, the linestyle of <code>highway=track</code> might differ between <code>tracktype=grade1</code> and <code>tracktype=grade3</code>) but if you then follow links into the Wiki, you should be able to find out more than you ever wanted to know.</p>

<ul>
<li>Purple dashes (image 1) are boundaries, e.g. state boundaries.</li>
<li>Pink lozenges (image 2) mean 'private access'.</li>
<li>Brown dashes (images 2 and 3) mean 'track'.</li>
<li>White, 'cased' lines (shown in image 2 with pink lozenges superimposed) are just roads.</li>
</ul>
<p>You may want to consider using a service that has dedicated bike cartography made from OpenStreetMap data, rather than the all-purpose standard OpenStreetMap style. <a href="http://cycle.travel/map">cycle.travel</a> is one such site (disclaimer - my site!).</p>
