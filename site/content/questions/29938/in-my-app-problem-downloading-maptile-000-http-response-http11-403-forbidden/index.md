<div class="answer-body">
<p>(In addition to what Simon Poole says) - you've probably got something like this in your code:</p>
<pre><code>    mapView.setTileSource(TileSourceFactory.MAPNIK);</code></pre>
<p>if you change it to a different tile provider such as:</p>
<pre><code>    mapView.setTileSource(TileSourceFactory.MAPQUESTOSM);</code></pre>
<p>it'll work. You must, of course, adhere to the terms of use of whatever tiles you are using, which in the case of these Mapquest open tiles seems to be <a href="http://developer.mapquest.com/web/info/terms-of-use">here</a>.</p>
<p>The source, FWIW, seems to be <a href="http://code.google.com/p/osmdroid/source/browse/trunk/osmdroid-android/src/org/osmdroid/tileprovider/tilesource/TileSourceFactory.java?r=622">here</a>.</p>
<p>I guess that you could also overload TileSourceFactory and define a source of other tiles too, such as some you've created yourself via something like <a href="http://switch2osm.org/serving-tiles/">switch2osm</a>.</p>
</div>
