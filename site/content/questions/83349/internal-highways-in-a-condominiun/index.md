<p>In Colombia, we call "conjunto cerrado" to a set of buildings or houses inside a barrier, with an only entrance area/door with a guardian. This could be called a condominium in English, but I wanted to explain before to prevent assumptions.</p>
<p>Well, those condominiums usually have internal highways that lead to parking spaces. I want to know how to exactly map them.</p>
<ul>
<li>These highways are just like <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:highway%3Dresidential">residential</a> highways, but we use to map as residential the public highways that have associated names/ref; because these internal highways are private we are thinking of something else.</li>
<li>We also thought to tag them as <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:highway%3Dliving_street">living streets</a>, but the same happens here. Living streets are public, with some legal stuff to call them like this, and the blue signal.</li>
<li>Last option is <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:highway%3Dservice">service</a>, and we are currently using this, but not really sure. The definition says these are for driveways, alleys, or parking, but none of the examples really fit in the definition of highways inside a "Conjunto cerrado".</li>
</ul>
<p>There is a current discussion around this, and I would like to see what other mappers think or propose to standardize the <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Colombia/Guide_for_mapping">mapping in Colombia</a>. The <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Highway_tagging_samples/urban">examples proposed here</a> also does not fit our issue.</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>For larger gated communities where there are essentially a network of normal residential streets behind a gate the usual mapping would be to use <code>highway=residential</code> and add <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:access"><code>access=private</code>/<code>access=no</code> or <code>access=destination</code></a> as appropriate.</p>
<p>In your example they look to me like <code>highway=service</code> with <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:service%3Dparking_aisle"><code>service=parking_aisle</code></a> and <code>access=private</code>/<code>access=no</code> or <code>access=destination</code> as appropriate. The <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:barrier%3Dgate"><code>barrier=gate</code></a> can be mapped as well as a point on the highway, again with the appropriate access tag.</p>
