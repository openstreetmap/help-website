<p>Hi,</p>
<p>I cannot find an appropriate tag for halls that are available for holding a big event, such as banquets, weddings, conventions, seminars, exhibitions and/or flash sales.</p>
<p>Note that all large hotels have such halls; but they are not mapped separately. But if these halls are "stand alone" entities, we need to map them.</p>
<p>They cannot be labeled as "Town Halls" because of two reasons: (a) every city has several such private banquet halls, but only ONE official Town Hall. (b) These are private halls for hire, not meant for public events.</p>
<p>Similarly, they cannot be labeled as "Community Center" because they are commercial ventures, not a community asset.</p>

<p>It depends on what you mean by "public". In Belgium, we have many such halls that are operated by a local government, but you can't just go in. An organisation or person must rent the hall, and use it for what they want (a party, an amateur theater, a banquet, ...). We usually tag these as community center. But they're direct competition to privately operated halls. So I guess we also have a big number of private halls tagged as community center. The private halls usually just offer a bit more service (like a staffed kitchen instead of an empty one).</p>

<p>Talking about India. We have plenty of stand-alone halls that are mainly used for conducting the actual wedding ceremony (marriage) or related functions, such as engagement.</p>
<p>But they are very rarely used for other purposes, such as exhibitions.</p>
<p>Therefore the tag should reflect its general purpose, or at minimum the "wedding" keyword, because most Indians would be searching for this keyword.</p>
<p>[EDIT] I checked the tag you referred to, and found a link from there to <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Proposed_features/Events_centre">Events Centre</a>. That fits my needs exactly. I wish this proposal goes through!</p>

<p>Oops! You are right: Our wedding hall is not an "event center".</p>
<p>The Indian wedding is typically attended by 500-1000 invited guests. At one end, there is a stage where the couple is getting married/engaged. The others sit in the main hall. Then food is served in a separate area (or at the far end of the same hall).</p>
<p>Each event is typically a half-day affair.</p>
<p>The marriage as a whole may have up to five such events on different days.</p>

<p>By all means tag the venue but keep in mind that OSM is not a yellow pages directory.</p>
<p>Our main aim is to map geo stuff and not enable people to find a nice wedding venue - there should be other, specialist web sites for that, which will hopefully also have photos, information about seating and capacity, and so on. OSM can serve as the backdrop for such specialist sites but in order for that to work, mapping the address should be totally sufficient.</p>
<p>There's previous discussion on wedding receptions <a href="https://lists.openstreetmap.org/pipermail/tagging/2015-July/025563.html">on the tagging mailing list.</a></p>
