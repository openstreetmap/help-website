<p>I been adding the bus stop names for omnitrans in the san bernardino valley. I noticed that omnitrans names bus stops as so, "Sierra @baseline SB FS". The FS stands for farside station. But some bus stops have the farside station indicator, when they are located nearside. should I change it to just Sierra @ Baseline southbound Farside, or Sierra @ Baseline southbound Farside Station. Or should I just leave the bus stops as Sierra @ Baseline Southbound and only add the farside/nearside indicator when there are stops on both sides of the cross street? I just don't see how adding farside/nearside station would be useful with most stops. Guidance would be appreciated</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>What's the sign at the stop say? That's your name. Be aware that in the US, it's very unusual for most stops to get named if it's not a BRT or rail system outside of maybe major stops and bus stations. The usual case in the US is only to number stops, which goes in ref=*</p>
<p>If you're pulling names from the GTFS, you're doing it wrong. Use the ground truth.</p>
