<div class="answer-body">
<p>The buildings <a href="http://osm.org/go/b_TNykJPY--">you have mapped</a> are tagged as building=residential and building=apartments and in the default rendering you see at www.osm.org these deliberately render without the line around them to make other buildings stand out more, such as in <a href="http://osm.org/go/0EHmQreUC--">this example</a> where you can see which buildings are residential and which aren't quite easily.</p>
<p>Anyway, your tagging is correct, but I think you would prefer the rendering to be different. I'm one of those who like it like it is currently; there are lots of things the example rendering on the site doesn't include, but the great thing about OSM is anyone can use the data to render things how they want (see for example <a href="http://switch2osm.org"></a><a href="http://switch2osm.org">http://switch2osm.org</a> )</p>
<p>Your Paris example seems to use building=yes for all the buildings, presumably as they were imported and not enough is known about them to tag them as anything more specific (or they were imported before anything other than building=yes was widely used).</p>
</div>
