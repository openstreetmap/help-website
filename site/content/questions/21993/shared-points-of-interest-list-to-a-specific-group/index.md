<div class="answer-body">
<p>To give a general answer:</p>
<p>the OSM data is a kind of open data according to its licence. So you can do whatever you want with the OSM geodata as long as you do not break the licence.</p>
<p>And there are many opensource frameworks around that demonstrate how you can process the OSM data and how it is displayed etc.</p>
<p>Have an intensive look e.g. at the <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org">OSM wiki</a> to find collections of websites and webservices dealing with OSM data in a special way.</p>
<p>BUT: "Sharing points of interest list to a specific group" is not a purpose of the OSM database or the main OSM website at osm.org</p>
