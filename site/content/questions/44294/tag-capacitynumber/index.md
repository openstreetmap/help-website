<div class="answer-body">
<p>You clearly <strong>should not</strong> use the capacity tag which will usually refer to a design maximum and is generally used for car parks, chairlifts etc when the phrase "xxx has a capacity of n" would be widely understood. Many roads/bridges etc have a design capacity which is regularly exceeded. You are looking for a tag to describe usage.</p>
<p>Reusing existing tags for a different meaning generally makes both the new &amp; the older meanings harder to interpret &amp; use.</p>
<p>Note: I came across capacity being used instead of count only this morning.</p>

<p>Under no circumstances should you add information from the Highway Capacity Manual to OpenStreetMap because it is "© Copyright 2010-2014 Transportation Research Board of the National Academy of Sciences" and their site says "This page and its contents are not to be reproduced without express written consent of the Transportation Research Board."</p>
<p>This data is not compatible with OSM's license unless you obtain a permission (or the printed book is under a suitable open license).</p>
<p>Even if it were legal to add the information, it would still be questionable in terms of OpenStreetMap's <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Verifiability">Verifiability requirement.</a></p>

<p>Hey again! Thanks for the suggestions and comments. I discussed with my colleagues and unfortunately, traffic_hourly is a rather difficult metric than capacity in transportation. Capacity is a very common metric in transportation analysis and it represents the maximum number of vehicles that can pass in a time interval from a link, without congestion. The values used are appropriate and actually based on the Highway Capacity Manual 2010 (HCM, 2010). I think that a traffic_capacity tag might be necessary. I am going to start a suggestion process for that. In the meanwhile, is it possible to use the "traffic_capacity" tag?</p>
<p>I hope I am not causing any inconvenience. Although I think that many transport related organization would benefit from such a tag, if you still believe it to be a problem I will of course store them locally and connect them to the OSM using ID.</p>
<p>Reference: Highway Capacity Manual. "Highway capacity manual." Washington, DC (2010)</p>
