<p>Quick &amp; dirty reply presuming you are using Potlatch 2 as your editor:</p>
<p>1 Connect GPS to PC and open OSM<br />
2 Click on 'GPS Traces' tab<br />
3 Click on 'Upload a trace' link<br />
4 Click 'Choose file' button and locate your file<br />
5 Complete Description and tags then select Visibility you want<br />
6 Click 'Upload' button to upload your file to the server (may take a while to be published - you'll get an email when done)</p>
<p>When done:<br />
7 Click on Edit tab in OSM<br />
8 Click 'GPS data' dropdown button in blue bar at top of map and select My tracks<br />
select your track and job done! :-)</p>
<p>Hope this helps.</p>

<p>Checkout <a href="http://viking.sf.net">Viking GPS</a>. It's FOSS that runs on Linux and Windows. You can enter GPS Coordinates manually, or read them from a GPS. It can dispay them on top of almost any tile server, including ones that use the OSM data (e.g. Mapnik, Cloudmade). You can also export created map to a file.</p>
<p>I personally user Viking when I am creating maps for road rallies so I can show people where they should have been.</p>
