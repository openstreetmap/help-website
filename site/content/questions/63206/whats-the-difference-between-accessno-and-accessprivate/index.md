<div class="answer-body">
<p>From the perspective of a data consumer, I've always treated them as identical.</p>
<p>As <a href="https://help.openstreetmap.org/users/3443/hendrikklaas">Hendrikklaas</a> mentions, the <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:access#Access_tag_values">access wiki page</a> does have some different wording for "no" and "private", and "no" tends to get used when a transport mode is "not allowed" (e.g. motor_vehicle=no on a street that you could physically drive along, but no-one is allowed to) whereas "private" tends to get used when only certain people are allowed to by the owner, but as far as deciding what to do based on the data, I'd treat them as equal.</p>
</div>
