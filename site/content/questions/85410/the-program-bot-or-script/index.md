<p>what should a software bot or script look like, or how to automatically remove the name:ru tag from a certain administrative unit with one edit. For example, to from this <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/relation/11925687#map=10/49.8787/24.0092">https://www.openstreetmap.org/relation/11925687#map=10/49.8787/24.0092</a></p>
<p>I want to delete name:ru streets of Western Ukraine. What for ? Because the streets of western Ukraine do not have Russian official names at all, and there are no signs with Russian street names on the houses.</p>
<p>Everything that Russian cartographers wrote in name:ru is a fantasy of online translators.</p>
<p>There is a group of cartographers of the Ukrainian branch of the Openstreetmap community, which also believes that name: ru in western Ukraine should be removed</p>

<p>Technically I wouldn't use either a bot or a script for this and would simply select the objects either with a search pattern in JOSM or by downloading just the relevant data from overpass.</p>
<p><strong>BUT</strong></p>
<p>Besides that such an edit needs to follow <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Automated_edits">https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Automated_edits</a> , the requirement for names is not that they are sign posted in a different language, but that the non-local name is in common use in the language in question. So very likely in larger cities there -will- be objects that fit that definition where the name:ru tag shouldn't be removed and that will need human validation.</p>
