<div class="answer-body">
<p>There is no way to "pay for the map" as such, but you are welcome to make a donation. There is a <a href="http://donate.openstreetmap.org/">make a donation</a> link on the bottom left of the main osm website.</p>
<p>Note however that bulk-downloading rendered tiles for a whole country from the main server might be considered too-heavy usage. Consider instead downloading a data extract as one file, for example from cloudmade. Your software will have to work a little harder to diplay this, but it will be much more compact and will enable you to search for streets, etc.</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>All maps shown on the OpenStreetMap front page use OSM's data, however not all of them are operated by the organisation behind OSM. Instead, some of them are operated by independent third parties, and are included on <a href="http://osm.org">osm.org</a> to show case the various things one can do with the data OpenStreetMap provides.</p>
<p>As such, each map has a different "tile usage policy" depending on who operates that particular map and may have a different stance on bulk downloading.</p>
<p>Although the organisation behind OpenStreetMap does not offer payed for tile downloading and thus their tiles (which corresponds to the "mapnik" tile view on <a href="http://osm.org">osm.org</a>) are only available under its <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tile_usage_policy">tile usage policy</a>, which does not allow for bulk downloading, their are <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Commercial_OSM_Software_and_Services">commercial companies</a> that do.</p>
<p>Coming to answer your question more directly, the <a href="http://www.opencyclemap.org/">cycle map</a> shown on <a href="http://osm.org">osm.org</a> is operated by Andy Allen, who also runs a consultancy for OSM services, so it might well be possible to buy bulk downloading rights from him. For more details, you would need to contact him directly though.</p>
<p><a href="http://donate.openstreetmap.org/">Donations</a> to OpenStreetMap directly to support the collection of the data, however, are of cause also very welcome... :-)</p>
