<p>One place where it definitely makes sense is where is in countries where routes can overlap - where the same piece of road can belong to different routes. The USA is one such place - you get route signage such as <a href="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/e9/Greensboro_road_signs.jpg">this</a>.</p>
<p>In other places it doesn't really make sense - in the UK for example a road will have only one UK road number.</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>While it might make sense, it isn't really done.</p>
<p>Route relations are sometimes added for numbered highways/routes that have different streetnames along their length. There are some relation types to group objects relating to a street together either <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Relation:associatedStreet">for addressing</a> or <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Relation:street">generally</a>, but they still seem to be comparatively rare.</p>
