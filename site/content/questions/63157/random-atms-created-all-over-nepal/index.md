<div class="comment-text">
<p>It's certainly not a "classic" case of vandalism. An example of <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Vandalism">vandalism</a> would be drawing doodles in an area, changing the name of the next town's football team to something unflattering, that sort of thing. The wiki page talks about "intentionally ignoring the consensus norms of the OpenStreetMap community"; here it's not even clear that this mapper is aware that there even <em>is</em> an OpenStreetMap community. It looks like they're trying to map <em>something</em>, it's just not clear what.</p>
<p>They're using MAPS.ME, they've received <a href="http://resultmaps.neis-one.org/osm-discussion-comments?uid=4820109">one changeset comment</a> long after they stopped mapping, and that was probably not in their native language.</p>
<p>In this case there's probably little more that we can do other than just revert the data, but it'd be great if local mappers could try and engage in a dialogue earlier. You can select a country and monitor usage at <a href="http://mmwatch.osmz.ru/">http://mmwatch.osmz.ru/</a> , and can then comment on changesets you see there. You can also, if communication fails, revert changes.</p>
