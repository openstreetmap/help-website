<div class="answer-body">
<p>According to the wiki article for <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:highway%3Dstreet_lamp">highway=street_lamp</a>, the <code>support=*</code> tag is what you would use to indicate that the lamp is on a pole (ie. <code>support=pole</code>). The <code>lamp_mount=*</code> tag is for describing how the lamp is mounted on the pole.</p>

<p>No, I double-checked the wiki articles to make sure what I stated was correct. <code>lamp_mount=ground</code> would be non-sensical, because it's saying the lamp is mounted on the pole/wall/etc. using a "ground" attachment method. You use <code>support=*</code> to describe how the lamp is supported (ie. <code>pole</code>, <code>wall</code>, or <code>ground</code> if it's right in the ground), and use <code>lamp_mount=*</code> to describe how the lamp is mounted on that support (ie. <code>bent_mast</code>, <code>straight_mast</code>, <code>suspended</code>, etc.).</p>
<p>I'd be curious where you read that text saying "<code>lamp_mount=*</code> is used describe how a <code>highway=street_lamp</code> is supported", because that isn't correct.</p>
