<p>Hi.</p>
<p>I originally mapped a cycleway alongside Thorpe Road and Thorpe Lea Road in Pooley Green using a scheme like <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Cycling_in_the_United_Kingdom#Cycle_tracks">https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Cycling_in_the_United_Kingdom#Cycle_tracks</a> . This is a shared pavement, sometimes segregated by paint, with several crossings of side streets. This was deleted in <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/changeset/77485289">https://www.openstreetmap.org/changeset/77485289</a> so I restored it last week after visiting the area (see Mapillary).</p>
<p>I think that separately mapping cycleways is useful where these lose priority at side streets - journey planners can then take into account crossings and how easy it us to get to junctions on the other side of the road. In addition, in this case, the changes in width and segregation are hard to take into account with cycleway=track. Furthermore, where a cycleway has been separately mapped, deleting it causes a loss of fidelity.</p>
<p>See also <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/changeset/81083097">https://www.openstreetmap.org/changeset/81083097</a> where another cycleway was deleted.</p>
<p>Can someone adjudicate please?</p>
<p>Jon</p>

<p>Cycleway=track is forbidden for an separately-mapped cycleway tagged with highway=cycleway - it can only be used on a road to indicate an adjacent cycle track. I used to use cycleway=track/shared/segregated on off-road cycleways and was told of my error - apparently only cycleway=crossing has any meaning on a separately mapped cycleway.</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>We agree with the original poster that this should be tagged as a separate Way, ideally.</p>
<p>A cycle track or shared-use pavement (however poor in this case) alongside a road, is a distinct flow and has distinct properties, e.g. width, as suggested. The stop-start nature of lack of priority at sideroads similarly is good to represent separately through a distinct Way.</p>
<p>We discussed this issue at State of the Map 2019 in full detail at:</p>
<p><a href="https://media.ccc.de/v/sotm2019-1038-is-the-osm-data-model-creaking-">https://media.ccc.de/v/sotm2019-1038-is-the-osm-data-model-creaking-</a></p>

<p>I have commented on <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/changeset/81083097">https://www.openstreetmap.org/changeset/81083097</a> with the following opinion:</p>
<p>If the shared footpath is separated from the road by any physical barrier (even if just a kerb or strip of grass that <em>could</em> be traversed by a vehicle) then mapping the shared footpath as a separate way is always acceptable, even though it might be worth checking around especially when it comes to sidewalks along residential roads. In some areas mappers frown upon mapping them as separate geometries because it can make the map more difficult to handle.</p>
