<div class="answer-body">
<p>No. I worked on this previously, but if you make use the Flash fullscreen mode, the potlatch2 doesn't get any keyboard input. Without that (especially the shift key) potlatch2 becomes quite erratic and hard to achieve anything!</p>
<p>See <a href="http://help.adobe.com/en_US/FlashPlatform/reference/actionscript/3/flash/display/Stage.html#displayState"></a><a href="http://help.adobe.com/en_US/FlashPlatform/reference/actionscript/3/flash/display/Stage.html#displayState"></a><a href="http://help.adobe.com/en_US/FlashPlatform/reference/actionscript/3/flash/display/Stage.html#displayState">http://help.adobe.com/en_US/FlashPlatform/reference/actionscript/3/flash/display/Stage.html#displayState</a></p>
<p>However, if you <strong>press the M key</strong> while using potlatch2 on <a href="http://openstreetmap.org">openstreetmap.org</a>, it will cover the full page - not quite the full screen, but pretty close.</p>

<p>What Andy said, <strong>M key</strong> is the way, and if you combine that with using <em>Full Screen</em> mode in your web browser* then it will pretty much fill the screen, which makes it usable on even the smallest on netbook displays.</p>
<p>*(in Internet Explorer or Firefox you can select <strong>"View-&gt;Full Screen"</strong> or press the <strong>F11 key</strong>, other browsers may vary)</p>

<p>Note that P2 even catches F11 (see <span></span><span>@GrahamS</span>' answer), which is a bit annoying, if you try to <em>get out of fullscreen mode. Workarounds:</em></p>
<ul>
<li>A) One then need to first de-maximize Potlatch2 (press M again), then click on the osm.org website parts outside of the Potlatch2 area and then hit F11.</li>
<li>B) Bring up the browser controls somehow otherwise (touching the top the screen with your mouse works in Firefox) and demaximize there/click and F11.</li>
</ul>
