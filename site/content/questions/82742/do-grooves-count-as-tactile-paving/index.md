<p>StreetComplete has been asking me about tactile paving for crossings and bus stops. I've looked at the <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:tactile_paving">Key:tactile_paving</a> page and most of the examples there use some form of these bumps. So I've been answering Yes when I see these:</p>
<p><img src="https://www.hyperborea.org/images/ramps/ramp-with-bumps.jpg" alt="Ramp with bumps" /></p>
<p>But there are a lot of curb cuts around that have only the sets of grooves, usually at the point where the sidewalk meets the ramp: <img src="https://www.hyperborea.org/images/ramps/ramp-with-grooves1.jpg" alt="Ramp with grooves at the edges" /></p>
<p>And sometimes on the ramp too:</p>
<p><img src="https://www.hyperborea.org/images/ramps/ramp-with-grooves2.jpg" alt="Ramp with grooves at the edges and on the ramp" /></p>
<p>Are these both correct uses of tactile paving? Or are they incorrect? Or do they not count at all for the purpose of this tag?</p>
<p>If they do count, what about crossings that have one style on one side and another style on the other side? Should that be tagged as Yes, or as Incorrect? (These are all from the same intersection!)</p>
<p>Thanks!</p>
