<p><span class="small">(based on BCNorich's answer)</span></p>
<p>For example download a map from <a href="http://garmin.openstreetmap.nl/">http://garmin.openstreetmap.nl/</a> :</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="http://www.enable-javascript.com/">enable JavaScript</a> in case you have it off (this is not a usual default)</li>
<li>In the section "Choose a predefined country:" → fourth box → select "Dominican Republic".</li>
<li>A download link ("Download map now!") appears in the section "Request your map or download it directly:" – click it.</li>
<li>Select the download for "MapSource".</li>
</ul>
<p>OpenStreetMap data is distributed under a free license, so everybody can make maps based on this data. You will find other Garmin download options at <a href="https://help.openstreetmap.org/tags/garmin/?sort=mostvoted">https://help.openstreetmap.org/tags/garmin/?sort=mostvoted</a> and <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/OSM_Map_On_Garmin/Download">https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/OSM_Map_On_Garmin/Download</a></p>
