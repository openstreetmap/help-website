<div class="comment-text">
<p>I assume you mean streets when you write "routes", then the answer is yes. The data that is used to create the map (the image you see on the website) is coming from another database than the data that is used to calculate the route you can follow from A to B. That second database is updated less frequently and hence, the calculated routes do not necessarily use the streets that are visible on the map. I believe that the same problem also occurs on the website of <a href="http://map.project-osrm.org">osrm</a> itself. The osm.org just integrates services from multiple websites and this causes this "weird" phenomenon.</p>
</div>
