<div class="answer-body">
<p>The wiki suggests <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:trench"><code>military=trench</code></a> for the trench itself, a more generic tag might be to add <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:cutting"><code>cutting=yes</code></a> to the way if there is not enough detail to map the walls separately.</p>
<p>If the walls are mapped separately then they are likely <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:barrier%3Dretaining_wall"><code>barrier=retaining_wall</code></a> in most cases, which should be drawn with the high side on the left, from your description <code>material=concrete</code> sounds like and appropriate secondary tag.</p>
<p>The appearance of the wall doesn't have standardized tagging, but the <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:mimics"><code>mimics</code></a> tag was recently approved for mobile phone towers that are made to look like trees etc. <code>mimics=sandbags</code> might be a reasonable (if undocumented) tag for this aspect.</p>
<p>To my mind the outline of the site should probably be tagged <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:historic%3Dmemorial"><code>historic=memorial</code></a> unless the local community discourages this.</p>
