<div class="answer-body">
<p>This is a perfectly fine level of detail - it's easy to confirm from aerial imagery. Some specific notes:</p>
<ul>
<li><code>access=private</code> on the driveway: normally this is reserved for roads behind a gate or a "no trespassing" sign. Private ownership is not important here (but the <code>access=private</code> tag has historically been overused in the past).</li>
<li>Optionally, building entrance nodes can be identified as <code>entrance=yes</code> or <code>entrance=home</code></li>
</ul>

<p>Re "access=private on the driveway: normally this is reserved for roads behind a gate or a no trespassing sign"; this is very regionally dependent.</p>
<p>In some places (including England, where I am) something that is privately owned is by default not publicly accessible. 150 miles up the road in Scotland that isn't the case.</p>
<p>At the risk of tautology, it makes sense to tag as access=private if those really are the access rules there. That may be influenced by signage, but also other things too.</p>
