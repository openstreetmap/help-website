<div class="answer-body">
<p>"<em>Community Wiki posts work by transferring ownership of the post from the original author to the community. They make the post easier to edit and maintain by a wider group of users, but they do not contribute to any user's reputation.</em>"</p>
<p>That's from <a href="http://meta.stackoverflow.com/questions/11740/what-are-community-wiki-posts/11741#11741">'What are “Community Wiki” posts?' on meta stackoverflow</a> &lt;-- see this link for much more detail.</p>
<p>Note: The help.openstreetmap.org site uses <a href="http://www.osqa.net/">OSQA</a> which is not the exact same software as stackoverflow, but it's very similar and I think everything in the above linked answer applies to the way community wiki mode works here. One important difference is that stackoverflow actually no longer offers this option to people asking questions. I guess they decided it creates too much confusion, and in fact there's no good reason for the author of a question to put it in this mode themselves. See also <a href="http://meta.stackoverflow.com/questions/55888/when-to-mark-the-community-wiki-checkbox-on-a-new-question">When to mark the 'community wiki' checkbox on a new question?</a> (Answer: mostly don't do it) However there are useful reasons for a question to <em>transition</em> into community wiki mode</p>
