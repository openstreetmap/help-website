<p>Hello,</p>
<p>So you're talking about the GPX database of osm (which is different from the main OSM database, that can be confusing).</p>
<p>These tracks are not meant to be displayed on the map, only in the various editors, as a basis to trace elements of the main OSM database.</p>
<p>However, if your tracks are "public" (can't read russian, but as it's on a green background, I assume it is, but I can't find them on your user page), you can see them as an overlay, if you check "Public GPS tracks" in the layers menu of openstreetmap.org. This overlay is a raster layer generated from the public and identifiable tracks of the database, it can take some time before it is regenerated. And of course when you use your favorite editor, you'll see them, among the others.</p>
<p>As far as I know only JOSM download the actual tracks, and thus can filter them, if you want to display only yours.</p>
<p>More information about GPX database in the <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Upload_GPS_tracks">wiki</a>, specifically have a look at the <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Visibility_of_GPS_traces">visibility settings</a> which are totally confusing !</p>
<p>Regards.</p>
<p>Ps. you should move your screenshots from the answer section, to your question.</p>
