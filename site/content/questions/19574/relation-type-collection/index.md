<div class="answer-body">
<p>As always don't simply delete the object. First try to contact the author and give him or her some time to react. Especially in this case as these relations don't do any harm. Try to understand the intention of the author and try to correct mistakes instead of deleting them.</p>
<p>Furthermore there is also the <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Relations/Proposed/Site">site relation</a> type. Although never approved it is the <a href="http://taginfo.openstreetmap.org/search?q=#relations">fifth most used relation type</a> and in my opinion shares some similarities with a <em>collection</em>.</p>
<p>The first relation you mentioned can be easily converted to a <em>site</em> relation by just changing the <em>type</em>. The second one doesn't seem to fit into the <em>site</em> category but could possibly be used as a starting point for a <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Relation:route"><em>route</em></a>.</p>
