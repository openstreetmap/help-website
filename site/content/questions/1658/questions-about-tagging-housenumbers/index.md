<p>Hi folks,</p>
<p>I'm relatively new to OSM and I couldn't find sufficient information in the wiki about tagging houses with housenumbers.</p>
<p>My questions:</p>
<ol>
<li>Is it better to tag the houses directly with "addr:housenumber" or place a new node near the entrance and tag that?</li>
<li>How do I tag a house which is in reality marked as "23–27"? Also as 23-27, as 23,25,27 or as 23/25/27? The house is on the side of the street with odd housenumbers.<br />
2.b What kind of dash should I use for 23–27? A - (minus) or a – (en dash)?</li>
<li>Should I also tag the houses with addr:city, addr:country and addr:postcode or is this not necessary?</li>
</ol>
<p>You can see my first effort in placing housenumbers here: <a href="http://osm.org/go/0GAnD_bL1--">http://osm.org/go/0GAnD_bL1--</a> If I made a mistake, please correct me.</p>

<ol>
<li>The prefered way is to place a node at the entrence so that the routing software will navigate you to the front door. This is espessialy importent for the blind.</li>
<li>From the <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:addr:housenumber">wiki</a>: If a single entry has multiple house numbers, separate them by ",". e.g. "12b,12c". Alternativly mark the entrences with the address or use intrepolation ways.</li>
<li>This is generaly not taged on each address. But it could be tagged on poligons around the city/country/postcode area.</li>
</ol>
