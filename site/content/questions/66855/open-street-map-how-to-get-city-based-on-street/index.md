<div class="answer-body">
<p>City based only on longitude, latitude? What you mean is called "reverse geocoding". See</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Frameworks#Forward_and_reverse_geocoding">https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Frameworks#Forward_and_reverse_geocoding</a> and</li>
<li><a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Nominatim#Reverse_Geocoding">https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Nominatim#Reverse_Geocoding</a></li>
<li><a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Category:Geocoding">https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Category:Geocoding</a> (e.g. Photon also can do reverse geocoding) and, of course,</li>
<li><a href="https://help.openstreetmap.org/tags/reversegeocoding/?sort=mostvoted">https://help.openstreetmap.org/tags/reversegeocoding/?sort=mostvoted</a></li>
</ul>
<p>If that does not help, please describe your use case.</p>
<p>Please note, that although you do not use maps, you still need to obey <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/copyright">our few license requirements</a> and (if you do not run your own server) the usage restrictions/terms of the server which you will be using.</p>
