<div class="answer-body">
<p>You can zoom in more in other <span>editors</span> (e.g. iD or JOSM). But those editors just scale the images up, you do not get more information that way (the highest resolution images are the same) - although you may be able to see the included information better and it may be (much) easier to draw.</p>
<p>For some locations of the world <span>other aerial imagery</span> is available <em>which we are allowed to use</em>. Potlatch2 offers a selection in its "background" menu drop-down.</p>
<p>It is not allowed to use Google's sat images - see <span>legal FAQ</span>. In general: you are not allowed to use anything except if you are clearly allowed to (that, of course, applies to the default beackground image pre-sets in our main editors featured on our openstreetmap.org homepage).</p>
