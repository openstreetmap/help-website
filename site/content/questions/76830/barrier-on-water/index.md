<div class="question-body">
<p>A common feature on UK rivers is a 'barrel line' approaching a weir, to prevent boats passing over the weir by accident and damaging themselves: see <a href="https://www.geograph.org.uk/photo/2782562">here</a>.</p>
<p>That particular barrel line has been <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/264203291">mapped</a> by dzizek23 as a barrier=chain, with a description to explain the feature.</p>
<p>That seems quite good, and it renders nicely (*ducks for cover).</p>
<p>As a water user I would quite like barrier=floating-chain or barrier=barrel-line as that would be specific for the water feature rather than repurposing a land feature for it. It would be useful for canoeists and others planning what barriers they may be able to pass over/under. But maybe that's just adding complication?</p>
<p>Does anyone know if these are mapped elsewhere? Are there views on whether they should be a barrier=chain or something else? Or should this question be asked elsewhere?</p>
<p>Thank you.</p>
