<td><div class="item-right">
<div class="answer-body">
<p>In general the convention in OSM is to map the bird's-eye view &amp; not the footprint of the building. Most building will be mapped from aerial or satellite imagery so accurate mapping of footprints requires additional on-the-ground inspection. Mapping the building as visible from above generally ensures buildings are mapped consistently.</p>
<p>Note that this is different from the typical approach of national topographic maps.</p>

<p><a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Simple_3D_Buildings">Simple 3D Buildings</a>, which is the tagging standard that the <code>building:part=*</code> key originates in, states that the building outline should overlap all the parts. That includes parts above the ground.</p>
