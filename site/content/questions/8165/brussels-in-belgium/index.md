<p>actually, Brussels belongs to both Communities (Flemish and French) and, as you say, it is also a Region of its own. In Belgium, Region and Community are two distinct kinds of "administrative entities", and yes, in the case of Belgium there are overlaps between them.</p>
<p>In short: 3 Regions :<br />
- Flemish (Flanders only, without Brussels)<br />
- Walloon (French+German parts of Belgium without Brussels)<br />
- Brussels<br />
+ 3 Communities:<br />
- Flemish (Flanders + Brussels)<br />
- French (French part of Walloon Region + Brussels)<br />
- German (German part of Walloon Region)<br />
</p>
