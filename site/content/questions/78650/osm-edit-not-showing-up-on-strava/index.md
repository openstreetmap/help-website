<p>I reached out to them yesterday as a follow up. In their original response they said, "Thank you for contacting Strava. The underlying map data for routes comes from our map provider, OpenStreetMap.</p>
<p>If you notice any problems with the map used for routes, you can report those problems directly to OSM: <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/">https://www.openstreetmap.org/</a></p>
<p>Changes you make may not be reflected exactly the way you may expect them on Strava. For example, if you mark a road as closed to bicycle traffic, that road may still be included in suggested cycling routes if other data sources indicate that the road is open to bicycles. We strive to bring in new updates from OSM in a timely manner while still ensuring fairness, correctness, and completeness of data. Generally, you should be able to see your changes reflected within a month.</p>
<p>I hope this helps. If you have any questions, please do not hesitate to ask."</p>
<p>So this makes me think that they use OSM for only the mapping of routes feature, not necessarily the integration of of uploading your activity to Strava.</p>
