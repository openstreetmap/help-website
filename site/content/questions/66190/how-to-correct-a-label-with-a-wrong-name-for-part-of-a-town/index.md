<div class="answer-body">
<p>Fixing this could be somewhere between very simple and slightly advanced.</p>
<p>If the place is mapped with a "point" the best course of action would be to move it to the proper place, and create a new point where "Tervoorst" should be.</p>
<p>This is simple to do with the in-browser editor on openstreetmap.org, there is an intro that you should have a look at first, but making the edits is quite straightforward.</p>
<p>Actually there does seem to be a "Tervoorst" node.</p>
<p>Tervoorst is <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/2561920809#map=18/50.91436/5.87187">https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/2561920809#map=18/50.91436/5.87187</a></p>
<p>and</p>
<p>Terstraten is <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/3238503525#map=18/50.91309/5.86535">https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/3238503525#map=18/50.91309/5.86535</a></p>
<p>So you may simply have to move them to better positions.</p>
<p>(It would be more complicated if area objects were associated with the areas but that doesn't seem to be the case).</p>
