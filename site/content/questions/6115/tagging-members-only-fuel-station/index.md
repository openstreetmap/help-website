<p>The warehouse club chain BJ's Wholesale often has a fuel station (amenity=fuel) at it's store location, but the fuel station is only accessible for members of BJ's. [The pumps actually require scanning your membership card before turning on.] My question is what to do about these. There are two options I have come up with</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Just don't add them</strong>.<br />
</li>
<li><strong>Tag them with an "access"</strong></li>
</ol>
<p>While (1) seems like the easiest, I think leaving them out isn't fair to people who may be BJ's members who are looking for fuel in a new area.</p>
<p>That leave's (2). The next question is "What <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Access">access</a>?" Without creating a new access tag, I think either "private" or "customer" would work. I find myself more drawn to "private" as one could say "I am going to buy fuel there, therefore I am a 'customer'."</p>
<p>Thoughts?</p>

<p>I'm not sure it's really "private" - there are truly private fuel stations at places like remote farms. <a href="http://taginfo.openstreetmap.org/keys/access#values">Taginfo</a> suggests perhaps "access=customers" or perhaps even "permissive"?<br />
</p>
<p>You could also ask on the <a href="http://lists.openstreetmap.org/listinfo/tagging">tagging</a> mailing list - I'm sure that you'll get lots of suggestions there. Some of those I'm sure will be wildly contradictory, but they might give you more ideas. Ultimately you're best placed to pick a value because you're familiar with "BJ's Wholesale" and we're not.</p>
