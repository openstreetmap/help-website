<div class="answer-body">
<p>osm.org is about building a database of geodata (to create <span>maps or other services</span> from the data). So you can use OSM's data with many programs. Depending on the application and what data processing(!) steps other people did for you it is simple or not so simple.</p>
<p>The most simple bit to use OSM's data in a mobile application is to just <span>(<em>online</em>) view pre-rendered maps</span> (based on OSM) in your application. Your mobile does need a Internet connection for this. Many apps are able to save the map parts (<a href="http://www.oruxmaps.com/index_en.html">Orux too</a>: <span class="small">"Online maps can be cached for offline navigation."</span>) on your device which you've already seen ("caching"), so they do not need to be downloaded again and it enables you also to view them without a Internet connection. This is what <span>andy mackey mentions in his answer</span>. I recommend this method to you.</p>
<p>See <span>how/where to get <em>offline</em> maps for OruxMaps</span> (in our wiki). Such maps cover a quite big area and you do not need to view them on your device in advance (you just need to copy such a map to your device and then you can go outside). However, it looks like <em>you cannot (directly) use an export from osm.org</em> with OruxMaps (I guess that is your question about). So using OSM-based maps offline seems not to be that easy.</p>
<p>Detailed <a href="http://www.oruxmaps.com/manual_en.html">help on the use of OruxMaps's interface</a> is not really in the scope of this OSM help page, sorry. OruxMaps specific questions may be asked at <a href="http://oruxmaps.foroactivos.net">their forum</a>. If OruxMaps is to hard to use for your needs you could try <span>another app</span>.</p>

<p>How did I install a mapforge osm map</p>
<ol>
<li>I went to mapforge and found England map and down loaded the england.map</li>
<li>I connected the device with usb cable to my PC and searched to see the device’s files looking for “oruxmap” folder</li>
<li>I copied the england.map into the oruxmaps folder</li>
<li>I then disconnected the device and turned the device off.</li>
<li>I waited for short time and then turned device on (SD can’t be found otherwise), tapped orux to start it</li>
<li>In orux settings map chose sd, oruxmaps, england</li>
<li>in orux tapped world map icon, switch map, off line map, oruxmaps, england, used back key and the map appeared. I did have to turn device off and on and turn again a few times and checked settings and choose england for it to work. I got the map from here <a href="http://download.mapsforge.org/maps/europe/">http://download.mapsforge.org/maps/europe/</a></li>
</ol>
