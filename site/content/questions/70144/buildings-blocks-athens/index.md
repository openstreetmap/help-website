<p>What you're seeing on the standard layer at openstreetmap.org is lots of things drawn on top of each other. There are roads drawn on top of <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/86440081">https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/86440081</a> , which is why it looks like that landuse=residential area is several separate areas. If you zoom in and out (so that drawn road widths change) you'll see that the landuse=residential always goes all the way to the drawn edge of the road.</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>What you are referring to as a "city block" is I think <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/86440081">this</a> landuse=residential area. If that's what you want, extract "landuse=residential" (or <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/86440285">commercial</a>) using Overpass.</p>
<p>To see what anything is on the openstreetmap.org map, zoom in and click on the "query feature" icon at the right-hand side, then click where you are interested on the map.</p>
