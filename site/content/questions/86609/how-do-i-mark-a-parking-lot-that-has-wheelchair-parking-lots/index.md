<div class="answer-body">
<p>Hi.</p>
<p>Please be aware that the keyword to mark parking lots for disabled people is "<a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:disabled">disabled</a>", not "<a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:wheelchair">wheelchair</a>" as on other features (see <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/How_to_map_for_the_needs_of_people_with_disabilities#Designated_parking_space">here</a>).</p>
<p>Schematically, adding <code>wheelchair=yes</code> on a parking, means that people in a wheelchair can access the parking, but says nothing about cars or parking lots.</p>
<p>The right tag to say there are disabled parking lots (and how many) in this parking is <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:capacity:disabled">capacity:disabled=</a>. Or you can tag individual parking lots (as in this <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:disabled">example</a>).</p>
<p>Best regards.</p>
