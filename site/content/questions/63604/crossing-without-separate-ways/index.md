<p>It isn't true that a crossing mapped as a node is "meant for car navigation". The node provides all the information required for pedestrian navigation.</p>
<p>Some essential features for pedestrian navigation are <em>only</em> possible with this style of mapping, in fact. These include the ability for a pedestrian to cross roads away from marked crossings (which is common and legal in many parts of the world), and the ability to provide instructions such as "walk down the right sidewalk of Main Street".</p>
