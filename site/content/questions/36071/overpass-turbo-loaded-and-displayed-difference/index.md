<div class="answer-body">
<p>It states : loaded 60 nodes, 6 ways, 0 relations displayed pois: 14, ways: 0 , polygons 6.</p>
<p>Hence the 6 ways of the "loaded" line are polygons (areas, not ways). 60 nodes - 14 for the pois = 46, so the 6 polygons consist of 46 points in total.</p>
<p>So there are 14 picnic sites on that screen represented by a single point and 6 by polygons</p>

<p>"Loaded" counts the actual OSM data loaded from the server, "displayed" counts the resulting objects displayed on the map. For example, a query that finds one street will only "display" one object (the line) but needs to "load" many elements to do this (the way and all its constituant nodes).</p>
</div>
