<ol>
<li>There are quite a few instances of <code>amenity=bench</code> + <code>highway=steps</code>. When mapping the line, you should treat it as a staircase, align it along the incline, and add the <code>incline=</code> (mostly <code>=yes</code> or <code>=no</code>). <code>step_count=</code> would be very helpful here. While the direction of a bench line is usually parallel to seating, <code>highway=steps</code> should suffice to indicate otherwise.</li>
<li>There has been proposals about mapping wide stairs (but not as your example) as areas. No solid result.</li>
</ol>

<p>This depends on whether the "and" in "long/wide benches &amp; wide stairs" is together and separating. Nevertheless, he mentioned "some benches are between 5 &amp; 10 meters long".</p>
<p>About the stairs, problem with <code>leisure=bleachers</code> would be that it includes more than stair-shaped seating. The most common original meaning of bleachers seems to be those ladder-looking stands not necessarily able to walked over as stairs. See 1st photo and 2nd row of <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:leisure%3Dbleachers#Examples">Tag:leisure=bleachers#Examples</a>.</p>
<p>Stairs designed to be sat on are often referred to as benches, and there are really many meanings to a "bench".</p>
<p>It seems reasonable to tag a stair that is intended to be sat on as a bench as well.</p>
