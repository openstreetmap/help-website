<p>I want to mark an area on the map, it can be any area, doesnt matter where it is, in terms like are there any buildings, roads, rivers, just any area... So, a government declares these "no fly" zones for drones, where you need various permissions to fly drones over it. These are usually around airports, government and security related buildings, high profile infrastructure objects, etc...</p>
<p>Some of these zones are defined as an "area defined by the following GPS coordinates (X1,Y1), (X2,Y2), (X3,Y3), ... (Xn,Yn)", and when you connect the dots, you get a shape of a no fly area. Some are defined as "100/300/500m away from the object". Some are defind like "500 away from object and 3000m above it" (so if you fly 3001m above the object, you are not in a no fly zone.</p>
<p>I hope its clearer now...</p>

<p>A quick google search tells me that these no fly zone should not be mapped in OSM (<a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Aviation#Things_Not_to_Map">wiki</a>). The point is that it is not verifiable on the ground.</p>
<p>This (old) <a href="https://www.directionsmag.com/article/1202">article</a> list a number of projects computing these zones from OSM data.</p>
