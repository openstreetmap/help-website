<div class="answer-body">
<p>Assuming your local imagery is available under a compatible license or you have explicit permission.</p>
<p>There are two plugins for the JOSM editor that could help you with this.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/JOSM/Plugins/ImportImagePlugin">ImportImagePlugin</a> for georeferenced imagery</li>
<li><a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/JOSM/Plugins/PicLayer">PicLayer</a> for any image</li>
</ul>
<p>If you are allowed to upload the imagery to the web, then you can also use <a href="http://mapwarper.net/">Map Warper</a>.</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>Thank you for your interest in helping the local OSM community :)</p>
<p>I guess the JOSM editor has the <a href="http://josm.openstreetmap.de/wiki/Help/Menu/Imagery">best ability</a> to add local stored imagery, even if some external rectifying services can also be used by iD.</p>
<p>The general steps are:<br />
</p>
<ul>
<li>Add images and align to a georeference</li>
<li>rectify the result, so it matches well without any strange deformations and drifts</li>
<li>start tracing</li>
</ul>
<p>Please take care that the source matchs with existing OSM data and gps tracks as producing misplaced objects is a big problem of <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Armchair_mapping">armchair mapping</a>. You will get a better idea if you look at some (outdated) video tutorials: <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Video_tutorials">https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Video_tutorials</a></p>
