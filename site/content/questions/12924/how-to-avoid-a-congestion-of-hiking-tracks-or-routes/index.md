<div class="answer-body">
<p>If 10 hiking routes start or end at one place, there will usually not be 10 paths on the ground - only one or two, and the routes will share the existing paths. We model it in OpenStreetMap just like that - using route relations (see <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Walking_Routes">Walking Routes</a> on the wiki).</p>
<p>That said, neither your "library of books and routes" nor the "routes in GPS coordinates out there on the Internet" are likely to be acceptable sources for OpenStreetMap, as the information in those books and tracks is likely copyrighted. Once you have walked a route yourself you can of course upload the information you gathered, but you can't just take published information and enter that into OSM without the author's consent.</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>Maybe a "worked example" would help?<br />
</p>
<p>When walking <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/edit?editor=potlatch2&amp;lat=52.69224&amp;lon=-0.41157&amp;zoom=17">here</a> I noticed signs that said that the east-west path formed part of the Macmillan Way long-distance path, so I want to add it to that relation.</p>
<p>If there were any nearby relations, I'd click on the path in Potlatch, click "advanced" and then click "Add To" at the bottom. Unfortunately, there are no relations nearby, so I'm going to have to find out the relation to use by some other method. If I search the wiki for Macmillan Way I find <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/WikiProject_United_Kingdom_Long_Distance_Paths">this</a> page for UK long-distance paths (I'd expect other countries to do something similar)**. That tells me that the relation to use is <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/browse/relation/1149730">1149730</a>, and like with any other OSM browse page I can click a link to <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/?relation=1149730">view the relation on a larger map</a>. Now that I know the relation number I can go to Potlatch and click "Load Relation" on the "Select Relation" screen that was empty, and enter 1149730. The way has now been added to the relation (it appears in "advanced" in the "relations" list. Then I'll save and close the changeset (it's <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/browse/changeset/11691362">11691362</a> for info) and that's it!</p>
<p>Although the standard OSM map won't look any different, any map that displays walking routes (such as <a href="http://hiking.lonvia.de/en/?zoom=14&amp;lat=52.14316&amp;lon=-1.35335">this one</a>) will now be able to display it correctly. One thing that it's perhaps worth mentioning that I've not done - I've not added the <em>name</em> "Macmillan Way" to the existing footpath. Just because a path or road is part of a route doesn't mean that it is called the same name as the route (many roads have their own name; many paths are part of several routes).</p>
<p>** I've not done it yet, but when I've finished mapping that section (including stiles, gates and surface information) I'll update the wiki page too.</p>
