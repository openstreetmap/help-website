<p>Let's say somebody maps a school. The school has the info about the name of the village where the school is located. But the village isn't mapped with a name (there may be a node already stating it is a village, but no name yet).</p>
<p>My question is:</p>
<p>If I add the name to the village node, what should be the best source tagging?</p>
<p>I use to tag it as <code>source=osm</code>, but maybe there are better tagging schemas, like <code>source=node 123456789</code>, <code>source=changeset 987654321</code> or similar.</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>When it comes to noting your sources, nobody will <em>ever</em> complain to you that you're being too specific. If you want to put <code>"node 123456789"</code> go for it. If, after a month of this, you're feeling lazy: <code>"OSM"</code> is fine... Nobody will be able to know exactly where the info's from, but they'll get the general idea.</p>
<p><code>"inference"</code> imo doesn't really say anything about the source. It's a fancy way of saying <code>"source=I figured it out"</code>. Figured it out from what? Inquiring minds wanna know. It's still better than nothing, of course.</p>
