<div class="answer-body">
<p>At time of writing, several of your links don't work for me so this may be overly general.</p>
<p>The <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:junction%3Droundabout">junction=roundabout</a> tag is reserved for 'modern roundabouts' where traffic on the roundabout has priority over traffic attempting to joint the roundabout. Depending on jurisdiction, this may be indicated by yield/give way signs on each of the inbound ways, road marking indicating the same, or signage/indication that 'roundabout rules' apply.</p>
<p>If a particular junction doesn't give preference to traffic on the loop then it is not classified as a roundabout within OSM tagging. In OSM these may be tagged as <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:junction%3Dcircular">junction=circular</a> (especially if they keep getting mis-tagged as 'true' roundabouts.</p>
<p>In your case, the junctions in question may be roundabouts missing the relevant tags or they may have odd priorities which mean they shouldn't be tagged this way. For example by having parts stopping for incoming traffic.</p>
