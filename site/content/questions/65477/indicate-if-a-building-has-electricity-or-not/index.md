<p>OSM has an <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Any_tags_you_like">Any tags you like</a> approach. The wiki lists "approved" tags, but tags don't have to be approved. I suggest you look at the object history of those objects with that <code>electricity</code> tag and contact the mappers who added it. Start discussing the idea with them (since they are people who are interested in mapping this). You can post on the tagging mailing list to get feedback, and even start a wiki proposal as well, or document it on the wiki That can get feedback and raise it's visibility.</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>Based on a quick browse of Overpass, it looks the main use of the <code>electricity=</code>: tag is to describe whether electrical power is available (mostly used in campsites and shelters in Europe, and medical clinics in Africa). Some values like <code>solar</code> and <code>generator</code> indicate the sources of electrical power, but I don't know offhand how you'd specify "connected to the town's electricity network"... Maybe <code>electricity=grid</code>?</p>
<p>Following the current namespace-happy tagging trends, I could imagine tagging with <code>electricity=yes/no/intermittent</code> and <code>electricity:source=solar/generator/windmill/grid/whatever</code>. Other possible details might be <code>electricity:voltage=</code>, <code>electricity:fee=</code>, etc.</p>
<p>If you intend to create a page on the wiki, definitely bring this up on the Tagging mailing list, there may be some valuable input there. If you want to create a formal proposal, see <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Proposal_process">https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Proposal_process</a>.</p>
