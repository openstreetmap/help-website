<div class="answer-body">
<p>I'd probably use a <strong><code>picnic_table=yes</code></strong> tag, which should be added onto a larger (hopefully immovable!) containing feature, like <strong><code>leisure=park</code></strong>, <strong><code>tourism=picnic_site</code></strong>, <strong><code>leisure=outdoor_seating</code></strong>, or <strong><code>amenity=shelter</code></strong> + <strong><code>shelter_type=picnic_shelter</code></strong>. You can see more suggestions on the <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:picnic_table">picnic_table page on the wiki</a>.</p>
<p>It might be a good idea to use a <strong><code>description=</code></strong> tag to indicate the movability. You could also make up your own tag, like <strong><code>picnic_table:movable=yes</code></strong>.</p>
<p>If the site in question had multiple picnic tables, maybe something like this:</p>
<pre><code>tourism=picnic_site  
picnic_table=yes
picnic_table:count=6
description=picnic area with 6 movable picnic tables</code></pre>
