<div class="answer-body">
<p>A GPX file can consist of three basic types: waypoints (<em>wpt</em>), tracks (<em>trk</em>) and routes (<em>rte</em>). To offer routing functionality, most devices require a GPX <em>route</em> instead of a GPX <em>track</em>. So you have to convert your track to a route first before transferring it to your device. This can be done using <a href="http://www.gpsbabel.org/">GPSBabel</a>.</p>

<p>Judging from page 5 to 7 of the <a href="http://static.garmincdn.com/pumac/Dakota_OM_EN.pdf">owner's manual</a> you can use tracks for navigation. But that just means that you see the track as "colored line". I do not see a hint that your Dakota 20 offers turn by turn announcements or at least notifications (my stone age Garmin, other model, beeps if I navigate a track but only if I am approaching a sharp turn). There is just "colored line" in the manual. Note that navigation by tracks is usually totally unrelated to the map on the device - no map is needed for navigation by a track.</p>
<p>However, <a href="https://buy.garmin.com/en-US/digital/on-the-trail/handhelds/dakota-20/prod30926.html">the specs</a> mention "Automatic routing (turn by turn routing on roads)" so that should work (see also my and SomeoneElse's comment above. You can also try a "route" (made of a series of waypoints), see <a href="/questions/24825/garmin-dakota-20-how-to-get-notifications-when-turning/24826">scai's answer</a>.</p>
