<ol>
<li>For the default Standard Tile Layer using OSMCarto style, there should be no special rendering for <code>access=</code> in non-vehicular roads.</li>
<li>Is it really <code>=permit</code>, rather than <code>=private</code>? <code>=permit</code> doesn't mean "by permission", which is often confused with <code>=permissive</code> as well. <code>=permit</code> refers to a open license granting system; while <code>=private</code> has to be authorized on a case-by-case basis with the managing entity directly.</li>
</ol>

<p>With regard to the Alltrails map, you'll have to ask AllTrails about that. AllTrails uses OSM data, but only they control how OSM data appears on their map, and how long it takes for data that is updated in OSM to appear on their map.</p>
<p>We (OSM's Data Working Group) have commented to AllTrails in the past that their representation of OSM data that is private, and data that is not known to be public, is not great and really would benefit from an improvement.</p>
