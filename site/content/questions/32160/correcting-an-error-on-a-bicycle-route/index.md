<p>Hi heibaa, I have done similar things in The Netherlands. I have a Garmin navigation device and a Gps-logger on my bike. Below is what I would have done :</p>
<p>I found Rimbach(R1) here :</p>
<p><a href="http://www.mtb-geo-naturpark.de/index.php?option=com_content&amp;view=article&amp;id=139:rundstrecke-rimbach&amp;catid=12:rundstrecken&amp;Itemid=65">http://www.mtb-geo-naturpark.de/index.php?option=com_content&amp;view=article&amp;id=139:rundstrecke-rimbach&amp;catid=12:rundstrecken&amp;Itemid=65</a></p>
<p>On the top menue there is drop down box. Click it's small arrow and choose "GPX Track". Click Ok next to "GPX Track" and download the track.</p>
<p>1) If there is no copy right on Rimbach(R1) then open both the area and the <em>.gpx track in Josm and use the</em> .gpx track to edit the OpenStreetMap</p>
<p>2) If there is a copy right issue than open the Rimbach(R1) <em>.gpx track in BaseCamp and use the track to plan a route. Copy the route to your Garmin. Mount your bicycle, take your Garmin with the proposed route and a good Gps-logger with you. While on your way look for the Rimbach(R1) signs/directions on the road. Make notes with the time of your Gps-logger so you can find them back on the map. You now have the official Rimbach(R1)</em> .gpx track because you followed the route directions on the road. Which don't have a copy right. Continue at 1)</p>
<p>Gys de Jongh</p>
