<div class="answer-body">
<p>You cannot upload hypothetical roads to OpenStreetMap of course.</p>
<p>If you download data from OpenStreetMap and combine this with your own, hypothetical, data into a new database from which you then make derived products, then the ODbL's share-alike provision means that <em>if</em> these derived products are published ("publicly used"), whoever receives the products can also ask you for the database behind them (i.e. the OSM data that you have modified), and you have to give them the data under ODbL.</p>
<p>If the derived products are only used internally by the transportation planners, then the share-alike provision doesn't even kick in.</p>
