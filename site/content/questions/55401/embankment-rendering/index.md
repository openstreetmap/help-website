<p>man_made=embankment will render on the standard map at openstreetmap.org .<br />
For embankments that have ways on top, you can draw a parallel way next to the road or path on one or both sides tagged with man_made=embankment. The low side is to the right of the direction you draw the way.<br />
In JOSM you can select the section of the highway with the embankment and select edit&gt;duplicate and place the duplicate close to the way, tag with man_made=embankment, and do the same for the other side with the direction reversed if the embankment is on both sides. Then adjust the embankment width if it varies along it's length.<br />
highway=track + man_made=embankment will also render at on the standard map but it is more difficult to see the embankment.<br />
Here is a bit of the flood levee at Charleville <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/#map=17/-26.38214/146.26202">https://www.openstreetmap.org/#map=17/-26.38214/146.26202</a><br />
and at the Ernest Henry Mine there are many ways with embankments on each side <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/#map=15/-20.4494/140.7220">https://www.openstreetmap.org/#map=15/-20.4494/140.7220</a></p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>One tip:</p>
<p>Do not add <strong>embankment=yes</strong> or <strong>cutting=yes</strong> to multiple roads or railways running parallel <em>and in reality being on the same embankment or inside the same cutting</em>, so not separated by a lower or higher section of ground.</p>
<p>I have seen people adding embankment=yes or cutting=yes to e.g. all railways lines on a main hub railway station with up to a dozen tracks running parallel on the same embankment, meaning no sensible cartographic rendering can be made for embankment/cutting=yes in this case, as all the embankment symbols would start to overlap and clutter the map.</p>
<p>In these cases, as others also pointed out, always use <strong>man_made=embankment</strong> to draw a single line along the side of the <em>entire</em> embankment, instead of using embankment/cutting=yes on each individual track or highway section.</p>
<p>Additionally, if the "embankment" is in reality a stone or concrete wall holding back the higher ground, tag <strong>barrier=retaining_wall</strong> on an line instead of <strong>man_made=embankment</strong>. If it is vertical, it is not a embankment in traditional topographic cartography.</p>
<p>An example of all of this: <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/#map=18/52.38868/4.87316">https://www.openstreetmap.org/#map=18/52.38868/4.87316</a></p>
