<p>How do I mark-up a road where it is legal to drive anywhere on it, while it is not allowed to drive its full length without a significant stop?</p>
<p>The term in Norwegian is "Gjennomkjøring forbudt". Google translation of that is "No thoroughfare", but I'm not sure that is the exact meaning. If your GPS routes you through a road with "Gjennomkjøring forbudt" in Norway, the fine can be heavy.</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>This sounds like the destination value for the <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:access">access keys</a>. Depending on the actual sign you should either use vehicle=destination (which would even forbid through-traffic for bicycles) or motor_vehicle=destination.</p>
<p>According to the wiki the value <em>destination</em> is defined as:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Only when travelling to this element/area, i.e. local traffic only. NOTE: This restriction often only applies to certain modes of transportation (e.g. only to vehicles). Take care to use the right transport mode restriction, e.g. vehicle=destination when only vehicle traffic is restricted.</p>
<p>English: "no thru traffic" / "local traffic only" (USA), Dutch: "bestemmingsverkeer/uitgezonderd plaatselijk verkeer (Flanders/Belgium)", German: "Anlieger frei"/"ausgenommen Anrainerverkehr" (Austria), French "Interdit sauf riverains/excepté circulation locale (Wallonie/Belgium)"</p>
</blockquote>
