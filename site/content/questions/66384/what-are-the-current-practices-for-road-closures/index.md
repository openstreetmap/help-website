<div class="answer-body">
<p>It's somewhat left to local discretion. Advantages of marking temporary closures:</p>
<ul>
<li>Routing applications such as OSMAnd and online OSRM will quickly pick up the change and route around the obstacle.</li>
</ul>
<p>Disadvantages of marking temporary closures:</p>
<ul>
<li>The original mapper may move or change hobbies by the time construction completes. The road will permanently be marked closed until someone else happens to notice.</li>
<li>A routing application that updates quarterly may pick up a short term one-week closure and thus not know that the road was back open for the remainder of the update cycle.</li>
</ul>
<p>My own rule was that I wouldn't mark temporary closure unless it was planned to be longer than a year, but I am reducing that time to less than a month since hearing of OSMAnd users' experiences.</p>
