<p>It is very standard, at least for topographic maps in my area to have every 5th contour a bit wider and have the elevation on it. This map, though not of an area I am familiar with, follows that convention. In my area, the wider contours with numbers are called "index contours". I don't know if that is a universal term or not.</p>
<p>If you zoom in some more you will see numbers on the darker/wider "index contours" the are 50 meters apart. The thinner contours, without numbers, are then 10 meters apart.</p>

<p>The labels are shown only depending on the zoom factor. On cycle map the 100m space lines are labled from zoom level 15 onwards while the 50m space lines are only visible from zoom level 16 onwards.</p>
<p>There are other maps out there showing contour lines. Maybe they are more to your liking. Try for example <a href="https://www.opentopomap.org/#map=13/56.21386/50.87245">OpenTopoMap</a> or browse the <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/List_of_OSM-based_services">maps listed on the wiki</a>.</p>
