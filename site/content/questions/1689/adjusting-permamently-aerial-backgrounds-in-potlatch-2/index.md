<div class="comment-text">
<p>The problem is that aerial imagery can and will be updated without such a service noticing. Assume that you had bing imagery with 50m offset and had supplied the offset data to the True Offset Process. Bing then updates with imagery that is better than 1m offset georeferenced, you end up moving correctly georeferenced imagery by 50m.</p>
<p>In summary there doesn't seem to be a better solution than manually checking before you edit. However there are ideas floating around about providing more and better documented reference points that could even be part of a "automatic" pre-edit setup process.</p>
