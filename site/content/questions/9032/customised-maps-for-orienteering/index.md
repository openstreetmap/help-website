<div class="answer-body">
<p>Absolutely no problem legally speaking. The OpenStreetMap license is intended to make it easy for interesting uses of maps like this. Just make sure you <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/copyright">give us credit as required</a>.</p>
<p>You're talking about temporary data for a specific orienteering event. Locations of checkpoints etc, so this is not data which you would put directly into the OpenStreetMap database, but you can easily layer this on top of a map in your desired outputs.</p>
<p>You asked about paper outputs and files for Garmin. Two quite different things, each with a many different approaches possible. I suggest you ask a different question more specifically when you get stuck on a particular aspect of these things, but broadly speaking as a starting point <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/OSM_on_paper">OSM on paper</a> lists various options of for putting maps on paper. <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/OSM_Map_On_Garmin">OSM map on Garmin</a> lists options for loading OSM onto garmin devices (including set-up for generating your own IMG files)</p>
<p>Oh and the other thing you'll find intersting is "<a href="http://oobrien.com/oom/">OpenOrienteeringMap</a>"! (And you're allowed to take printouts from this. All free open maps under the same license)</p>

<p>There is a service rendering OSM maps specifically for Orienteering that may get you towards your goal.
