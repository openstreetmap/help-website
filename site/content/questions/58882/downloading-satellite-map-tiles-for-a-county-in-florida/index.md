<div class="answer-body">
<p>OpenStreetMap is created by volunteers and is a project to collect geospatial data as vector data. Our volunteers cannot afford to buy a satellite. That's why we don't have satellite imagery. You could either check if governmental agencies offer public domain aerial or satellite imagery of Florida. For example, the Landsat imagery is public domain.</p>
<p>Or you go to a provider of satellite imagery as a tile service. Please check if the plan you are interested in allows fetching tiles in bulk for offline usage. Fetching tiles in bulk automatically puts much strain on a tile server. That's why it is <a href="https://operations.osmfoundation.org/policies/tiles/">not permitted</a> on the tile servers available at *.tile.openstreetmap.org because they are paid by donations and maintained by volunteers.</p>
