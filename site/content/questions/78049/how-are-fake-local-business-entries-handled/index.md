<p>Not really.</p>
<p>To prevent such abuse, we have only the interest and knowledge of local OSMers who are constantly working on improving the map and who have a vested interest in keeping it honest. Should OSM ever become as popular as Google Maps I believe some measures to ensure data integrity will need to be instituted. The OSM philosophy is to interfere with mappers as little as possible. While that's very nice in some ways, it leaves the door open to sabotage and misuse.</p>
<p>Others in the forum may have more to add to this. I hope this bit helps.</p>
