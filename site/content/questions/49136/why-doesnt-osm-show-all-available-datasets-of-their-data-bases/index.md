<p>The whole point behind OSM is that a great deal of data is packed into the underlying database, but that data is then used in different ways to serve specific mapping purposes. Compare, for instance, the <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/#map=14/53.0770/8.8040">"main" map</a> and the <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/#map=14/53.0770/8.8040&amp;layers=T">"transport" map</a> - they show the same area in completely different ways, each using a subset of the underlying data.</p>
