<p>highway=service<br />
access=private<br />
tiger:source=tiger_import_dch_v0.6_20070809<br />

tiger:reviewed=no<br />
</p>
<p>The way seems completely misplaced/nonsensical.</p>
<p>Viewing the underlying Bing satellite imagery, I can discern no road that would correspond to it. E.g., the way cuts diagonally across the map (while roads in the area are NS/EW oriented); and it has an oddly shaped loop at one end, etc.</p>
<p>** Can anyone point me to a page on the OSM wiki that discusses this type of situation, and what to do?</p>
<p>(I am inclined to delete the way, but I figure it is best to first find out what the agreed practice is for the situation.)</p>
<p>Thanks very much.</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>The policy is quite simple: If it is incorrect, correct it :-). That includes deleting ways that don't exist.</p>
<p>Before deleting, you might want to check:</p>
<ul>
<li>Does the way really not exist at all, or is it just misplaced? (You seem to have checked that already).</li>
<li>Is the satellite imagery up to date? Our reference is what actually exists on the ground, not sat imagery.</li>
</ul>
<p>If you have verified that the way does not exist, I see no problem with deleting it.</p>
<p>BTW: The <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tiger#Status">TIGER data</a> is known to be of problematic quality sometimes, so it's not quite unconceivable for it to be wrong.</p>
