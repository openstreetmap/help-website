<div class="answer-body">
<p><a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/w/index.php?search=trench&amp;title=Special%3ASearch">The wiki</a> leads to <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:barrier%3Dditch">barrier=ditch</a> and <a href="http://taginfo.openstreetmap.org/search?q=trench#values">taginfo</a> leads to <a href="http://taginfo.openstreetmap.org/tags/military=trench">military=trench</a> (I guess applying both tags is fine) and after asking a hundred of questions you should already be capable of using these two tools yourself.</p>
<p>If your trench is made of wood you can use the <a href="http://taginfo.openstreetmap.org/keys/?key=material#values">material</a> key and add <em>material=wood</em>. Tag the <a href="http://taginfo.openstreetmap.org/keys/surface#values">surface</a> with the most frequent material (probably <em>ground</em> in your case).</p>
<p>I can't understand your issue with the monument tag. If your editor doesn't have a monument <em>preset</em> for a way then just add the tag manually using the advanced tab. Yet, the <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:historic%3Dmonument">monument</a> tag is for objects <em>built to remember and show respect to a person or group of people</em>. Your trench wasn't built to show respect to anyone but as a fortification. Therefore this tag seems wrong. Maybe you are searching for a tag like <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:tourism%3Dattraction">tourism=attraction</a>. You can still add <em>historic=yes</em> of course.</p>
