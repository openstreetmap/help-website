<p>Refer to the photo below:</p>
<p><img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/A_track_with_two_different_surfaces.jpg/640px-A_track_with_two_different_surfaces.jpg" alt="One track with two different surfaces" /></p>
<p>This is a highway in a park. Beyond the diverging point - to the left - is a path with <code>surface=paving_stones</code> reserved for foot (jogging, running, etc).</p>
<p>To the right (I presume, after walking around) is closer to a service road with <code>surface=asphalt</code>. Probably more suitable for cyclists, but I've seen a vehicle that looks like a golf cart (this is not even a golf course) is using this service road as well. And of course, motor vehicles aren't allowed here.</p>
<p>The combination of these two is currently mapped as a <code>highway=path</code>. So, any ideas how to represent this two things together?</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>Especially</p>
<ul>
<li>if the access rules are different for both parts and</li>
<li>if the ways diverge also at other locations,</li>
</ul>
<p>I would map them as two parallel ways (with own highway (path/service), access and surface/smoothness tags). At your photo they even diverge and have to be mapped separately anyway.</p>
<ul>
<li>highway=path</li>
<li>foot=designated</li>
<li>surface=paving_stones</li>
<li>smoothness=intermediate</li>
</ul>
<p>and</p>
<ul>
<li>highway=service</li>
<li>private=yes <em>(that is just guessed because of your "golf carts" - adjust for reality!)</em></li>
<li>bicycle=permissive</li>
<li>surface=asphalt</li>
<li>smoothness=good <em>or even</em> smoothness=excellent</li>
</ul>
<p>Another option would be to treat the left path as "sidewalk" of a road (the one on the right). See <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Sidewalks#Sidewalk_as_refinement_to_a_highway">https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Sidewalks#Sidewalk_as_refinement_to_a_highway</a></p>
<p>Also note the comments below.</p>

<p>Separate ways should only be used where there's physical separation, which isn't the case here. The reality on the ground changes where the ways split/merge, and the mapping should reflect that.</p>
<p>I think using a sidewalk tag is the least bad option here.</p>
