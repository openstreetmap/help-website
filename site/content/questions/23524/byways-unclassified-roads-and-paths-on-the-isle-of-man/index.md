<div class="answer-body">
<p>OpenStreetMap is an international map and therefore can't show every single nuance of different countries' classification systems. In the UK, that means that the default map (the one you see as standard at openstreetmap.org) doesn't pick out byways, RUPPs etc. differently.</p>
<p>However, because OpenStreetMap gives away its raw data for free, anyone can download it and make a map that highlights their items of interest. One such map is <a href="http://www.bywaymap.com/">Byway Map</a>, which does exactly what you're after.</p>
<p>This does, of course, depend on whether local mappers have recorded information about byways/RUPPs. On the Isle of Man it looks like they haven't, and you might be best advised to stick with the Ordnance Survey map. If you were travelling to Wiltshire, the coverage there is much better!</p>
