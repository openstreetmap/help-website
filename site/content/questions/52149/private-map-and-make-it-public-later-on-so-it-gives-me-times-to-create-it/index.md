<div class="answer-body">
<p>While aseerel has given a good direct answer to your question, I would suggest that you do not make a big offline/private edit to later upload in one go, especially if you are a relative newbie:</p>
<ul>
<li>Somebody could have modified the data between the time you downloaded it and the time you upload your changes, complicating the upload</li>
<li>You'll miss out on automated and community feedback as you progress through your work. It's better to spot problems early before the bad practice has been applied to your whole work.</li>
<li>There's no benefit to withold edits until they are "perfect" or "final". Maping is an iterative process, a map is by nature incomplete and uneven. Embrace that fact while you work towards making OSM the best map.</li>
</ul>
<p>Upload a few permit zones at a time. If you are unsure about your edits, ask for somebody to review them, before they get so big that they get cumbersome to review.</p>

<p>You can use the editor <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/JOSM">JOSM</a> and save your .osm file locally, re-edit it later on, and at some day integrate it into the OSM data. Keep in mind that OSM data is ever changing, so "integrate" is important!</p>
<p>Now the downside: if you are "very new on this site" this is not that easy. JOSM is not that easy to use directly at the start (maybe depends if you are a techy person or not).</p>
<p>There are not really other options. Somehow a workaround is to make a map overlay in, for example, <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/UMap">uMap</a> as your playground and after you have collected all your info you can enter it, manually, as new data into OSM.</p>
<p>Suggestion: get to know OSM, work on other stuff first and then, later, return to your zones.</p>
