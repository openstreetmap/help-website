<p>Great that you want to use OSM maps! We like to see our maps being used!</p>
<p>Regarding OSM maps in general those previous questions should help you:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/questions/26432/">use-on-local-news-broadcast</a></li>
<li><a href="/questions/12861/">can-attribution-be-given-to-openstreetmaps-at-the-end-of-the-television-show-instead-of-on-the-maps-during-the-show</a></li>
</ul>
<p>For MapQuest layer (I try to explain in short: they, MapQuest, make a map using OSM data) see MapQuest's terms. <del>The general terms apparently are <a href="http://info.mapquest.com/terms-of-use/">there</a> - found via the mapquest link on the bottom of your map on osm.org. But I do not know if those also cover the "mapquest open" layer.</del> See SomeoneElse's link below to the terms for MapQuest Open. You may also want to look at <a href="http://open.mapquest.com/">http://open.mapquest.com/</a> . Maybe you could find previous relevant questions here about MapQuest - I could not in a quick search.</p>
<p>Maybe you would like to instead use for example <a href="http://openmapsurfer.uni-hd.de/?zoom=9&amp;lat=56.52073&amp;lon=21.13557&amp;layers=B000000FFFF">GIScience Heidelberg's Road map</a> which <a href="http://openmapsurfer.uni-hd.de/contact.html">is</a> "available under Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 2.0 license" (attribution of OpenStreetMap is still needed of course).</p>
