<div class="answer-body">
<p>The first place I'd look would be the "<a href="http://switch2osm.org/serving-tiles/">serving tiles</a>" section of the <a href="http://switch2osm.org/">switch2osm</a> website. That'll give you a bit of background about how the "map" actually works.</p>
<p>Next, have a look at the "<a href="http://switch2osm.org/using-tiles/">using tiles</a>" section, which describes using <a href="http://www.openlayers.org/">OpenLayers</a> or <a href="http://leaflet.cloudmade.com/">Leaflet</a> to display the map. It's using a toolkit such as that that you can "display something at a certain latitude and longitude". Neither OpenLayers nor Leaflet are specifically just OSM projects - there are lots of examples of what you can do with them elsewhere on the web.</p>
