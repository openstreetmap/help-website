<div class="answer-body">
<p>I think this might be considered an elaborate form of <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:traffic_sign=city_limit"><code>traffic_sign=city_limit</code></a> and or <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:man_made%3Dgantry"><code>man_made=gantry</code></a>.</p>
<p>There is also a set of tags for welcome signs <code>tourism=information</code> with <code>information=board</code> and <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:board_type"><code>board_type</code></a><code>=welcome_sign</code>.</p>
<p>Tagging the <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:maxheight:physical">clear height</a> beneath it may also be useful for some routers.</p>
<p>A combination of some or all of these on a node on the road may adequately capture the meaning, but an additional short description may not be a bad idea.</p>
