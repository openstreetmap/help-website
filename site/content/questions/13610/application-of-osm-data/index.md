<div class="answer-body">
<p>Taking these questions one at a time...</p>
<blockquote>
<p>I want to have a viewer to read the map on PC</p>
</blockquote>
<p>I'm presuming here that you want to do something with the data rather than having a map that looks just like the <a href="http://www.openstreetmap.org/?lat=37.1784&amp;lon=-83.4835&amp;zoom=13&amp;layers=M">OSM standard map</a> on your PC (after all, you can do that by just opening a web browser). If so, I'd start by looking at the <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Rendering">"rendering" wiki page</a> and probably <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Maperitive">Maperitive</a>.</p>
<blockquote>
<p>is it possible to combine the Vector from Open Cycle map and OSM?</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Not directly. The Opencylemap is created by combining OSM data, other third-party data (such as SRTM height info) and a lot of hard work. You could do something similar, but you'd have to combine the data yourself. From memory at least one worked example on the Maperitive site uses contour data though.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>I want to add routes, checkpoints on the map too, which software should I chose on PC Platform?</p>
</blockquote>
<p>It depends a bit what you mean by "routes". For example, I will at some point this summer walk from <a href="http://www.openstreetmap.org/?lat=53.6895&amp;lon=-0.7543&amp;zoom=12&amp;layers=M">here</a> to <a href="http://www.openstreetmap.org/?lat=53.965&amp;lon=-1.072&amp;zoom=11&amp;layers=M">here</a>, but as the route exists nowhere but in my own head (or as a series of waypoints on a GPS I'll be carrying) it doesn't belong in OSM. However, as some point in the future I'll also follow the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viking_Way">Viking Way</a> north from <a href="http://hiking.lonvia.de/en/?zoom=12&amp;lat=52.99131&amp;lon=-0.6195">here</a>. That does belong in OSM, because it's signposted on the ground.</p>
<p>If what you're talking about is a route of the first sort not the second, then I'd have a search through the OSM wiki (category <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Category:Software">software</a>) and this help site. You'll find a lot that targets specific GPS units (specifically Garmin - proprietary software such as <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/BaseCamp">Basecamp</a> and <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/MapSource">Mapsource</a> may meet your requirements). I'm sure that people will chip in with other suggestions.</p>

<p>I have tried JOSM, but it's too large to open it</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Just checking, but you're not trying to open the 21Gb of OSM planet that you downloaded in JOSM are you? If not, how much RAM does your PC have?</p>
<p>To download just a small area in JOSM I'd do the following:</p>
<ul>
<li>File / Download from OSM</li>
<li>Enter the placename I'm interested in and click "search"</li>
<li>Click on the entry I'm interested in in the search results and click "download"</li>
<li>Fetch the imagery I'm interested in using the "imagery" menu.</li>
</ul>
<p>If you haven't already seen it, have a look at the <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Beginners%27_guide">beginners' guide</a> pages, particularly the editing ones for <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Beginners_Guide_1.3.2">Potlatch</a> and <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Beginners_Guide_1.3.3">JOSM</a>.</p>
