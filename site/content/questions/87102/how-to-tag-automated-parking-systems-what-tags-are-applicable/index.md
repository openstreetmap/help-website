<p>At the very least I would think that the entrance could be tagged <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:amenity%3Dparking_entrance"><code>amenity=parking_entrance</code></a>.</p>
<p>As for the parking type for an <code>amenity=parking</code>, there don't appear to be any <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:parking">documented</a> values.</p>
<p>At time of writing there OSM appears to have:</p>
<ul>
<li>42 instances of <a href="https://taginfo.openstreetmap.org/tags/parking=mechanical%20lifts#overview"><code>parking=mechanical␣lifts</code></a></li>
<li>14 instances of <a href="https://taginfo.openstreetmap.org/tags/parking=mechanical_tower#overview"><code>parking=mechanical_tower</code></a> and</li>
<li>11 instances of <a href="https://taginfo.openstreetmap.org/tags/parking=automated_parking_system#overview"><code>parking=automated_parking_system</code></a></li>
</ul>

<div class="comment-text">
<p><code>=mechanical lifts</code> is unclear because a carpark can have car elevators still requiring drivers to park manually at each floor in ordinary parking spots in a hybrid mode, or varying extent of automation. In fact, there is one old design similar to the cylindrical type found in at least Japan, where the driver has to stay in car to back in and access the car on each level.</p>
<p>For <code>=automated_parking_system</code> , it's possible to have stacks, swapping platforms, and sleds in both <code>=underground</code> and <code>=mutli-storey</code> , meaning the use of <code>parking=</code> is a conflict.</p>
<p>The other fundamental issue with <code>parking</code> and <code>amenity=parking</code> is different type areas of the same carpark can't be represented consistently. There is commonly a mix of automated and manual parking spots, often to accommodate different heights and needs.</p>
