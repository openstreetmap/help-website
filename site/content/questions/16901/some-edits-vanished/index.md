<div class="answer-body">
<p>Getting the overview of the editing history of an area in osm is (yet) somewhat difficult. Nevertheless there are various tool allowing you to shed at last some light on an areas editing history:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="http://zverik.osm.rambler.ru/whodidit/">WhoDidtIt</a> - gives you the changesets that had nodes edited in a specific area. Yet, the "eternity" setting is set to 1000 days, IIRC. The tool typically gives a good overview of who has been active in a given area and more details can be obtained by browsing the respective changesets.</li>
<li>If you do know the ID of a deleted object, you can browse its history through the regular api, e.g. <a href="http://www.openstreetmap.org/browse/way/179389330/history">http://www.openstreetmap.org/browse/way/179389330/history</a> for the way you mention.</li>
<li>If you do not know the ID of a deleted way, you may try <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Potlatch_1/Primer#Undoing_mistakes">Potlatch1's "undo"-function</a>. In order to use Potlatch1, you now have to set Potlatch1 as your default editor on your osm-profile, and then open the area with the edit-tab. Once Potlatch1 is open, pressing the "U"-key (undelete) should bring up a list of deleted ways, giving the option to recover any of them.</li>
</ul>
