<p>In Czechia, lots of guideposts are near railway stations and they are always named like "Záboří nad Labem (žst)" <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/2211290197">see</a>. The shorthand <strong>žst</strong> means "železniční stanice" (railway station). Although it is not and abbreviation, the <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Names#Abbreviation_.28don.27t_do_it.29">Not abbreviate</a> rule coulb be used on first sight.</p>
<p>The problem however is, that the <em>expanded version</em> of the name "Záboří nad Labem (železniční stanice)" is very <strong>artificial</strong> and <strong>nowhere</strong> used. I.e.</p>
<ul>
<li>official listings of the operator have "Záboří nad Labem (žst)"</li>
<li>official maps from the operator have "Záboří nad Labem (žst)"</li>
<li>other touristic maps have "Záboří nad Labem (žst)" or "Záboří nad Labem - žst"</li>
<li>guidepost itself shows "Záboří nad Labem (žst)"</li>
<li>even the official Czech norm (ČSN 01 3411) for names in maps requires use ŽST in maps</li>
</ul>
<p>Should in this case rule "map as it appears on the street" be followed or we should use the very artificial long version.</p>
