<div class="answer-body">
<p>Mapbox imagery is made of different layers: <a href="https://www.mapbox.com/help/mapbox-satellite-sources/">https://www.mapbox.com/help/mapbox-satellite-sources/</a></p>
<p>I contacted Mapbox support on December 2016, and here is the answer:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Much of our <a href="https://www.mapbox.com/maps/satellite/">Satellite layer</a> is composited imagery, so we don't offer timestamps. <strong>Generally the imagery is between 6-18 months out</strong>, though coverage varies. Here are a few interesting reads about how our Satellite team <a href="https://www.mapbox.com/blog/satellite-imagery-updates-telemetry/">prioritizes</a> and <a href="https://www.mapbox.com/blog/three-million-km/">purchases</a> new imagery for Mapbox Satellite.</p>
<p>Glad you're loving our maps to help with OSM contributions! Have you seen these posts:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://www.mapbox.com/blog/streetlevel-photography/">https://www.mapbox.com/blog/streetlevel-photography/</a></li>
<li><a href="https://www.mapbox.com/blog/announcing-id-v2/">https://www.mapbox.com/blog/announcing-id-v2/</a></li>
<li><a href="https://www.mapbox.com/blog/mapping-3d-buildings/">https://www.mapbox.com/blog/mapping-3d-buildings/</a></li>
</ul>
</blockquote>
<p>Also, there is a tool to report areas that need a refresh (e.g., too cloudy): <a href="https://www.mapbox.com/bites/00240/#3/0.00/0.00">https://www.mapbox.com/bites/00240/#3/0.00/0.00</a></p>
<p>And also:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>I've also recommend this <a href="https://remotepixel.ca/projects/satellitesearch.html">Remote Pixel</a> tool (that uses Mapbox, but also directs you to other providers). I've heard mixed results, but sometimes people love it.</p>
</blockquote>
