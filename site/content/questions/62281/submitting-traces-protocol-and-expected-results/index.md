<div class="answer-body">
<p>Someone has to manually map the trace. In this case someone did it <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/557489098">22 days ago</a> in response to you asking about it on the forum, apparently. Normally people map using their own traces but there's no reason someone can't use yours (or anyone else's, as long as it's not private) too. If you want to ask the person who mapped your trace you can look at the author of the <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/557489098">new track</a>, see who added it and either send them a comment about the changeset or send them a personal message.</p>

<p>In addition, after the data gets added to OSM, there is a short wait until it makes its appearance on the garmin.openstreetmap.nl site. The owner of that site usually provides a fresh map with recent additions every week or so, sometimes longer. The new data is actually available almost immediately but for various reasons (server load, etc.) there is a short wait until it's available to consumers.</p>
