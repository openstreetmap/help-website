<p>psv= <em>stands for "public service vehicle" and includes bus=</em> and taxi=<em>. It is not clear wether hov=</em> includes psv or not.</p>
<p>HOV lanes can be tagged access=no, psv=yes, hov=yes. Bus lanes can often be found in bus terminals or in crowded city centers and can be tagged as access=no, psv=yes or bus=yes.</p>

<p>I'm not sure if <code>psv=*</code> actually includes taxis. The wiki page is not clear on that. Personally I never did and always set <code>psv=yes</code> and <code>taxi=yes</code> to roads that allow both and didn't bother for <code>taxi=no</code> otherwise. I doubt it's never wrong to set all of <code>psv=yes</code>, <code>bus=yes</code> and <code>taxi=no</code>. (Although I'm not sure if <code>bus=yes</code> includes non-psv buses).</p>

<p>I'm also not sure whether PSV should include taxis. In the UK 'public service vehicle' is an official legal term and taxis are not normally counted as PSVs. The legal term also seems to be used in the UK transport industry in the same way. I'd therefore not use the tag psv to refer to taxis.</p>
<p>See <a href="http://www.dft.gov.uk/vosa/repository/PSV%20Operator%20Licensing%20Guide%20for%20Operators%20April%202009.pdf">http://www.dft.gov.uk/vosa/repository/PSV%20Operator%20Licensing%20Guide%20for%20Operators%20April%202009.pdf</a> pages 8 and 9 for the full UK legal definition.</p>
<p>However other countries may have different definitions and I suppose OSM doesn't necessarily have to follow the legal definition.</p>
