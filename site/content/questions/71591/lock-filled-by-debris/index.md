<div class="answer-body">
<p>I'm still not clear what you question is. Up there you asked "<em>How to tag the debris that filled the lock</em>" and wrote something about a way being blocked and water not so. But I rather get the feeling you would like to get some guidance on how to tag the lock in its entirety.</p>
<p>A normal lock is tagged by e.g. <code>waterway=canal</code> + <code>lock=yes</code>. On the aerial image it looks like there is no canal anymore (it's been filled). Thus, I would remove the waterway altogether. Instead map the outline of the old lock and tag it with <code>man_made=lock</code>. This is consistent with bridge tagging where you put <code>bridge=yes</code> on the road and <code>man_made=bridge</code> on the actual structure. You could also use <code>man_made=lock_gate</code> to map the gates. Both tags <a href="https://taginfo.openstreetmap.org/search?q=man_made%3Dlock">have been used a couple of times</a>. If you want additionally tag the area inside with <code>surface=debri</code>s or an extra area with <code>landcover=debris</code>.</p>
<p>Alternatively, you could use <code>abondoned:waterway=canal</code> + <code>lock=yes</code> and <code>abondoned:waterway=lock_gate</code> at the nodes where the old gates are.</p>
<p>The current tagging (<code>disused:waterway=canal</code> + <code>waterway=lock</code> on the way of the canal and <code>barrier=debris</code> on the outline of the lock) is wrong in my opinion. For one the simultaneous use of <code>disused:waterway</code> and <code>waterway</code> seems wrong and <code>waterway=lock</code> is not the standard way of tagging locks. Additionally, I don't like <code>barrier=debris</code>. You probably think of a barrier against water but I think the usual OSM meaning of barrier is against some sort of traffic. Barriers against water are tagged e.g. as <code>waterway=dam/weir/lock_gate</code>.</p>
