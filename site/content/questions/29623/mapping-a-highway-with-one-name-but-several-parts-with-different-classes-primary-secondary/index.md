<div class="answer-body">
<p>I wouldn't assume a highway should be classified as different road types just because of different states of maintenance or surface quality.<br />
Have a look into the OSM wiki at <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Highways#Classification">how highways are classified in OSM</a>.<br />
Furthermore have a look at <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Surface">surface</a>, <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Proposed_features/smoothness">smoothness</a> and <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tracktype">tracktype</a>. Tracktype is used not only in combination with highway=track. 4wd_only=yes exists, too.</p>
<p>Own experience: Two years ago I travelled a highway whose surface was partly new asphalt, partly crumbled asphalt, partly fine gravel, partly pebblestone. All the way I tagged it highway=primary - since it was/is one. :)<br />

<p>Definitely yes, the highway should be classified by its importance/use. Use the surface or track type tags to indicate what the condition is like. There is an current discussion on this on the tagging email list if you are interested. <a href="https://lists.openstreetmap.org/pipermail/tagging/2014-January/thread.html">https://lists.openstreetmap.org/pipermail/tagging/2014-January/thread.html</a></p>
