<p>Newbie here struggling with how somewhere is displayed - probably down to zoom levels. If I search for Scarborough (UK)the first result in the search is <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/relation/149163">https://www.openstreetmap.org/relation/149163</a> this shows me Scarborough Borough Council's area and places up to around 50 miles away. The same if I search for Bridlington - <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/20628524">https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/20628524</a></p>
<p>I can work out that two of those links have the word "node" in them whereas the Scarborough one has "relation" in it. So it's obciously something to do with node and relation. But the Scarborough one includes the town of Whitby yet if I search for Whitby I get a link with "node" in it.</p>

<p>Scarborough <em>is</em> the town-level (in OSM terms) administrative relation here. The admin hierarchy can be seen at <a href="https://nominatim.osm.org/ui/details.html?osmtype=R&amp;osmid=149163&amp;class=boundary">https://nominatim.osm.org/ui/details.html?osmtype=R&amp;osmid=149163&amp;class=boundary</a> . In terms of administration, everything within that relation is part of the "Scarborough" admin area, including places within Whitby: <a href="https://nominatim.osm.org/ui/details.html?osmtype=N&amp;osmid=20923539&amp;class=place">https://nominatim.osm.org/ui/details.html?osmtype=N&amp;osmid=20923539&amp;class=place</a> .</p>
<p>There is a "Whitby Town Council": <a href="https://www.whitbytowncouncil.gov.uk/whitby-town-deal.html">https://www.whitbytowncouncil.gov.uk/whitby-town-deal.html</a> . The area of that doesn't seem to be mapped in OSM; if it was, I'd imagine it would be admin level 10 ("parish" in OSM terms).</p>

<p>The area shown is that covered by Scarborough Borough Council, which is higher level authority than a town council. There are 3 towns within SBC's area - Whitby, Scarborough and Filey. Whitby and Filey each has a parish/town council (probably the admin level 10 SomeoneoneElse refers to). Scarborough does not have a parish/town council. So perhaps the zoom level is governed by the admin level as suggested by ghia. thank you both. Jools</p>
