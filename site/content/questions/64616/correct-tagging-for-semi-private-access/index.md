<div class="answer-body">
<p>Welcome Twizy -- The routes should be drawn and tagged as pertains to their use by the general public, not those with a private arrangement with the landowner. How this is best tagged depends on the way that the payment is taken and the permission issued.</p>
<ul>
<li>If there's a physical barrier on the route where anyone may enter after paying a fee, tag this as <code>barrier=toll_booth</code> and further clarify the payment with a <code>toll=*</code> tag and possibly <code>toll:*=*</code> subtags.</li>
<li>If the payment is not a toll paid for the route, but rather something like a general use fee for the area that anyone may pay (even though some special people may be allowed to visit for free), tag the route <code>access=customers</code> and clarify the fee with a <code>fee=*</code> tag and possibly <code>fee:*=*</code> subtags.</li>
<li>If public access is generally not permitted but exceptions are made on an individual basis, tag the route <code>access=private</code>. You can also still add <code>fee=*</code> info if applicable.</li>
</ul>
<p>Note that there's no guarantee that any particular navigation app will respect these tags -- but it's a good idea to add them anyway.</p>
