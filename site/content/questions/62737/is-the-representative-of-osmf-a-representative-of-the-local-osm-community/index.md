<div class="answer-body">
<p>I think you are trying to approach the matter a bit from the wrong side.</p>
<p>The OSMF local chapters are formal local representatives of the OSMF. The OSMF is there to support the OSM community and the same should be the case at a national level. So they should be more thought of a service organisation for the mappers, that if necessary and sensible for a specific task, can take on the role of a representative of the community.</p>
<p>That model can very well co-exist with individual community members doing things as "members" of the local/national/regional OSM community.</p>
<p>As to informal representation: a number of countries have informal user groups that partially take on the same role as local OSMF chapters, however there is currently no really agreed on framework for them, outside of the offer to host and run a domain for them, see last question in <a href="https://wiki.osmfoundation.org/wiki/Trademark_Policy_FAQ">https://wiki.osmfoundation.org/wiki/Trademark_Policy_FAQ</a></p>
