<div class="answer-body">
<p>I would probably tag the area (shore and water) with <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:leisure%3Dswimming_area"><code>leisure=swimming_area</code></a>. The portion ashore could be tagged with <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:natural%3Dbeach"><code>natural=beach</code></a>, <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:landcover%3Dgrass"><code>landcover=grass</code></a> or whatever seems appropriate for the individual site.</p>

<p>The description of <code>natural=beach</code> says it is used "to mark a loose geological landform along the coast or along another body of water consisting of <em>sand, gravel, shingle, pebbles, cobblestones or sometimes shell fragments</em> etc". I wouldn't use it for the situation in your picture as that is clearly more grass than sand/gravel. But I know beaches along the rivers here that fit the description.</p>
<p>In your picture I would rather tag the shore area with <code>landcover=grass</code>.</p>
