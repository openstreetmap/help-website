<div class="answer-body">
<p>We have <code>amenity=brothel</code> but that's not a good fit imo. The wiki says it's for an "establishment." Nonetheless there are 16 occurrences worldwide of <code>amenity=brothel</code> + <code>brothel:street_prostitution=yes</code>, so it seems that's the most popular solution.</p>
<p>If it feels wrong (it's verging on "troll tagging"), you could just go with <code>prostitution=yes</code>, also in use. (Probably as a property of a bar or hotel, I'm guessing, so maybe the standalone version would just be <code>amenity=prostitution</code> -- but it seems that tag is not yet in use anywhere.)</p>
<p>Regardless, these tags would not go on the street itself, but rather on a node or area (or multipolygon) to the side.</p>
<p>J</p>
<p>edit - If you do map something like this, it's likely that another mapper might remove your addition as unverifiable. Consider adding a <code>source</code> tag with a link describing the regulations that permit this activity in this area, if possible. If the area is signed, describe the signs in your changeset comment.</p>
