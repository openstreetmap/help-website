<p>I don't know of a pre-made one without names, although you might have luck on one of the services listed on the <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/OSM_on_Paper">OSM on paper page</a> of the wiki.</p>
<p>If you're interested in trying to make one yourself I know Anita Graser has <a href="https://anitagraser.com/2014/06/07/toner-lite-styles-for-qgis/">published</a> a Toner like map style that you might be able to strip the labels off and use as a starting point in QGIS.</p>
