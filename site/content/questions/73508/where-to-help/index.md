<p>IMHO, what is most needed is people adding local knowledge about things that cannot be seen on aerial images: e.g. POI with opening hours, wheelchair access, access permissions, public toilets, benches, and a lot more.</p>
<p>If that is not what you prefer to do, you can take a look at one of the many tasks offered via the <a href="https://tasks.hotosm.org/">HOT tasking manager</a> where the main focus is on digitizing aerial photos for humanitarian purposes.</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>The <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Quality_assurance">QA</a> page of the wiki lists a lot of tools which can point you to problematic data. When I don't know where to start I usually load <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Osmose">Osmose</a> around my place. <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/MapRoulette">MapRoulette</a> is a nice one to go from one side of the world to the other, on thematic tasks.</p>
<p>The country-specific projects, usually documented on the wiki, can also be useful. In the US, I hear mainly of the <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/TIGER_fixup">TIGER import cleanup</a>, which seems to be a huge task, mostly doable remotely.</p>
<p>Following mailing-lists takes a lot of time, but gave me a lot of ideas of what could be improved.</p>
<p>Lately I played with <a href="https://github.com/KaartGroup/JOSM_MapWIthAI_plugin">MapWithAI</a> to import detected by FB which are not on OSM. Not exactly precise, but still gave me a lot of work, exploring missing tracks in my countryside.</p>
<p>Regards.</p>
