<p>This question is very similar to <a href="/questions/47977/how-to-map-a-paddlelock-gate-to-control-water-level-in-ponds">this question</a>, but asking about a different kind of flow / water level control mechanism.</p>
<p><span><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Moench_teichwirtschaft_15112011.jpeg/320px-Moench_teichwirtschaft_15112011.jpeg" alt="Pond water level mechanism photo" /></span> <span><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/de/e/e7/M%C3%B6nchsbauwerk4.png" alt="Pond water level control mechanism schematic" /></span><br />
<span class="small">(credits: image 1: Patrice77 <span>CC BY-SA 3.0</span>; image 2: Gerd Baumann)</span></p>
<p>Managed ponds (especially those to used for fish farming) often have a water level control mechanism similar to a sluice gate that consists of a drain pipe with an adjustable height in a small case. I don't know what it is called in English, but in German and some other languages it is called "monk" (see e.g. <span>German Wikipedia</span>). What is the best way to tag these?</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>Unless you find something better, I would go for this:</p>
<ul>
<li><code>waterway=flow_control</code></li>
<li><code>flow_control=moench</code></li>
</ul>
<p>But what about the tags <code>flow_control=overflow</code> or <code>flow_control=water_level</code> from <a href="http://taginfo.openstreetmap.org/keys/flow_control#values">http://taginfo.openstreetmap.org/keys/flow_control#values</a> ?</p>
<p>In such rare tagging cases I like to add a <code>description</code> (possibly just your Wikipedia link) tag, too.</p>
