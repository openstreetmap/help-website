<p>Many Japanese onsen towns have ashiyu places (足湯).<br />
They are public foot baths using hot water from a hot spring.</p>
<p>References:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ashiyu">https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ashiyu</a></li>
<li><a href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:People_in_ashiyu">https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:People in ashiyu</a></li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Question</strong>: How to tag an ashiyu?</p>
<p><code>bath:type=onsen</code> is not a correct choice, as <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/JA:Tag:bath:type%3Donsen">https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/JA:Tag:bath:type%3Donsen</a> explicitly says 足湯[...]には使用しないでください (translation: "<em>please do not tag ashiyus as onsen</em>")</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>I don't quite see what the issue is, simply use a distinct bath:type value.</p>
<p>See <a href="https://taginfo.openstreetmap.org/keys/bath%3Atype#values">https://taginfo.openstreetmap.org/keys/bath%3Atype#values</a></p>
<p>If you don't believe the already in use "foot_bath" captures enough information use "ashiyu".</p>
<p>Document it in the wiki <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key%3Abath%3Atype">https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key%3Abath%3Atype</a> for extra points.</p>
