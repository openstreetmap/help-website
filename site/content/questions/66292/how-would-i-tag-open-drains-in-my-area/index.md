<div class="answer-body">
<p>You can add all kinds of <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:waterway">waterways</a> to OSM. For <em>artificial</em> drains see <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:waterway%3Ddrain">waterway=drain</a>. Also take a look at the differences to <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:waterway%3Dditch">waterway=ditch</a>.</p>
<p>In case you are talking about manhole-like storm drains take a look at <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:manhole%3Ddrain">manhole=drain</a> and other <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:manhole">manhole</a> types.</p>

<p>If there is no visible bridge then the object going under the road is a tunnel. So either use tunnel=yes or <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:tunnel%3Dculvert">tunnel=culvert</a> (if appropriate) for the part of the drain that passes under the road. Optionally add layer=-1 for this part.</p>
