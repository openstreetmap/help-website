<div class="question-body">
<p>Hi,</p>
<p>Is that possible to use a tag to identify some shops that are part of a local organization?</p>
<p>I'm working for a local organization that explains to storekeepers that some customers would like to buy products without packaging (bulk products) to reduce their amount of waste generated. The organization certifies the shops once the storekeeper understands this purpose and accepts that customers buy some products (meat, vegetables...) with their own wrapping solutions (could be a jar, a re-used paper bag...).</p>
<p>Do you think it would be best to use a local tag like <em>&lt;organization_name&gt;_certified</em> or a more generic tag like: <em>customer_wrapping_solutions_accepted</em>?</p>
<p>Thanks</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>For me something generic like <code>customer_wrapping_solutions_accepted=yes</code> seem to be the better way to go. Who should know all the possibly thousands of local organizations who might issue such "certificates". Plus it has a slight touch of being spam to add some organization's name to shops.</p>
<p>I just do not know what a good tag key might be.</p>
<p>Some ideas:</p>
<ul>
<li><code>customer_wrapping_solutions_accepted=yes</code></li>
<li><code>bring_your_own_packaging=yes</code></li>
<li><code>bring_your_own_containers=yes</code></li>
<li><code>byop=yes</code></li>
<li><code>bulk_buy=yes</code></li>
<li><code>zero_waste_buy=yes</code></li>
</ul>
<p>After some searching with taginfo I also found keys which are already in use:</p>
<ul>
<li><code>zero_waste=yes</code> (<a href="https://taginfo.openstreetmap.org/keys/zero_waste">key usage: 1</a>)</li>
<li><code>bulk_purchase=yes</code> (<a href="https://taginfo.openstreetmap.org/keys/bulk_purchase">key usage: 88</a>; even has a <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key%3Abulk_purchase">OSM wiki page</a>, also is listed as a <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:shop%3Dsupermarket#Useful_combination">useful combination with <code>shop=supermarket</code></a>, however I (and apparently some others) think that the words "bulk" might be misleading - see <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Talk:Key:bulk_purchase#Key:bulk_purchase_-_is_it_only_large.3F">a discussion</a>)</li>
<li><code>bulk=yes</code> (<a href="https://taginfo.openstreetmap.org/keys/bulk">key usage: 1</a>)</li>
</ul>
<p>So, after all, I would suggest to use <strong><code>bulk_purchase=yes</code></strong> or (if the shop only has this form of shopping) <strong><code>bulk_purchase=only</code></strong>.</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>Hi,</p>
<p>I would like to relaunch this discussion. After a long thought, I think we need to distinguish different kinds of behaviours:</p>
<p>1) the shops that sell bulk products and the customer can decide if he wants to use the shop's packaging or not. The customer doesn't need to ask not to have the packaging because he can pick the product up himself without the need of someone. That's the case when the shop uses this kind of boxes that the customer can directly use:</p>
<p><img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/f8/Vrac_prixbas.jpg" alt="alt text" /></p>
<p>2) the shops that sell bulk products but where someone, often a shop assistant or seller, will package it automatically when buying the product. This shops are shops like bakery, butcher's, coffee roasted, cake shop... Some of these shops will accept that the customers use their own packages but the difference with the previous category is that the customer needs to ask the seller for this service. Also that's often something that is not visible from the outsite of the shop, or even inside the shop without talking with a seller. In some cases the shops accepting that customers uses their own packaging are identified with a sticker like this one (from the Zero Waste organization) and visible by the customers without asking:</p>
<p><img src="https://zerowastelille.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/Sticker_ZWLille-2-350x350.jpg" alt="alt text" /></p>
<p>For the first category, I propose to use the tag <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:bulk_purchase">bulk_purchase</a> and for the second one, I propose to create a new tag <code>bring_your_own_container</code> with values <code>no</code>, <code>yes</code> and <code>only</code>.</p>
<p>This solution is a mix of what <a href="https://help.openstreetmap.org/users/5179/aseerel4c26">@aseerel4c26</a> proposed and the <code>bulk_purchase</code> tag, making a distinction.</p>
