<div class="answer-body">
<p>Flooded areas are usually coming from a coastline or riverbank temporarily broken in OSM. This is usually fixed quickly by other contributors but then you will have to wait until the change is forwarded to the data consumers like www.osmmaps.com.</p>
<p>You can check the nearest costline or riverbank in your area, verify its quality (e.g. for coastlines, <a href="http://tools.geofabrik.de/osmi/?view=coastline&amp;lon=-74.94067&amp;lat=40.29964&amp;zoom=6&amp;overlays=coastline,coastline_error_lines,line_not_a_ring,line_overlap,line_invalid,line_direction,questionable,coastline_error_points,unconnected,intersections,not_a_ring,double_node,tagged_node">OSM Inspector</a>; or for a multipolygonal riverbank, <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Servers/analyser.openstreetmap.fr">this relation analyzer</a>). Maybe the problem is already fixed and you just have to load the latest version of the Garmin map. If the OSM data is wrong and correcting is is out of your OSM skills, you may request some help at the wwww.osmmaps.com <a href="http://www.osmmaps.com/contact.html">contact</a>, or directly check with the US community at <a href="http://forum.openstreetmap.org/">forums</a> or <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Mailing_lists">mailing list</a> and ask if some more experimented contributor could check the area.</p>

<p>As I understand it, you're seeing tile flooding on a Garmin map. The map data used by the current download on that site is January 02, 2013. I'm not seeing any flooding there <a href="http://www.openstreetmap.org/?lat=36.509&amp;lon=-79.8067&amp;zoom=13&amp;layers=M">on the OSM standard map</a> so perhaps (a) it's been fixed since 2nd Jan or (b) it's an issue specific to the way these particular Garmin maps were produced. I'd <a href="http://www.osmmaps.com/contact.html">contact</a> the author of the particular maps that you're using.<br />
</p>
<p>You may also want to try an alternative Garmin download purely for comparison purposes (perhaps <a href="http://garmin.openstreetmap.nl/">this one</a>) to see if flooding exists there too.</p>
