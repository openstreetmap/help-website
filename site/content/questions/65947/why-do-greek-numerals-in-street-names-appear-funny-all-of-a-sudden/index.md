<div class="question-body">
<p>There's this funny little system in Greece (more common in some places than in others), where some minor streets will be named after the main street from which they branch out: "X Street" will have branches "1st X Side-Street", "2nd X Side-Street", and so on. In Greek, that will be written with Greek numerals, like this: "Οδός Χ" and its subsidiary "Α΄ Πάροδος Χ", "Β΄ Πάροδος Χ" etc. Until recently, the keraia character (΄) appeared correctly next to its respective letter, as in the preceding examples. Now it appears above them, as in the following image (see "Α΄ Πάροδος Προύσης", "Α΄ Πάροδος Ζαλόγγου" and "Γ΄ Πάροδος Ζαλόγγου").</p>
<p><img src="/upfiles/Map_screenshot.png" alt="alt text" /></p>
<p>What's changed? And how can it be fixed?</p>

<td><div class="item-right">
<div class="answer-body">
<p>The behaviour depends on which precise character is used for the keraia. In the examples above, it is Unicode U+0384 GREEK TONOS. I also checked another object I had created (in Cyprus) that has U+00B4 ACUTE ACCENT: this also behaved in the same way (accent typeset above the character, extra space after it).</p>
<p>However, according to <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_numerals#Description,">https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_numerals#Description,</a> 'The modern keraia is a symbol (ʹ) similar to the acute accent (´), the tonos (U+0384,΄) and the prime symbol (U+02B9, ʹ), but has its own Unicode character as U+0374.'</p>
<p>So, I have modified way 100506456 (<a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/100506456)">https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/100506456)</a> to use the correct character U+0374 GREEK NUMERAL SIGN, and it now renders correctly.</p>
<p>The trouble is, there may be no easy way of entering this symbol on standard Greek keyboards!</p>
