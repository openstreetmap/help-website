<div class="question-body">
<p>In the alpine area, near the tree line there are a lot of areas that are covered with just a few trees. There are far too many to map each tree individually, but the trees are too far apart to call it a forest or woodland. The trees are mostly pines, so the scrub tag is not correct. On other maps I have seen that these areas are rendered as a green dotted area, but I do not know if OSM has a tag for this. Has anyone got any examples of the correct tags?</p>

Long ago someone suggested to me Hexbinning tree locations as a way of estimating density.</p>

<p><code>wood:density=</code> (~15k instances) was raised in <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Talk:Tag:natural=wood#Density_of_woodland">https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Talk:Tag:natural=wood#Density_of_woodland</a> , most being <code>=sparse</code> (~13k instances) <a href="https://taginfo.openstreetmap.org/keys/wood=density#values">https://taginfo.openstreetmap.org/keys/wood=density#values</a> . Other <code>*:density=</code> was proposed for <code>=scrub</code> and <code>=shrubbery</code>.</p>
