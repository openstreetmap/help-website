<td><div class="item-right">
<div class="answer-body">
<p>Elevation data is largely gathered from the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shuttle_Radar_Topography_Mission">NASA SRTM</a> project (yes, that NASA), not from survey. It is not held by OpenStreetMap, and OpenStreetMap has no way to correct it.</p>
<p>If you wish to raise a systemic problem with the OpenCycleMap rendering, you can do so at <a href="http://trac.openstreetmap.org">trac.openstreetmap.org</a>, making sure to select the correct 'component'. Use your OpenStreetMap username and password to log in.</p>
<p>That said, it's well known that there are "voids" (holes) in the SRTM data - it's just a limitation you have for getting a lovely free source of elevation data from the Space Shuttle guys.</p>
