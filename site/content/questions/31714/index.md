<div class="answer-body">
<p>That depends whether there are remaining visible structures or if they have been completely removed. If they are gone forever and there is no trace left on the ground, then just delete the corresponding buildings. If they are still visible on aerial imagery it might be also a good idea to just remove the tags and add a <em><a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:note">note</a></em> explaining that the buildings have been demolished.</p>
<p>If the buildings are just damaged you can use <em><a href="http://taginfo.openstreetmap.org/tags/building=damaged">building=damaged</a></em> instead, which is already used by the <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Damaged_buildings_crisis_mapping">Typhoon Haiyan mapping</a>. But this tagging scheme is not very ideal because usually the <em>building</em> tag describes the <em>type</em> of the building and not its <em>condition</em>.</p>
<p>Additionally you should also add a corresponding <em><a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Landuse">landuse</a></em>. If the village is now part of the cloal mine then <em>landuse=quarry</em> and <em>resource=coal</em> will fit.</p>
</div>
