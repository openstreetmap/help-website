<p>I think you can do that in some of the editors but so far I have preferred to manually trace my tracks.<br />
If there is more than one gpx trace, you can draw your way by best matching between them all. You will also likely have satellite imagery in addition to your gpx trace to assist in making the most accurate route. I would expect that manually drawing the way will result in less nodes too and a truer representation of the actual way with out all the gpx wiggles. You won't have to contend with the birds nests in the trace, though these should be tidied up before uploading the gpx trace to the osm site.<br />
But I do see the advantages of getting the editor to convert it, especially where you have made a long and remote gpx along an unmapped hiking path where there is no assistance from satellite imagery.</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>You <em>cannot auto "convert"</em> it in iD.</p>
<p>A road is created by drawing a line over a trace, You have to use the trace as a background for drawing a road.</p>
<p>If using a PC follow these steps:</p>
<ol>
<li>connect the device with the gpx file</li>
<li>zoom in on map and click edit choose ID</li>
<li>click in the item on the right hand side the looks like a pile of papers ( layers)</li>
<li>scroll down list to the end and click gpx files</li>
<li>find the gpx file and drag it on the map</li>
<li>Do you see the trace? good</li>
<li>Now use the line tool to draw your path or road over the trace</li>
<li>Tag it as road or path and name it if it a a name.</li>
<li>Is it all OK then SAVE it.</li>
</ol>
