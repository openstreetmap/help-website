<div class="answer-body">
<p>First of all, this degree of micro detail is not widespread yet. You will be counting the amount of <code>entrance=</code> added in OSM only. Better find your country's open data, if they have this info at all on buildings.</p>
<p>This seems difficult to define. What about private, service, and emergency ones? Then there's the problem of internal doors to a courtyard, so you would need think about building doors vs site access points (which may not be a door, or have any gates) to public streets. An access point could further have multiple sides, with individual doors added at the extreme end. If there is a external staircase, above-ground access, or even individual doors on each floors, you will effectively be double counting many. Computing this will be expensive, or not possible entirely from the lack of tags.</p>
<p>Could be more narrow yet specific to count <code>addr:flats=</code> (interestingly only half are used together with <code>entrance=</code>), and <code>addr:unit</code> if it is used according to the original definition of different parts accessible by a certain door only (instead of the common meaning of "unit" that's akin to <code>addr:door=</code>). Address data is expected to be more complete in OSM, although the extent is more on <code>addr:housenumber=</code> depending on if there is an import. Then you will at least have some idea of how they are distributed.</p>
