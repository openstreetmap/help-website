<p>I have stumbled (almost literally) over a situation when a footpath is practically unwalkable. A very narrow passage, kind of a step on the wall facing the sea. Here is the <a href="http://osm.org/go/xR1W~qT7V?layers=N&amp;m=">link</a> and here is the <a href="http://binged.it/20RD18r">aerial image</a>. The width in the narrowest place is about 50 cm, and it's like one or two metre above the sea level. One can pass, and it is definitely worthy mapping. However I was with a baby push-chair, so it was out of question. I was disappointed to find no way out and being forced to turn back.</p>
<p>So I am wondering: what's the best way to tag it? So far I've added the width tag to it and tagged it as unsuitable for bicycles and wheelchairs. But probably it will in no way reflect its display in e.g. OsmAnd. Is there maybe a better tag?</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>You should add width, wheelchair (which you did), smoothness, incline and if appropriate sac_scale tags.<br />
If it has steps, map it as step (or at least some bunches of steps).<br />
If it is a path, map it as path.</p>
<p>The combination should make users and routers aware of how walkable the footpath is.</p>
