<td><div class="item-right">
<div class="answer-body">
<p>Combining the two tags <strong>barrier=wall</strong> and <strong>wall=noise_barrier</strong> is now (2018) by far the most common approach. The tags are documented in the OSM wiki on the <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:barrier%3Dwall">Tag:barrier=wall</a> page and others.</p>
<p>Some historical context: A possible alternative at the time of the question was <code>barrier=noise_barrier</code>. Back then, neither had been used much (<a href="http://taginfo.openstreetmap.org/tags/barrier=noise_barrier">58</a> and <a href="http://taginfo.openstreetmap.org/tags/wall=noise_barrier">29</a> times, respectively), and neither was documented in the wiki. Another variant, <code>barrier=wall + type=noise_barrier</code>, was also found in the database in small numbers. However, the very generic key name "<code>type</code>" is already being used widely for relation types and should therefore be avoided for other uses. As explained on the <span>Key:type</span> wiki page, preferable alternatives would be <code>wall:type=*</code> or the aforementioned <code>wall=*</code>.</p>

<p>I think just barrier=wall would work (and is used). You could add tags for other attributes if you wanted like <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:height">height</a>. It looks like some people have added <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:wall">wall</a>=foo, in some cases describing the material the wall is made of (eg stone) or its purpose (eg noise barrier). (see <a href="http://taginfo.openstreetmap.org/keys/wall#values">taginfo</a>) It could be argued that what a wall is made of should be tagged with something like material= <em>instead of wall=</em></p>
