<div class="answer-body">
<p>Following a practice I have found this to be the easiest procedure.</p>
<ol>
<li>Zoom right in to max at edit point</li>
<li>click edit choose Potlach2 and click the plus + to mag a bit more</li>
<li>Click on road where it will join mini roundabout while pressing shift to create a node and click scissors symbol to cut it</li>
<li>repeat for other 3 roads</li>
<li>click on middle node and with just that highlighted click bin/delete symbol.</li>
<li>highlight and delete to other bits</li>
<li>click and highlight one of the four roundabout nodes now create a new node between this and the next of the four and continue until you have rough circle of 8</li>
<li>use circle tool to make neat</li>
<li>While still highlighted tag from box on left as a suitable road type and choose the correct one way tag</li>
<li>if all looks good save it and write for example "Mapped roundabout using bing, survey and local knowledge". that's it, job done</li>
<li>It would take five minutes with practice.</li>
</ol>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>I've taken Andy Mackey's answer, but just switched around a few of his points to make it a lot easier (for me at least). Where he starts, making nodes on the original road(s), made them behave (sometimes) erratically (i.e. deleting a complete road).</p>
<ol>
<li>Zoom right in to max at edit point.</li>
<li>Click edit and zoom to a decent detail-level.</li>
<li>Make a circle (your future roundabout) and click on the 'circle' tool to make it round. MAKE SURE that the roads that will be included in the roundabout, will have a node ON them. Do this by clicking ON the road(s) while making your circle (you should see a node point with an extra circle around it).</li>
<li>With your circle still highlighted, add the appropriate road type (go to the left window), be sure to also pick the 'Roundabout=YES' type from the most-right tab of this window. This will automatically set it as 'one way'. Make sure the one-way direction is correct, if not, click on the 'change-direction' icon.</li>
<li>Click on one of the roads to highlight it, then click on the node/intersection with your future roundabout.</li>
<li>Use the 'scissor'-tool to divide it.</li>
<li>Do this for the other roads also.</li>
<li>Click on the roads INSIDE your roundabout, and then use the 'Bin' to delete them.</li>
<li>If all looks good, save it and write for example "Mapped roundabout using .........".</li>
</ol>
<p>Good luck !!!</p>
