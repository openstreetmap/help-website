<div class="answer-body">
<p>Do you want to easily find out tile numbers in your web browser?</p>
<ol>
<li>Just go to <a href="http://mc.bbbike.org/mc/?lon=51.5273&amp;lat=25.2806&amp;zoom=14&amp;num=1&amp;mt0=mapnik">http://mc.bbbike.org/mc/?lon=51.5273&amp;lat=25.2806&amp;zoom=14&amp;num=1&amp;mt0=mapnik</a> (or any other slippy map with OpenLayers) (on the OSM homepage this does not work anymore),</li>
<li>navigate to your desired location,</li>
<li>right click the desired tile on the map,</li>
<li>select the first entry "show image"(Firefox)/"open image in new tab"(Chromium),</li>
<li>then just look at your browser's address/URL bar which will show e.g. <a href="https://a.tile.openstreetmap.org/14/10537/7001.png">https://a.tile.openstreetmap.org/14/10537/7001.png</a> .</li>
<li>In this address you find the tile numbers (<code>14/10537/7001</code>) in a format which you can paste directly into the JOSM download window.</li>
</ol>
<p>Other interesting tools are at <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Slippy_map_tilenames#Tools">https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Slippy_map_tilenames#Tools</a></p>
</div>
