<div class="answer-body">
<p>Those are two independent tagging questions:</p>
<ul>
<li>If there is a turning circle, add <code>highway=turning_circle</code> to the node.</li>
<li>If there is (from your reality knowledge!) no way out from that point (except going back the street) for any mode of transport (e.g. due to walls, private properties, grass area with no paths on it), then you ''could'' add <code>noexit=yes</code>, too. This information is meant for other mappers but not for users of our data. If there is no doubt (as stf said) that there is no exit, then you could omit the <code>noexit=yes</code> tag – but it also does not hurt if you add it.</li>
</ul>
<p>Also see <span>Key:noexit</span> in our docu wiki and the discussion at the other answer here.</p>

<div class="comment-text">
<p>In this case the satellite imagery shows a turning circle so that is applicable.</p>
<p>The noexit tag is totally independent and is really only used for two purposes: First for QA tools to know that a way that almost but not quite connects to another is correct and second to let other (probably armchair) mappers know that two ways do not connect. Neither are the case in the area you linked as it is obvious that the road ends there with no continuation.</p>
<p>Routing software does not use the noexit tag but simply relies on the connections that exist between ways in the database.</p>
