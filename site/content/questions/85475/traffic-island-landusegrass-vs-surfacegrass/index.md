<div class="answer-body">
<p>Technically they are all different tags.</p>
<p><code>landuse=grass</code> mostly exists because of the easily unpleasing semantics of <code>natural=</code>, and lacking support of <code>landcover=</code>. There were no other top-level feature in the past. Problem is it is inside a <code>landuse=highway</code> in this case, and the rest are inside other <code>landuse=</code> (including <code>landuse=railway</code>).</p>
<p><code>surface=</code> alone is not valid syntax. It is an attribute describing a feature, usually <code>highway=</code> and <code>leisure=pitch</code>, also <code>natural=beach</code>. <code>landcover=</code> would be more orthodox on its own.</p>
<p>There has been <code>area:highway=traffic_island</code> + <code>surface=grass</code> for a long time (a decade). That's what should be used.</p>
<p>(A deeper issue is a "traffic island" is not the same as a median / central reservation, if that's what you are looking for. The definition is less distinctive on short sections, or at junctions. In any case, don't use <code>traffic_calming=island</code> if it's not traffic-calming.)</p>
</div>
