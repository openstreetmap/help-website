<p>If barrier=gate is applied to a node in a single navigable way, or a node that joins the ends of two navigable ways, it's simple enough to interpret.</p>
<p>But what does it mean if barrier=gate is applied to a T junction or crossroads?</p>
<p>For example: <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/1590988097">https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/1590988097</a> <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/1354048462">https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/1354048462</a> <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/25417796">https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/25417796</a> <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/3161500758">https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/3161500758</a> <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/90386298">https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/90386298</a></p>
<p><strong>Edit:</strong> And if these are errors, what should I do with them? I've got 260 in the UK, but I gather <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Notes">creating notes automatically</a> isn't cool.</p>

<p>Looking at your examples, I suspect it means they've been mapped in the wrong place. As you suggest it makes no sense to put a gate node at such a point - in each of your examples it should have probably been mapped as a short distance along the minor or private way at each junction, though it would need re-surveying to be sure of that.</p>

<p>Personally, I'd do the following:</p>
<ol>
<li>If it's somewhere local, I'd go and have a look to make sure.</li>
<li>If not, and the edit is by a current OSM contributor, I'd introduce myself and politely explain (probably in a changeset comment) what the problem is, and what options may sense.</li>
<li>If the edit's by a contributor who has long gone, but there are lots of local mappers, I'd probably contact one of them, or more likely just add a note that they will see.</li>
<li>If none of the above apply, and the actual location is <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/1354048462">obvious</a>, I'd just fix it, with a changeset comment explaining what's happened.</li>
</ol>
