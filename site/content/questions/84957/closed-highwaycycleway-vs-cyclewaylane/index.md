<div class="answer-body">
<p>Depends what you mean by "an inherent part of the road itself".</p>
<p>If it's just a painted lane, then you shouldn't draw a separate way.</p>
<p>If there's physical separation (e.g. a kerb or a stepped track) then a separate way is usually more appropriate.</p>
<p>It's similar to the way that we use separate ways for dual carriageways (divided highways), but not for multi-lane roads.</p>

<p>Hi, have you found this : <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Bicycle">https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Bicycle</a> ? It is the most elaborate tag list for cycleways I found. It clearly states : Cycle features OSM distinguishes between cycle lanes and cycle tracks. A cycle lane lies within the roadway itself (on-road), whereas a cycle track is separate from the road (off-road). Tracks are typically separated from the road by e.g. curbs, parking lots, grass verges, trees, etc.</p>
<p>So drawing a separate way, when the cycleway is an inherent part of the road itself is wrong and should be corrected, Imo</p>
