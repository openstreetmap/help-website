<div class="answer-body">
<p>Some will say you should not map anything without ground survey. I'd say that you need a really clear imagery, or street-level photos, to map trails without ground knowledge.</p>
<p>If you really need it, you could follow the logic of <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Lifecycle_prefix">Lifecycle prefixes</a>, something like <code>probable:highway=path</code>. That way, the feature will not be used by any renderer or router, but you can still display it and go check on the ground.</p>
<p>You could also use the <code>fixme</code> tag, that will display as red in some renderers (OsmAnd with the right option activated for example), to warn users that the mapping should be confirmed.</p>
<p>Hope this helps.</p>
