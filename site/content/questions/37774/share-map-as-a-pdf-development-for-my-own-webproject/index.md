<div class="answer-body">
<p>The www.osm.org's website's source is fully open (AFAIK). See <span>The Rails Port</span> in our docu wiki. So you can look up every bit you are interested in (and you can even re-use - "licensed under the GNU General Public License 2.0", see the readme/license file). For the "share" function a starting point would be <a href="https://github.com/openstreetmap/openstreetmap-website/blob/b194a3bf962cadc0c2faa0c49941e72a4b8d533f/app/assets/javascripts/leaflet.share.js">/app/assets/javascripts/leaflet.share.js</a> in the source tree.</p>
<p>For the Leaflet toolkit see <span>leaflet in our docu wiki</span>. It may work similarly with OpenLayers of course. You can look up much of the technical details of OSM-related software in our docu wiki.</p>
<p>In case you want to use other server's resources, please take care to obey the usage limits/restrictions. In case of osm.org's server: I do not know if linking to osm.org's server-side pdf generation is allowed at all as this is quite resource-demanding, I guess. But, as always, you can run it on your own server.</p>
<p>Note: I am not really involved in that development myself, however, I think that it may help.</p>
