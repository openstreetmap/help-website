<div class="answer-body">
<p>You have not given links to which service you are downloading data and reading instruction from but I assume it may be <a href="http://www.gmaptool.eu/en/content/installation">http://www.gmaptool.eu/en/content/installation</a> or the following link.<br />
If you have still not progressed you could <strong>try one of the other providers</strong> as listed here <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/OSM_Map_On_Garmin/Download">https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/OSM_Map_On_Garmin/Download</a></p>
<p>This process is often confusing initially and some of the site help pages may be clearer than others for you, but once sorted out you will find a preferred method which suits you best.<br />
You do just need to copy a gmapsupp.img file to the correct directory on your gps and enable it under settings.<br />
For your mac you will probably need a .gmap file and then use Garmin Basecamp to read it, or you could use Garmin Basecamp to read the maps on your gps when plugged in. BC is not very intuitive to use but some like it.</p>
<p>There is a Garmin Maps sub forum on the OSM forum page if you get interested in making your own maps using Mkgmap at a later stage<br />
<a href="https://forum.openstreetmap.org/">https://forum.openstreetmap.org/</a></p>

<p>PCs can't normally open a Garmin .img file. In my experience only Garmin Basecamp software will open a Garmin map file or a Garmin GPS device. Older Garmins need the file renamed to Gmapsupp.img. I have a an Oregon 450 it can open .img files with other names. There are several explanations of the process in this forum. You could try searching it. This Guy gives a good description on his cycling blog. <a href="https://www.dcrainmaker.com/2019/08/how-to-install-free-maps-on-your-garmin-edge.html">https://www.dcrainmaker.com/2019/08/how-to-install-free-maps-on-your-garmin-edge.html</a> Please tell us your failures and progress so we can help further.</p>
