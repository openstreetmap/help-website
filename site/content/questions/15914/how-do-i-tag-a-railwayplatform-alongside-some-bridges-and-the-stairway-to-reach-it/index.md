<div class="answer-body">
<p>It is complete correctly tagged.</p>
<p>The platforms and the part of the tracks that are above other features (the street and the river) have a layer 1, because they are relatively over these features.</p>
<p>The stairway and the rest of the tracks don't need a layer tag because they are not relatively over something else.</p>

<div class="comment-text">
<p>No, not necessary. These are two different stories: Do you perceive the platform over the street and water as a different object than the rest of the platform, i.e. is it visibly a bridge? Then make it a distinct object. If the platform is rather an uniform object, then it can happily stay a single object.</p>
<p>The other is about the layer tags: they help to stack the objects (e.g. on a map) in a proper <em>relative</em> order. As there is nothing above the platform or a part of it, it may have an arbitrarily high label number. So "1" or "2" doesn't matter as long as all objects below have "0" or less.</p>
