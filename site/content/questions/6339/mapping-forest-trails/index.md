<p>Hi,</p>
<p>This question is about those forest trails that exist primarily beause a lot of people have used them (unpaved), or may be gravel paved but narrow and not suitable for normal bicycle use.</p>
<p>Are they highway=path, bicycle=no - or highway=footpath - or are both eually fine?</p>
<p>The picture in the UI of the new simple tag system in Potlatch implies that footpaths are constructed paths...</p>
<p>What about mountain bikes? Many such trails are rideable by mountain-bike, but it may not be allowed (which means that you could ride that trail on a normal bike - if you'd be willing to do that to your bicycle)</p>
<p>But other trails would not be rideable by a normal bicycle under any circumstances, only an mbt would do.</p>
<p>What I am after it some thing like this</p>
<ul>
<li>highway=trail</li>
<li>bicycle=possible</li>
</ul>
<p>Regards, Hans Olav</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>You should tag them with <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:highway%3Dpath">highway=path</a> and use <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:mtb:scale">mtb:scale</a> for bicycle classification. Further tags like <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:sac_scale">sac_scale</a>, <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:surface">surface</a> or <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:trail_visibility">trail_visibility</a> might be useful, too. Using these tags allows a better classification than the rather simple <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:highway%3Dtrail">highway=trail</a>.</p>
</div>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>The <code>bicycle</code> tag is for the legal access rights for cycling along that path, on any type of (non-motorised) bike. So this will depend on general access rights in your country, plus any local by-laws or restrictions.</p>
<p>If you have a general right to cycle along that path, it should be tagged as <code>bicycle=yes</code>. If cycling is generally allowed by landowner, that can be tagged as <code>bicycle=permissive</code>. If cycling is forbidden, then <code>bicycle=no</code>.</p>
<p>Then use extra tags (eg <code>mtb:scale</code>) for how easy it is to cycle along it, or whether a mountain bike is required.</p>
</div>

<p>This kind of path is often called a "footpad" - a trail that exists only through the action of feet on soil. There isn't a good, official way to describe it in OSM. highway=trail sounds ok, but it has very little usage.</p>
<p>I did just discover "highway=path, path=hiking". Maybe that's suitable?</p>
