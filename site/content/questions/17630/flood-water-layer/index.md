<p>I'm not quite sure what you mean by "100-year flood plain", but in OSM we generally (with few exceptions) map things that are visible on the ground right now - not things that are predicted, or planned, or historic!</p>
<p>One of the reasons for this approach is verifiability: We don't want opinion in OSM, we want facts. As long as everybody sticks to observable facts, everyone else can simply go and check whether the data accurately reflects the situation on the ground.</p>
<p>Having said that, everyone is of course free to display an OpenStreetMap map with a custom overlay on top of it that shows whatever it is they want to show - see <a href="http://openlayers.org/dev/examples/kml-layer.html">http://openlayers.org/dev/examples/kml-layer.html</a> for an example of how to overlay KML data on a map.</p>
