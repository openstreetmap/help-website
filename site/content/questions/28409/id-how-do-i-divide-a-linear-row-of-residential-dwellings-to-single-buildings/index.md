<p>Here's one way:</p>
<ol>
<li>Click on nodes of the way (create new ones if necessary) and split the way at them (shortcut X).</li>
<li>To continue one side of the way across the gap to the other side, click on the way, then shift-click on the end node, select Continue this line (shortcut A) and draw a line to the node on the other side.</li>
<li>If you end up with two L shaped ways (or one U shaped way and one line), you can merge them by selecting one way, then Shift-clicking on the other way, then click Merge these lines (shortcut C).</li>
<li>Add your tags, and you should be done...with that one :)</li>
</ol>

In iD, you can "create" a new node on a way by grabbing one of the way's midpoints (they are slightly greyed out nodes, when you hover over one a green + appears next to the cursor), and dragging it to where you want the new node to be on the way.
