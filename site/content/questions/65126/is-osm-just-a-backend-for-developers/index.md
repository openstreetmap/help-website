<div class="answer-body">
<p>The primary focus of the OSM project was always to create free maps. Do also note that OSM is a non-profit that quite simply does not have the funds to do what Google Maps etc are doing.</p>
<p>Also TripAdvisor &amp; Facebook are by far not the only users of OSM Maps. Much more interesting to many contributors is the ability to put free uptodate maps on <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Garmin">Garmin devices</a> or create updated maps for Open Source navigational apps like <a href="http://OsmAnd">Osmand</a>. Sites like <a href="https://hiking.waymarkedtrails.org/">Waymarkedtrails</a> or <a href="https://cycle.travel/map">Cycletravel</a>, which could not exist without OSM are also quite popular.</p>
<p>In a sense it's quite correct that OSM kinda "is just a backend for developers". That however does not mean it has no use for consumers.</p>
