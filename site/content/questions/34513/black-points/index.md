<p>As far as I know, there's no way to mark <strong>black points</strong> on map. I consider a black point a dangerous crossing, turn or other POI, where have been many accidents. There are institutions that have archives of this points, e.g. in Italy there's the <a href="http://blackpoint.smaniadisicurezza.it">BLACKPOINT project from ANIA</a>.</p>
<p>Categories of those points may be:</p>
<ul>
<li>bad road basement;</li>
<li>road protections absence;</li>
<li>poor visibility;</li>
<li>flooding possibility;</li>
<li>etc.</li>
</ul>
<p>There should be a way to mark those kind of POI.</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>OpenStreetMap always tries to be objective and verifiable. This means that ideally we'd like to map stuff that is visible on the ground - for example, if there's a sign that says "caution, accident-prone junction" then it is perfectly ok to map this (if there's nothing there already, just invent something - maybe <code>accident-prone=yes</code>). Or if your "black points" are similarly signposted, you might invent <code>danger=black_point</code> or something like that.</p>
<p>However, if whether or not something is a "black point" is simply a matter of definition, and a junction may be a black point to a travel advocacy group but not to the local mayor etc. then it is difficult to map it - because who decides what a black point is, and who can independently verify it?</p>
