<div class="comment-text">
<p>As an example, here's the full process that I used to obtain all mapped railway stations in mainland GB with lat and long in a spreadsheet:</p>
<p>1) osmconvert great_britain.osm.pbf -o=great_britain.o5m</p>
<p>2) osmfilter great_britain.o5m --keep="railway=station" &gt; stations.osm</p>
<p>3) osmconvert stations.osm --all-to-nodes --csv="<span>@id</span> <span>@lat</span> <span>@lon</span> railway name" --csv=headline &gt; stations.txt</p>
<p>4) Open stations.txt in your spreadsheet program of choice, and remove lines that don't have "station" in column D</p>
<p>Step (1) is needed because osmfilter doesn't understand pbf (protobuf format) files. You could process as a raw .osm file, but it'd be much bigger.</p>
<p>Step (4) is a sanity check to get rid of a few bits and pieces.</p>
</div>
