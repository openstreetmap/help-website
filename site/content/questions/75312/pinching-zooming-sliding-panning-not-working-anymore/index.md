<div class="question-body">
<p>The pinching / zooming and sliding finger / panning features do not work anymore on the openstreetmap.org web site.</p>
<p>As a town patroller, my colleagues introduced me to that wonderful site. We could locate quickly a street, civic address… This made the job much easier and literally made the difference between life and death situations.</p>
<p>We would typically use the GPS locator and then pinch / zoom, slide finger / pan as needed.</p>
<p>Unfortunately, for the last 3 months, yes, during the pandemic, we were unable to use this tool. These features are broken. Pinching will scale the whole browser instead of the map. Moreover, sliding the finger does nothing or freezes the map.</p>
<p>Gestures are simply broken now.</p>
<p>We can`t use the site anymore on patrol car tablets, nor on the main dispatching station tv touch screen.</p>
<p>It still seems to work with some, but not all cell phones, but the maps are then way too small and it is inconvenient while driving.</p>
<p>We are now using Google Maps instead. Not as cool, detailed or accurate, but at least it gets the job done.</p>
<p>I wish programmers would look at what bug was introduced on this wonderful site that rendered it basically useless, I’m sure, for millions of users.</p>
