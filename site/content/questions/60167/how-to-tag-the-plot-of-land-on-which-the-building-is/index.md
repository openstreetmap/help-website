<p>Hello,</p>
<p>sorry for this question which in the end is so ridiculously simple that the answer must be staring me in the face and I can't see it.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/532130359#map=19/38.83352/20.70667">https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/532130359#map=19/38.83352/20.70667</a></p>
<p>The above is the plot (I'm not certain this is the correct English word) where the building (a public library) is. The plot is clearly defined and has fences and gates that close during the night and have signs with the opening hours etc. Essentially it is a garden with footways.</p>
<p>(This is marginally related to what I am asking, but after some pondering I decided to tag the <strong>plot</strong> and not the building as <code>amenity=library</code>. This is because when the library is closed, the plot is closed; because the address refers to the plot; and because when people say "X is next to the library" they refer to the plot.)</p>
<p>I also have a similar problem with churches. Quite often the churches are on a clearly defined (and fenced) plot. The instructions about <code>landuse=churchyard</code> imply that the church is not part of the churchyard, however it seems obvious to me that there is a plot of land of which the building is a part, not a separate area adjacent to the building. That is, there is a plot of land that consists of building, yard, gardens, parking, etc.</p>
<p>How on earth do I tag this area?</p>

<p>Greetings, if you click on the "i" (information) button on your tag for "library", it states, "A place to read and/or lend books". If you click on the link below this statement, "View on Openstreetmap Wiki", you will see this sentence: "For OpenStreetmap only physical libraries as library buildings and/or as organizations are relevant." https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:amenity=library</p>
<p>Openstreetmap Wiki states, "library buildings", it does not mention building "grounds". Besides, if I want to tag a house, I tag the house building. I wouldn't tag the front and back yards as part of a house. They are part of the property or "grounds", certainly yes, but they are not the house. Church grounds are not a church. The grounds are only part of the church.</p>
<p>I feel if you tag the grass, trees, fences and sidewalks around a church, museum or library, this communicates that the area belongs to the church, museum or library. People viewing the map will understand perfectly. - happy mapping :)</p>

<p>I'm going to go against what other answers say and suggest that what you did is perfectly fine. The generally-accepted method for mapping a school is to put the tags on an object representing the entire school grounds, not on the school building itself (see <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:amenity%3Dschool">amenity=school</a>). I see nothing wrong with using the same method for other amenities if that amenity is more than just a building.</p>
