<div class="answer-body">
<p>OpenStreetMap is essentially just a big pile of data. People do write software and services around it, but because OSM data is essentially open they don't need to "plug into any ecosystem" in order to do that. There's no "developer zone" as such because there's no need to try and persuade people to develop for a particular platform (because OSM isn't one in the same way that e.g. Google is). Therefore, if you're interested in geofencing or routing, just look for independent advice about that anywhere - if it isn't tied into another platform, it'll probably be using OSM data as an example anyway.</p>
</div>
