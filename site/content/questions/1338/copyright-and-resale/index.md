<p><strong>Is it possible to use OSM basemaps to create maps for retail sale and publication?</strong> Yes, absolutely, you can use our data for anything you want, including commercial products. Any work that you derive from OSM must be licensed under the CC-BY-SA 2.0 but that does not mean you have to give it away for free.</p>
<p><strong>Is it possible to copyright a map that you create using OSM?</strong> Yes, absolutely, you will be the copyright owner of the map you create from OSM. However, you are only permitted to use OSM data if you, in turn, agree to license your product to others under the same terms that OSM was licensed to you. This means that while you are the copyright owner, you cannot for example produce a work that has "(c) encompass, unauthorized duplication prohibited" on it.</p>
<p><em>The above reflects the letter of the CC-BY-SA 2.0 license that is in use at OSM at the time of writing. It is possible that CC-BY-SA 2.0 is not applicable to data in your jurisdiction which would render the above advice void and you could do anything you want. Also, <a href="http://www.osmfoundation.org/wiki/License/We_Are_Changing_The_License">OSM is changing the license.</a></em></p>
