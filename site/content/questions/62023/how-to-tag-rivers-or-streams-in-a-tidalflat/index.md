<div class="answer-body">
<p>Not sure I did it right, but you can see some tidal wetlands I did at <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/#map=15/33.6417/-117.8834">https://www.openstreetmap.org/#map=15/33.6417/-117.8834</a></p>
<p>For the areas that have water all the time:</p>
<pre><code>natural=wetland
tidal=yes</code></pre>
<p>For the areas that are exposed during low tides:</p>
<pre><code>natural=wetland
place=islet
tidal=yes</code></pre>

<p>When I've mapped waterways that reverse direction depending on tides, I've used <code>waterway=stream</code> (or <code>waterway=drain</code>, etc., whatever matches it best) plus <code>oneway=no</code>. Like you mentioned, <code>tidal=yes</code> would be a good idea too.</p>
