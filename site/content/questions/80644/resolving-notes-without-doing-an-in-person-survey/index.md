<p><a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Notes">Notes</a> was a great idea! They present a low barrier for contributing—even accepted from anonymous users. Plus it's not just a <code>"This Area Needs Attention"</code> button—it lets the note-taker try to relay the needed information.</p>
<p>It may require more info, re-survey, or new tracks; so that "armchair mappers" can't help with a great many Notes, but that's expected.</p>
<p>But in looking around, some are obviously actionable. Should I? In looking through the wide variety of… kinds of Notes, and <em>quality</em> of Notes, I was left wondering how many of them out there could be resolved without an in-person survey. A note from a registered user is practically a survey, right? We should read them as ground truth if an anonymous user leaves Notes that appear to be written in good faith, right?</p>
<p><strong>Contrived Examples:</strong><br />
• recent Notes entry containing a shop's business details, clearly placed right on a retail area building by a registered user.<br />
• 4 year old entry. "market ggot an atm"<br />
• 9 month old anonymous entry on a rural river crossing, "bridge damaged. closed."<br />
• 3 year old entry, "no turns permitted here from every direction"</p>
<h2 id="are-there-established-best-practices-for-resolving-notes-without-a-re-survey">Are there established best practices for resolving Notes without a re-survey?</h2>
