<div class="answer-body">
<p>If you can see the route, you could zoom in to it and use the Query feature (explained in <a href="https://blog.openstreetmap.org/2014/12/01/new-query-feature">new-query-feature</a>) to see the tags/details on it.</p>
<p>You can't directly download "routes" from the OSM website as gps tracks, but lots of OSM based navigation apps (such as i.e. <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/MAPS.ME">Maps.me</a> or <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/OsmAnd">Osmand</a>) etc will route you along them.</p>
