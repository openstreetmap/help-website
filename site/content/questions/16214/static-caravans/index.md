<p>Assuming they are fairly permanent, ie if they will be probably be in the same place for a number of years, then I think it is fine to map them in OSM.</p>
<p>As for tagging, I think they are a type of building, so can be tagged as such. Maybe something like <code>building=static_caravan</code> to specify what they are. If they have names or numbers, this can be tagged with <code>addr:housename</code> or <code>addr:housenumber</code>.</p>

<p>If you have good aerial imagery it is often fairly clear. Static caravans are usually larger and squarer than a 'normal' caravan. eg in the UK, the legal length limit for towing with a normal car is 7 metres, so any caravan longer than that is probably static.</p>
<p>Plus static caravans are often arranged in neat rows, and lined up with roads or paved areas.</p>
<p>Though still a good idea to check and survey it where possible, to see what is actually there, and whether it has changed since the date of the aerial imagery.</p>
