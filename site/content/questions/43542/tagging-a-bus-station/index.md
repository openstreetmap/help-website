<p>I think that the wiki's just plain wrong here. It's supposed to describe how people map, not tell people how to map - and in this case it's clearly failing.</p>
<p>I'm sure that the "public transport v2" style tagging addresses a genuine need in a few cities with many transport modes, but it fails terribly at "how do I simply map a bus stop" and "how do I simply map a bus station" everywhere else, which is why many people don't use it.</p>

<p>The proposal doesn't explicitly mention you can put bus=yes on a platform, only at a stop_position. Which could be a choice, as the same platform might in fact serve both buses and trams, for example. So the real answer is that the 'new' proposal does not define public transport stations as things which serve a specific mode of transport. Which probably explains why only 14.000 of them exist, while there are 29.000 bus_stations.</p>
<p>I also just noted that most wiki pages state bus_station is out of date, but the proposal does not "replace, deprecate or obsolete" other tags. So I'll leave my bus station as is: amenity=bus_station &amp; public_transport=station .</p>
