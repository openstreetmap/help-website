<div class="answer-body">
<p>A pocket calculator (computer, mobile phone, etc.) should do it. A minute is a sixtieth of a degree, so take the decimal part of the position in degrees and multiply it by 60 to convert it to minutes. Thus in the example that you gave, latitude <em>42.358372°</em> becomes <em>42° (0.358372 x 60)'</em>, or <em>42°21.502'</em>.</p>
<p>Next, you need to know that negative latitude means South of the equator, and negative longitude means West of the Greenwich meridian. That is simply a convention adopted by all geographers.</p>
<p>So your position becomes <em>N42°21.502' W8°51.485'</em>. That is the format most commonly used for navigation at sea, where it is useful because a minute of latitude is a nautical mile. The same format is used for geocaching.</p>
<p>You can, if you wish, convert the minutes to minutes and seconds in the same way, as a second is simply a sixtieth of a minute. Expressed in that way, the position that you used as an example is <em>N42°21'30" W8°51'29"</em>.</p>
<p>It's in the coastal waters off North West Spain.</p>

<p>How hard could it be?</p>
<pre><code>sLat = (lat -lat%1) + &quot;° &quot; + (lat%1)*60 + &quot;&#39; &quot; + (lat&gt;0?&quot;N&quot;:&quot;S&quot;)
sLon = (lon -lon%1) + &quot;° &quot; + (lon%1)*60 + &quot;&#39; &quot; + (lon&gt;0?&quot;E&quot;:&quot;W&quot;)</code></pre>
<p>It is not like you need <a href="http://proj4js.org/">proj4js</a> or anything when you are just converting the display style.</p>
