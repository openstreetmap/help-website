<div class="answer-body">
<p>Routes are nearly always added as relations. This is not complicated: relations are simply lists of other OSM elements. In the case of routes there is not really a lot of additional semantics so the whole thing is easy to understand.</p>
<p>So to create your route relations you simply add the ways that already exist in OSM to the relation and give is the proper type and name: <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Relation:route">https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Relation:route</a></p>
<p>Once you are finished you should be able to view your handiwork on <a href="http://waymarkedtrails.org/">http://waymarkedtrails.org/</a></p>
<p>While JOSM clearly has the best relation support, I suspect adding a route is quite easy with iD too</p>

<div class="comment-text">
<p>You are using iD which is the standard browser embedded editor.</p>
<p>I just quickly checked how to best do this:</p>
<ul>
<li>select a way that you want to add to the route</li>
<li>down at the bottom of the form on the right you will find a "Relations" section.</li>
<li>click the "+" button</li>
<li>if you haven't created the route relation yet, create a new relation of the appropriate type</li>
<li>if the route relation already exists, select it.</li>
</ul>
<p>That's all.</p>
<p>Note it is not strictly necessary to have the elements in correct order, it simply helps with finding errors.</p>
