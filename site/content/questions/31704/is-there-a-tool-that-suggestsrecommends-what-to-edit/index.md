<p>With all the tools listed here: Please always keep in mind that tools only show hints what <em>might</em> be wrong. It does not mean that something <em>is</em> wrong. Please do not <span>map for a quality assurance tool</span>. Many tools also have a "false positive" button to hide such wrong hints; some sadly do not.</p>
<p>If possible take care to view object histories, to understand why something is the way way it is. You may find hints to systematic bad edits or to a user who may just needs some communication/help to learn how to do it better in future.</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>You are likely asking the wrong question. The great strength of OSM is that the data is gathered by local mappers on the ground, on location.</p>
<p>So the first and most important thing to check: is the area around me mapped correctly and is it complete? The result will depend very much on where you actually live and work, and could mean mapping from scratch to just adding details.</p>
<p>The size of the area you want to take responsibility for is up to you, but do be the steward for a piece of the world.</p>
<p>I could write a lot more on the topic, but I believe that the above is really the important bit.</p>
</div>

<p>There are many <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Quality_assurance">Quality Assurance</a> tools available for OSM. Just to name a few:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Keep_Right">Keep Right</a></li>
<li><a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/OSM_Inspector">OSM Inspector</a></li>
<li><a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Notes">Notes</a></li>
<li><a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Osmose">Osmose</a></li>
</ul>
<p>There is also a nice plugin for JOSM available, <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Quality_Assurance_Tools_script">Quality Assurance Tools script</a>, which offers a nice in-editor interface for multiple quality assurance tools.</p>
