<div class="answer-body">
<p>Assuming you don't want to use the <a href="https://www.mapiful.com/editor/location/">create your own</a> option on that website...</p>
<p>There is a collection of tools on the wiki page <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/OSM_on_Paper">OSM on Paper</a> although it isn't necessarily complete or up to date.</p>
<p>Mapbox studio is said to allow fairly extensive customisation, but there is a five print limit on the free tier and you will be expected to pay if it is for commercial use.</p>
<p>For a one-off, your best approach might be to get an <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/SVG">SVG export</a> of your area of interest from a suitable extract service and customise/tweak the line styles in that until you are pleased with the result. <a href="https://extract.bbbike.org/">Bbbike's extract service</a> has <a href="https://extract.bbbike.org/extract-screenshots.html#svg">some styles</a> that may be of use if you hide the labels and simplify the colour scheme. If you have used <a href="https://inkscape.org/">Inkscape</a> or another piece of vector graphics software I would suggest trying this approach first.</p>
