<div class="answer-body">
<p>How I understand it, you want to be able to enter the latitude/longitude inside the search box (at <a href="http://osm.org">http://osm.org</a>) and get brought there. <del>At the moment this is not possible.</del> You can do this by typing your coordinates into the search box, like this: "50.6950 13.3829", where 50.6950 is your latitude and 13.3829 is your longitude. Another way is to type them directly inside the URL:</p>
<pre><code>http://osm.org/#map=&#39;zoomlevel&#39;/&#39;latitude&#39;/&#39;longitude&#39;</code></pre>
<p>Replace the stuff inside the apostrophes with the following:</p>
<ul>
<li>'zoomlevel' = something between 0 (no zoom) and 19 (highest zoom)</li>
<li>'latitude' = the latitude you want</li>
<li>'longitude' = the longitude you want</li>
</ul>
<p>If this is not what you want, you have to explain your question better.</p>
<p>Edit: Thanks for Tordanik for pointing out that you can actually search for latitute/longitude inside the search box.</p>
