<td><div class="item-right">
<div class="answer-body">
<p>There's <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Good_practice#Map_what.27s_on_the_ground">a section in the "good practice" wiki page</a> that says what is a good thing to map and what isn't. If something's a physical business location (shop or office for example) that also happens to have a website and also happens to accept bitcoin, then it's perfectly sensible to map these in OpenStreetMap.</p>
<p>However, if the business is entirely virtual, and doesn't have a physical location that can be verified by other mappers by walking past, then it's not a good idea to map them. There have been examples of entirely virtual businesses adding themselves to OSM in the middle of a public square in London (because they're based in London, although not obviously in that square) and then getting deleted because in the physical world they simply don't exist.</p>
<p>OpenStreetMap isn't primarily a directory of businesses (either bricks-and-mortar or web only) - it's a record of things that exist in the physical world and are <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Verifiability">verifiable</a> by other mappers. This obviously includes verifiable bricks-and-mortar businesses, but not web-only ones.</p>
<p>Something that would NOT be valid to add to OSM would be something that wasn't a physical business, and was just advertising a web page of bitcoin services. However, that sort of spam gets deleted fairly quickly when local mappers notice it.</p>

<p><span><span>@bitkoin</span></span> Yes (provided they're verifiable to other mappers of course), but with a couple of caveats.</p>
<p>Any attempt at SEO via OSM tags will generally be frowned upon by other mappers (OpenStreetMap isn't really an "organisation", it's just "lots of people mapping things").<br />
</p>
<p>If you add tags that say that shop X is the best place to buy Y in a particular area, then that's pretty spammy and another mapper might change it, but if you just add shop X as a type of shop that sells Y, then that should be fine.</p>
