<div class="answer-body">
<p>In case you did not know: OpenStreetMap is basically just a collection of geodata, many maps and services are made of it. So there may exist other maps based on OSM which are suitable for visually impaired users.</p>
<p><span>Section "Blind" on the wiki page Accessibility</span> will help help you to find more information on compatibility with screenreaders. For example the prototype of <a href="http://www.amauro.map.at/">amauro Map</a> outputs map info as text which may be accessible by the Talkback screenreader. But it seems to be really only a prototype with limited coverage (Vienna?!).</p>
<p>There is also the other help question about "<a href="/questions/17461/">osm.org-for-users-with-visual-impairment</a>" with answers pointing to available options - however, mainly targeted at users with non-total impairment.</p>
