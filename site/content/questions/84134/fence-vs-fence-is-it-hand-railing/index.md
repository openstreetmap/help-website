<div class="answer-body">
<p>The two features <code>barrier=fence</code> and <code>barrier=handrail</code> are two different things.</p>
<p>The tag <code>barrier=handrail</code> is described with pictures <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:barrier%3Dhandrail">here</a>. A handrail is a rail that is designed to be grasped by the hand so as to provide safety or support.</p>
<p>A fence is a freestanding structure designed to restrict or prevent movement across a boundary, described <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:barrier=fence">here</a>.</p>
<p>The key <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:fence_type">fence_type</a> is used to describe the material that the barrier is made of. It could be wire, wood, barbed wire etc.</p>

<p>The wiki photos show the problem exactly. Example 3, 6, 7 do not look they fit the definition. They are more like a fence. For others, the tag mixes up railings with tops you can hold, and additional handrails attached to the side of a wall, fence, or other obstacles. In <a href="https://github.com/gravitystorm/openstreetmap-carto/issues/4297">https://github.com/gravitystorm/openstreetmap-carto/issues/4297</a> the usage reviewed seems to suggest most of them are more of a railing.</p>
<p>A lot of <code>fence_type=</code> duplicates <code>material=</code> and occupies the tag, which the <code>fence:type=</code> proposal had hoped to resolve (inactive, author not free). The wiki currently suggests considering such an alternative. Removing the conflict allows tagging <code>=railing</code>, <code>=bars</code>, and other forms to describe the structure better.</p>
