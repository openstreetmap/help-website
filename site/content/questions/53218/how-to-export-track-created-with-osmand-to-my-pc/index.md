<div class="answer-body">
<p>I guess you are asking about the trip recording plugin?</p>
<p>After saving the track in OsmAnd (REC -&gt; Stop or My Places -&gt; Tracks -&gt; Current track -&gt; Save) the track is listed under My Places -&gt; Tracks. Next to each track there is a three-dots-menu with a share button.</p>
<p>The saved GPX track is also stored in your OsmAnd data folder. In my specific case (I'm using the external sd card for OsmAnd) the saved tracks are located under <code>/storage/&lt;SD-Card-ID&gt;/Android/data/net.osmand.plus/files/tracks/rec/</code>.</p>

<p>SOLVED (in my case): For some reason the folder naming conventions and routes to finding the Osmand tracks on an Android device seem to vary between Android devices, Android versions, and access routes (USB cable, wireless, Bluetooth).</p>
<p>To find the Osmand .gpx tracks on my Samsung Galaxy S7+ tablet (Model SM-T970 running Android 13 and Osmand Plus) I had to connect the tablet to my PC (Windows 11) with a USB cable and use the PC's Windows Explorer with the address <strong>This PC\My Galaxy Tab S7+\Internal storage\Android\data\net.osmand.plus\files\tracks\rec</strong> (exchange the "My Galaxy Tab S7+" with the name of your tablet). From there I was able to upload the .gpx files, e.g. to OSM.</p>
