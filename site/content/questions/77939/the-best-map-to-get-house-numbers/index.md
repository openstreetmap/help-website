<div class="answer-body">
<p>I think there have been discussions on the Talk-GB mailing list about some of the older out of copyright OS maps containing housenumbers, with varying degrees of accuracy.</p>
<p>Personally, I find <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/StreetComplete">StreetComplete</a> to be the best way to capture building numbers on foot if the buildings have already been traced.</p>
<p>Photo mapping can also be quite effective for "driveby" surveys if you use a service like <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Mapillary">Mapillary</a> or tie timestamped photos back to a separate GPS trace after the fact. JOSM's is quite good for this.</p>
<p>For one-offs on the go, <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/OsmAnd">OsmAnd</a> isn't bad, but can only add addresses as nodes if the building doesn't have anything to make it stand out as a POI.</p>
<p>If you want to go full on "high viz and clipboard" I think <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Field_Papers">Field Papers</a> is still a thing too.</p>

<p>There is an old, out of copyright OS map available as a TMS (can be added to JOSM or iD) from the National Library of Scotland (NLS). It has housenumbers on it for houses present in the 1940s/50s for London and a lot of the south east. It is completely accurate in my part of east London.</p>
<p>But it is NOT available for OSM use until the end of 2022, which is a pity. But check back in two years!</p>
