<p>Hi Michael,</p>
<p>sounds like a nice idea, indeed we had some <strong>earlier approaches</strong> to make use of OSM in games or gamification concepts: <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Games">http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Games</a></p>
<p>Yes, it's <strong>absolutly legal</strong> to make use of OSM, but of course you need to <a href="wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Legal_FAQ">respect our open license</a>. Also I suggest to make a prominent hint/attribution to OSM, maybe with a "thank you", as a crowdsourcing project has also some <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/User:!i!/What_a_company_should_never_do">social aspects</a> and check if you might release your game/libs/... as open source to give something back to the community :)</p>
<p>But please pay attention that your gamers <strong>don't start editing OSM</strong> to create levels or to do any cheating. This is usually one of the fears of our community if games make use of our data and the gaming community doesn't understand that we collect real world data.</p>
