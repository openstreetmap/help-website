<p>Hello,</p>
<p>There is a discussion going on on <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/changeset/48588674">this changeset</a>.</p>
<p>Basically, this is a mini_roundabout but there are elevated physical features on the ground already in the database, from a high accuracy source:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/450543509">https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/450543509</a></li>
<li><a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/450543531">https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/450543531</a></li>
<li><a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/450578115">https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/450578115</a></li>
<li><a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/450543543">https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/450543543</a></li>
<li><a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/450543520">https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/450543520</a></li>
</ul>
<p>So the question is how to settle the case, as the roads could be drawn going straight to the only node at the center of the mini-roundabout, thus crossing the traffic_calming features (which are also used by pedestrians when crossing).</p>
<p>The <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Talk:Tag:highway%3Dmini_roundabout">wiki discussion page mentions area=yes</a>, but it seems not widespread, as well as having a circular way closed as a mini_roundabout.</p>
<p>In case you want, here is a <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Switzerland/Datasources">good aerial photography</a>: <code>tms[20]:</code><a href="http://osmdata.asitvd.ch/tiles/lausanne2012/%7Bzoom%7D/%7Bx%7D/%7By%7D.png"><code>http://osmdata.asitvd.ch/tiles/lausanne2012/{zoom}/{x}/{y}.png</code></a>.</p>

<p>mini roundabouts in the legal and OSM sense don't really exist in Switzerland, what does exist are traffic calming features that look like mini roundabouts. As always in OSM more detail is quite permissible and given what I've already said I can't see a reason to replace the existing detail with a simplified and potentially wrong modelling.</p>
<p>Notes:</p>
<ul>
<li>you should be asking the question on the Swiss mailing list (talk-ch) , not here</li>
<li>mapillary imagery of the area in question would be helpful in determining what is really there</li>
</ul>
