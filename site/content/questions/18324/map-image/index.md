<div class="answer-body">
<p><a href="http://warper.geothings.net">Geothings Map Warper</a> lets you upload your own image, add control points, then see the resulting image warped and tiled and layered over an OpenStreetMap background.</p>
<p>If you want to run such a process on your own machine the command line GDAL tools may be the best option. I believe this geothings web app actually makes calls to GDAL behind the scenes too.</p>
<p>...but I'm sure there are other alternatives to GDAL. Maybe this <a href="http://gis.stackexchange.com/questions/4230/rubber-sheeting-in-grass-or-qgis">question on gis.stackexchange: Rubber sheeting in GRASS or QGIS</a> will help. Let us know what solution you come up with!</p>
