<p>Hi,</p>
<p>I am a volunteer at a food bank. We have 4 locations and would like to publish these locations on the back of an A5 flyer, so that people can easily find us.</p>
<p>I have found that the "Transport Map" is ideal for our purpose, as it depicts bus routes, numbers and stops. I would like to get rid of lakes, shading, buildings and train lines, (in other words to have "no fill" if that makes sense) as I am printing in black ink onto a red sheet of paper and all of these things just add clutter.</p>
<p>All I want is just the roads with street names, the bus routes, bus stops and the route numbers.</p>
<p>Is there an easy way of doing this or do i need to use photoshop?</p>
<p>I have room of about 4 x 5 cm for each one of the 4 maps on the flyer, in which to depict an area of about 1 sq km each.</p>
<p>Many thanks for your help.</p>
<p>Tim</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>Likely the simplest way to do what you want is with maperitive see <a href="http://maperitive.net/">http://maperitive.net/</a></p>
<p>Download the OSM data for the area you are interested in (if the areas are reasonably small you can do that via the export function on the map page) and modify one of the maperitive styles to fit your use.</p>
<p>Just in case it is not clear (we have this happen now and then): de not remove objects from the database that you don't want displayed!</p>

<p>You will not be able to make your own version of the <span>Transport Map</span> style (and render a map version with it) since "The stylesheet is not shared openly."</p>
<p>You could use a style offered at <a href="http://maps.cloudmade.com/">CloudMade Maps</a> or make/derive your own there (style editor).</p>
<p>You also could <span>render your own map</span> - e.g. with Maperitive (mentioned by Simon).</p>
