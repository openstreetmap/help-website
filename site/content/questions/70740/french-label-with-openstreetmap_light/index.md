<p>You'll need to contact the company that provides the layer that you want to use. There are a couple of options:</p>
<ul>
<li>They may already make that layer available with different language labels on a different URL.</li>
<li>The style may be public, allowing you to create a modified version of it with English labels.</li>
<li>You may be able to pay them to create the style you want.</li>
</ul>

<blockquote>
<p>But, Is there an openstreetmap layer with only the labels, without the background ??</p>
</blockquote>
<p>I'm not aware of one. The problem that you'd typically get with combining two layers is label collisions (see the comments on <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/user/SomeoneElse/diary/47007">https://www.openstreetmap.org/user/SomeoneElse/diary/47007</a> for some discussion). I guess that you could render <em>all</em> the text on one layer and <em>all</em> of everything else on another, but it's not something that I've seen anyone try.</p>
