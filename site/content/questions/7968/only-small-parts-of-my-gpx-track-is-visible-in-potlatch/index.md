<p>It looks to me as you might have bad satelite coverage, and the GPS is splitting up your track. That means that the parts of the track you do not see is parts where the GPS does not have a fix.</p>
<p>You can try opening the GPX file in another program like JOSM, Merkaartor or MapSource and see if any of those have the same problem.</p>
<p>If that it is a problem you might want to find a better place in the car to lay the GPS (wind screen, sun roof, outside, ...), let the GPS get a good fix before mapping or just be aware of the problems with the coverage.</p>

<td><div class="item-right">
<div class="answer-body">
<p>It turns out that Potlatch chops up tracks when the points are more than a certain distance apart. The workaround for me was to interpolate the affected tracks using GPSBabel:</p>
<pre><code>gpsbabel -i gpx -f chopped.gpx -x interpolate,distance=0.095k -o gpx -F fixed.gpx</code></pre>
<p>I posted some more details about the problem on <a href="http://rubli.info/t-blog/2011/11/24/fixing-gps-tracks-chopped-up-by-openstreetmaps-potlatch/">my blog</a>.</p>
