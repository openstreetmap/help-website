There are broken and smashed up stone steps in the very steep bank coming down from the top and a lot of loose stone / scree; but once you arrive down at the big waterfall, you have to rock climb, out, over and down a very large rock face which is about 12 to 18 metres high, there are no steps and no path. Tthere is no way that a footpath should be showing running all the way through this Scar. How is this corrected please?</p>
<p>MapswithMe which uses this data, has a solid red line up to Gordale Scar, then a dotted blue line through the climbing section mentioned above, then a dotted red line to the top of the scar.</p>

<p>OpenStreetMap data in this location appears to be correct in that there is a public footpath (designation = public_footpath) along that route. Ordnance Survey mapping agrees.</p>
<p>If the path is not maintained to your satisfaction, don't shoot the messenger. Instead, you should complain to the Rights of Way service for the area in question. Malham is in North Yorkshire so you should contact the <a href="http://www.northyorks.gov.uk/index.aspx?articleid=3041">RoW team at North Yorkshire County Council</a>.</p>

<p>If you are going walking in the hills, you need a map for that purpose. General maps derived from OSM are definitely only suitable as a guideline. There are specialist maps on Garmin for Mountain biking, there is no reason why this should not be possible for hill walking.</p>
<p>As others have said the Ordnance Survey map does not provide more info about the path at Gordale Scar (although contours &amp; other detail suggest it might be not be straightforward). This is true of other places in the UK: Lords Rake, Jack's Rake, Crib Goch, the Bad Step in the Cuillin. All are just marked as paths.</p>
