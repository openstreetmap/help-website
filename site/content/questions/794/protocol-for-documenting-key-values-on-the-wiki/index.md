<td><div class="item-right">
<div class="answer-body">
<p>Let me first put the role of the wiki in perspective. Anyone is allowed to use any tag (and tag value) they see fit in OpenStreetMap, whether these are documented on the wiki or not. Software that uses OSM data - such as the renderers that make our maps - are free to evaluate or ignore any tags (and tag values), whether these are documented on the wiki or not. The wiki is a good platform for documenting things but it is in no way mandatory.</p>
<p>The wiki is widely seen as documenting keys that are in (relatively widespread) use, rather than tags that someone thought might come in handy some time. So if "duckboard" is commonly used then feel free to add it, and perhaps put your reasoning in the change comment.</p>
<p>If, on the other hand, it is only you and two others from the mailing list who think that this tag might be nice, then don't add it right away. Instead, use it (and convince others to use it) until it becomes common, and then add it.</p>
<p>You may create a proposal (<a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Creating_a_proposal">see the Wiki</a>) to discuss your ideas, or you may post on the <a href="http://lists.openstreetmap.org/listinfo/tagging">tagging mailing list</a> to do the same. Such a discussion is the right way to find the answer to questions like "would boardwalk be better than duckboard?". If you want, you can hold a vote after your proposal has matured. If your proposal is accepted then it is a little bit more likely for others to use your new tag value; however, an accepted proposal does not automatically mean the tag will be used (or even supported by renderers and editors), and a rejected proposal does not mean the tag may not be used. Under no circumstances should voting results be used as a justification for any kind of large-scale edit of the database (as in "the voters have rejected surface=boardwalk so I will remove it from all objects").</p>

<td><div class="item-right">
<div class="answer-body">
<p>The first step is to search the wiki thoroughly for existing tags that describes the same concept.</p>
<p>The second step is to <a href="/questions/1021/where-can-i-see-which-tags-are-in-active-use">search for undocumented tags</a> that describe the same concept. It may not always be possible to deduce what those undocumented tags are being used for, especially if the none of the tagged objects are physically near you. So this step is really optional.</p>
<p>In this case the new tag seems to be a trivial extension of an existing scheme. But, if you are still unsure, you can ask your question on the <a href="http://lists.openstreetmap.org/listinfo/tagging">tagging mailing list</a>.</p>
<p>After that you can <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:surface%3Dduckboard">start a new page</a> for your tag. Doing so will prevent many (semantic) problems like honomyns, tags based on words that are not specific enough and acronym namespace conflicts.</p>
<p>A wiki is a remarkably powerful tool for collaboration. If experienced OSM users see a problem with your tag (e.g. being a duplicate of an established tag), they can <a href="/questions/1021/where-can-i-see-which-tags-are-in-active-use">easily see how many times it has been used</a> and take appropriate action. For example, they can add a message to your wiki page.</p>
