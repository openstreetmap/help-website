<p>Hi,</p>
<p>We have ~100 geodesic towers around the country, 10-20 m high concrete tubes with steps inside.</p>
<p><code>man_made=tower</code> is sure, one may also add <code>tower:construction=freestanding</code> and <code>material=concrete</code>.</p>
<p>But what <code>tower:type</code> should be used? Their main purpose is to serve as a reference for the national surveyor grid - either to be aimed at by theodolites, also as a base point (at ground level). Only a few of them are open to the public, enabling the <code>observation</code> value.</p>

<p>They definitely look like a tower.</p>
<p>Though their primary purpose is survey, I cannot put two <code>man_made</code> tags on them. For the laymen, their most significant characteristic is that they are towers. And to my understanding a <code>survey_point</code> may be a small concrete heap, or a metal tripod, or even a steel bolt driven into the surface. These objects, however, are the most important part of the country's reference grid.</p>
<p>And, to complicate matters, some of them are used as viewpoints. <a href="https://hu.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cs%C3%B3v%C3%A1nyos#/media/F%C3%A1jl:Csovanyos2014_12.jpg">This one was extended for tourists, for example</a> You can see the original concrete tube in the center, maybe the steel tripod on the top as well. The wooden frame was added later.</p>
<p><a href="https://help.openstreetmap.org/users/387/someoneelse">@SomeoneElse</a>: I've checked TagInfo before asking this question. If I had found any suitable candidates, I wouldn't ask it here. <code>tower:type=monitoring</code> was looking promising until I've seen what it's being used for.</p>
