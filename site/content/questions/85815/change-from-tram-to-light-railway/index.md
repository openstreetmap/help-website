<p>I think I know why this is. The station was previously incorrectly classified as <code>light_rail</code>, Roslagsbanan is narrow gauge "smalspår", but it is still (to my knowledge) a regular commuter train (even though SL calls it "Light rail" in English), not a higher-standard tram which is essentially what light rail is. I improved and corrected the map along Roslagsbanan about a month ago, but I forgot the last stations (Ekskogen to Kårsta) which I have now fixed and all of them are classed as train stations. If you fetch the latest map data it should hopefully only show as train and not as tram/light_rail.</p>

<p>What do you mean? It is on a <code>railway=narrow_gauge</code>, and is already a <code>station=light_rail</code>. As prompted, the only possible addition is <code>light_rail=yes</code>, which however the other stations don't have either. What application are you using? Other edits around are from 2 years ago, so data update should not be an issue.</p>
<p>The only difference is the station on the west has the <code>=station</code> separate from the track. With a <code>railway=stop</code> + <code>public_transport=stop_position</code> but <code>train=yes</code>. <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/9993197481">https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/9993197481</a></p>
