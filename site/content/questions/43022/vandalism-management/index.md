<p>Likely all of this user's edits are either completely made up, changed or deleted existing data. <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/user/drakopineda">https://www.openstreetmap.org/user/drakopineda</a></p>
<p>Example changeset:<a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/changeset/30173761">https://www.openstreetmap.org/changeset/30173761</a></p>
<p>Changing road name:<a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/12842778/history">https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/12842778/history</a></p>
<p>Adding phony lake:<a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/338193859">https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/338193859</a></p>
<p>Deleting a cemetery:<a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/357552495/history">https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/357552495/history</a></p>
<p>Is there a proper way of dealing with this? I think known bad accounts should be on a list and have their changesets reverted. It's a chore to find and go through all these malicious edits and check every change, especially when some are subtle like changing a highway maxspeed to 65 mph when the state maxspeed for that type of road is maximum of 55 mph. <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/12795468">https://www.openstreetmap.org/way/12795468</a></p>
<p>Vandalism will of course increase as OSM grows, and the more we depend on it the less vandalism should be ignored as the problem it is.</p>
