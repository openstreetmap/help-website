<p>Hello,<br />
I am a very “old’ user of Garmin GPS, and I have successively used Etrex vista, 60, 64 and now the new Garmin 66. I mainly use these equipment’s for trail running or long Hiking.<br />
For a long time I used the Garmin Maps and more recently OSM Maps. In the case of OSM maps, I regularly participate to their improvement. I also work personally on the improvement of "typ" files for a good display on my GPS and on Basecamp.</p>
<p>As a general rule, I need maps for France but also maps for France near Switzerland, and Italy. So, to have the nice maps, I used for France, the maps downloaded from:</p>
<p><a href="http://garmin.opentopomap.org/">http://garmin.opentopomap.org/</a>),</p>
<p>and for the borderlines countries, the maps downloaded from:</p>
<p><a href="http://garmin.openstreetmap.nl/">http://garmin.openstreetmap.nl/</a> (ex: maps for Ultra Trail Mont Blanc on GPS )</p>
<p>The main problems with these choices:<br />
– Need some works and time to have good maps (adding contourlines,..)<br />
– Very poor “Typ” file, so you need improvements with tools like TYPWizz or mapedit to have an acceptable viewing on the GPS;<br />
– Not easy to have good DEM data; in general only SRTM data are available. And in Basecamp only the Longitude and latitude are displayed;<br />
– Poor description of polygons;<br />
– Only a few category of paths or tracks (in general all paths are of the same class: no group 1, group 2,..);</p>
<p>Recently, I discovered openMTBmap <a href="https://openmtbmap.org/">https://openmtbmap.org/</a></p>
<p>I immediately realized that the map that are produced by "openMTBmap" are all I have always wanted to have:<br />
– Very good “typ” files and especially very complete<br />
– Complete description of all sorts of polygons,<br />
– Very good DEM data on the basis of comparisons between registered tracks with the new Garmin 66 see: <a href="https://openmtbmap.org/download/dem-relief-shading/">https://openmtbmap.org/download/dem-relief-shading/</a> (Chapter: Contourlines/DEM data)<br />
– In Basecamp, DEM data (Altitude) are all displayed simultaneously with Latitude and longitude .<br />
– Installation process very easy;<br />
- During installation of map and after, you have a choice between 6 layout for different activities (mountain bike, trail, car,..)<br />
<a href="https://www.cjoint.com/c/IIop5TrI1Rl">Here is a link to a PDF file</a>, with Polygons and Polylines examples in the "typ" file, and maps near BELLINGHAM, at different zoom level.</p>
<p>Regards<br />
Gerard C<br />
France</p>
