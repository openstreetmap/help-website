<div class="answer-body">
<p>I would tag that as <code>landuse=disused</code>, <code>disused=quarry</code> and would add (if appropriate) <code>tourism=attraction</code> and a <code>name</code> tag.</p>
<p>There is no need to use a relation to connect the different feature as Openstreetmap is a database of geographic information which are already connected by virtue of their position.</p>
<p>If there are lakes they are probably <code>natural=water</code> unless they were created on purpose then they might be <code>landuse=reservoir</code> and should probably inner areas of a multipolygon.</p>
<p>The processing facilities are most likely <code>landuse=industrial</code> and <code>building=yes</code>.</p>

<p>My one issue with disused/abandoned quarries, that have been left for a long time and hence are now covered in vegetation, is that the choice of tagging becomes a bit binary,</p>
<p>You either have the landuse=quarry be dominant, so the rendering shows it as a quarry OR you have historic=quarry landuse=scrub type of tags, which renders as scrub.</p>
<p>It would be nice to have rendering showing the area being a quarry AND scrub, as both are correct.</p>
