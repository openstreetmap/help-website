<div class="answer-body">
<p>Yes you can. You will need to give a more detailed question to get a proper answer though.</p>
<p>One option is to download a region, then work with that file to extract what you need. <a href="http://www.geofabrik.de/data/download.html">Geofabrik</a> for example has a wide variety of regions and download formats.</p>
<p>If you want just a small area or on demand live data, you could use the Overpass API. There's a very userfriendly interface available. <a href="http://overpass-turbo.eu/?Q=%2F*%0AThis%20has%20been%20generated%20by%20the%20overpass-turbo%20wizard.%0AThe%20original%20search%20was%3A%0A%E2%80%9Cleisure%3Dpark%E2%80%9D%0A*%2F%0A%5Bout%3Ajson%5D%5Btimeout%3A25%5D%3B%0A%2F%2F%20gather%20results%0A(%0A%20%20way%5B%22leisure%22%3D%22park%22%5D(%7B%7Bbbox%7D%7D)%3B%0A%20%20relation%5B%22leisure%22%3D%22park%22%5D(%7B%7Bbbox%7D%7D)%3B%0A)%3B%0A%2F%2F%20print%20results%0Aout%20body%3B%0A%3E%3B%0Aout%20skel%20qt%3B&amp;C=51.05869;3.75153;15&amp;R">Check this Overpass-turbo example</a>. Data can be extracted in several formats through the site.</p>
<p>You'll need to define exactly what is and what isn't green areas, parks, squares, etc. The <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Map_Features">wiki</a> will be of great use there. Squares are a bit difficult, as these are hard to define. You could try with pedestrian streets mapped as a closed way.</p>
