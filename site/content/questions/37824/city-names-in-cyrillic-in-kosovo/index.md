<p>I suppose some of those name=* were changed from Albanian to Serbian language ? The name:sq and name:sr tags should be correct (and you can always use a map rendering that prefers <em>name:sq</em> over <em>name</em> if that's what you can read). Can you provide a link to one place that is wrongly tagged ?</p>
<p>Which language is chosen for the actual <em>name</em> tag is often a tricky subject in multilingual countries. The way to handle this in OSM is a combination on the ground rule (<a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Good_practice#Map_what.27s_on_the_ground">good practice</a> and <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Disputes#On_the_Ground_Rule">disputes</a>) and local mappers consensus.</p>
<p>I'm not sure what the <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Kosovo#What_needs_to_be_done.3F">status</a> is in Kosovo. Those edits might be correct, depending on that status. Try contacting the original mapper (you can find him by zooming in to a village that got renamed, activating the data layer, and clicking on the village node to see the editing history) or the <a href="https://lists.openstreetmap.org/listinfo/talk-kosovo">Kosovo Mailing list</a>.</p>
