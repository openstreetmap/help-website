<div class="answer-body">
<p>You can add the lake and vegetation themsevles. Biodiversity and degradation has verifiability issues. This is more appropriate for an external dataset, based on or linked to OSM data.</p>
<p>If it is signposted or otherwise indicated to the public, <code>hazard=contamination</code> can be used. <code>landuse=brownfield</code> is land previously as other facilities.</p>
<p>In particular, <code>swimming=no</code> may be used if it is not legally allowed at the polluted water body. (but this is not for suitability determined yourself)</p>

<div class="comment-text">
<p>Just one comment: brownfield land can be environmentally very rich!</p>
<p>Typical issues of degradation for land-based schemes could be treated using an appropriate plant_community. For water, although it has been discussed, I don't think there are accepted tags for eutrophication etc.</p>
<p>It is well-worth mapping potential sources of pollution/degradation for watercourses : things like sewage farms, slurry tanks &amp; pits, silage clamps, industrial areas and outfalls.</p>
<p>Probably the biggest issue is that many landuse=meadow areas in OSM will be heavily treated monocultures of agricultural grasses (Rye-grass in the UK &amp; Ireland) which often represent the largest loss of biodiversity across large ares of lowland.</p>
