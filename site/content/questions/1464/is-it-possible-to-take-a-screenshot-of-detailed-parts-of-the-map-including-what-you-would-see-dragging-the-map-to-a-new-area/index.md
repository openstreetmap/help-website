<p>You can use <a href="http://maperitive.net">Maperitive</a>, load OSM data for the city and then export it in whichever zoom level you like. It will produce a single bitmap.</p>
<p>Here's a sample of what I did for Berlin (using a non-standard map stylesheet): <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/breki74/4446075390/sizes/o/in/photostream/"></a><a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/breki74/4446075390/sizes/o/in/photostream/"></a><a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/breki74/4446075390/sizes/o/in/photostream/">http://www.flickr.com/photos/breki74/4446075390/sizes/o/in/photostream/</a></p>
<p>The export-bitmap command: <a href="http://maperitive.net/docs/manual/Commands/ExportBitmap.html"></a><a href="http://maperitive.net/docs/manual/Commands/ExportBitmap.html"></a><a href="http://maperitive.net/docs/manual/Commands/ExportBitmap.html">http://maperitive.net/docs/manual/Commands/ExportBitmap.html</a></p>
<p>If your area and zoom level is too big for the bitmap, you can use Maperitive to generate tiles for a web map.</p>
