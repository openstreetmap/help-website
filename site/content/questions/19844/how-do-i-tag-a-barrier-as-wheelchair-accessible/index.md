<div class="answer-body">
<p>You could tag the <span>gate</span> (should be usually a node of the way) with <span>wheelchair=yes</span>. But probably it is more clever to instead (or in addition) tag the <span>maxwidth</span> in meters (do not forget to add a tag indicating the source (e.g.: estimation of max physical passable width) of the maxwidth or mention it in the changeset comment) at the gate. The wiki page says that the key "maxwidth" is for the legal restriction - would not really apply since it rather is a physical/factual restriction ... I think it is still okay to use.</p>
<p>If the wheelchair accessibility explicitly applies to the whole way then you could tag the way with the wheelchair key. Again, maybe it is more clever to instead(or in addition) use physical tags like: <span>smoothness</span>, <span>surface</span>, <span>width</span>, <span>tracktype</span> (if it is a track), ... That way the info is also useful to all other users of that way (not just wheelchairs).</p>
