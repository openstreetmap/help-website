<div class="question-body">
<p>In the UK it is very common for the word "SLOW" to be painted in white across a lane when approaching areas where a reduction in speed is advised. There seems to be no consideration for this in OSM - not a single instance of slow appears as a tag.</p>
<p>There does seem to be a pretty much unused <code>road_marking</code> key that may be suitable. Another option is <code>highway=slow</code> as the semantics are similar to <code>stop</code> and <code>give_way</code>, maybe. Or just tag it as a <code>traffic_sign=slow</code> possibly with <code>traffic_sign:type=road_marking</code>? In fact, adding <code>traffic_sign:type=road_marking</code> may be beneficial as <code>=stop</code> and <code>=give_way</code> are often just indicated by road markings rather than an actual sign on a signpost.</p>
<p>So, any suggestions?</p>

<p>There is a <code>traffic_calming</code> tag, but that's usually used for <em>physical</em> features that calm traffic, speed bumps and the like. I don't think it would be good to use it for something that's non-physical. (Although there are <a href="https://taginfo.openstreetmap.org/tags/traffic_calming=slow">10 <code>traffic_calming=slow</code></a> tags in the DB).</p>
<p>Maybe you could make up a new tag? <code>traffic_advice=slow</code>?</p>
