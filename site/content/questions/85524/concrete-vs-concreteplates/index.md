<p>What exactly is the difference? Street complete gives me two options which look very similar,</p>
<ul>
<li>Concrete <code>concrete</code></li>
<li>Concrete Plates <code>concrete:plates</code></li>
</ul>
<p>Further the definition isn't exactly clear on <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:surface%3Dconcrete:plates">the tag wiki</a>,</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Heavy-duty plates chained closely together, might have tar or sand in between the connections.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>How do I know whether or not a plate is chained closely together? Does rebar in the form count? Do any two pieces of concrete with sand or tar in the crack count as a plate?</p>
<p>Moreover, the linking from <code>concrete</code> to <code>concrete:plates</code> says,</p>
<blockquote>
<p>For pre-fabricated plates, use surface=concrete:plates or surface=concrete:lanes if you know how the concrete is laid out and one of these tags fits.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Do concrete plates have to "pre-fabricated", which for me means fabricated off-site?</p>
<p>And specifically are roads that are poured one panel at a time, like this "plates"?</p>
<p><img src="https://help.openstreetmap.org/upfiles/maxresdefault.jpg" alt="alt text" /></p>

<p>I'm going to self answer this because I am sure by the description given I'm right. And 99% of people are using this tag wrong. We're given two hints on these tags pertaining <code>concrete:plates</code>,</p>
<ul>
<li>Heavy-duty plates chained closely together, might have tar or sand in between the connections.</li>

<p>See my SC answer with explanation <a href="https://github.com/streetcomplete/StreetComplete/issues/4344#issuecomment-1236169432">https://github.com/streetcomplete/StreetComplete/issues/4344#issuecomment-1236169432</a> why I think this is wrong. One definitely does not need to be a road engineer to use this tag.</p>
<p>Regarding: "And 99% of people are using this tag wrong", is just does not work that way in OSM. If "99% of the OSM editors" is using tag in some way, than by OSM definition it is <strong>the correct way</strong>. And if wiki disagrees with how "99% of mappers use the tag", then the wiki is wrong and needs to be corrected.</p>
<p>As it stands, wiki for those tags is very thinly defined and could definitely use an improvement, but <a href="https://help.openstreetmap.org/users/158/scai">@scai</a> answer is correct one.</p>
<p>Note that while concrete slabs are very often pre-fabricated elsewhere and brought to site (which is why wiki mentiones it, I guess), that is no hard requirement - they can certainly be made on the spot.</p>
<p>TL;DR - If there are regular holes between concrete slabs, then it is <code>surface=concrete:plates</code>, regardless of exact location or engineering method used to produce them. If there are not, and it is one monolithic piece of concrete, then it is <code>surface=concrete</code>. As simple as that.</p>
