<td><div class="item-right">
<div class="answer-body">
<p>You could either tag it as <code>access=no</code> and then make exceptions with <code>foot=yes</code> and <code>bicycle=yes</code>, or you could not place a general <code>access</code> tag and instead prohibit motor vehicle access with <code>motor_vehicle=no</code>. I'd probably tend towards the former. There will always be the odd routing engine that just sees <code>access=no</code> and doesn't look further but that's the exception; this tagging is well established by now.</p>
</div>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>Are the bike lanes and pedestrian facilities physically a part of the same road way as the one under construction or are they separated by curbs or other features? If there is separation, tag the way that's closed for construction as <code>highway=construction</code>, <code>construction=*</code> where <code>*</code> is the original value, and map the separate ways appropriately.</p>
