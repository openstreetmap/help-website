<p>Hello, there.</p>
<p>In forest or woods, there can be transitional areas on which all trees have been cut years ago, and which are merely scrub, maintained or not, with cut-lines or not. These areas are morphologically scrubs, but are maintained to be brought back to a forest; besides, the scrub state lasts for years, justifying its mapping as such, so how to map these areas?</p>
<ol>
<li>Tagging them as both <code>landuse=forest+natural=scrub</code>?</li>
<li>Tagging them as <code>natural=scrub</code>, and include them as an <code>inner</code> member of a <code>landuse=forest</code> multipolygon?</li>
<li>Another way?</li>
</ol>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>I've always tagged those as <em>landuse=forest+natural=scrub</em>, but note the caveat below.</p>
<p>While the <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Forest"><em>landuse=forest</em> vs <em>natural=wood</em> semantic debate</a> is not fully settled, I've always gone with the "landuse=forest is for forestry-like activities and implies natural=wood/landcover=trees unless otherwise tagged" interpretation. I feel this interpretation has become largely dominant, but that may be my observation bias.</p>
