<p>Hello OpenStreetMap-Community,<br />
I’m sorry if I’m asking a stupid question, but I can’t figure this one out by myself. Here is my situation: I am currently making a website about a hobby of mine, or better a guy helps me making this website. I want to show locations on a map where you can do this hobby, and I created a uMap-map for doing so. In uMap I used the Positron tile layout. However, when embedding the map in my website and viewing it on different screen sizes, the map doesn’t adapt, so you can’t see all the marked locations on the map right away without zooming out. Also, when zooming out the markers stay the same size, and on a small screen it doesn’t look good.<br />
So my friend suggested making a screenshot of the uMap, because images are automatically adapted to the screen size. That works well, but before the website goes online I wanted to make sure I’m not violating any copyright issues by doing so. So my question is this:<br />
<strong>Can I take a screenshot of a uMap with the Positron tile layout and put it on my website?</strong> The attribution/copyright information would be put directly under the picture, also with hyperlinks both to the OSM and Carto licensing sites. I can’t figure this one out by myself, sorry…<br />
Thank you for helping me!<br />
T<br />
PS: Also I’m sorry for my English, it is not my first language.</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>If I choose the "OSM Positron (Carto)" style in uMap I get the following attribution in the bottom right corner of the map:</p>
<p>"Map tiles by <a href="https://carto.com/attribution/#basemaps">CartoDB</a>, under <a href="https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/3.0/">CC BY 3.0</a>. map data © <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/copyright">OpenStreetMap contributors</a> under ODbL — About — Home"</p>
<p>I think the "<a href="https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/3.0/">CC BY 3.0</a>" link would be the best place to see what is permitted for that specific license (there is a drop down for a variety of languages).</p>
