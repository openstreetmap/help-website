<div class="answer-body">
<p>Depending on your programming skills, you could use</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="http://umap.openstreetmap.fr">umap</a> combined with <a href="http://overpass-api.de/">Overpass</a> as explained in <a href="http://www.mappa-mercia.org/2014/09/creating-an-always-up-to-date-map.html">this Mappa Mercia blog post</a></li>
<li>or use a Javascript framework such as <a href="https://leafletjs.com/">Leaflet</a> or <a href="https://openlayers.org/">OpenLayers</a> in which case you can still use Overpass to query the data or use a local copy of the OpenStreetMap data.</li>
</ul>
<p>p.s. Your question is rather vague where you got stuck or what you really need (which depends on your skills)</p>
