<p>You can find some topics about your aims if you enter keywords like book, publish, magazine or similar in the search field of this FAQ site.</p>
<p>But be aware: in late summer 2012 we had a change in licence when you publish OSM data. In some manner it also affects when you publish printed maps as a derived work or similar ... this can be under a capable free licence like CC-by-SA.</p>
<p>See <a href="http://www.openstreetmap.org/copyright/en">http://www.openstreetmap.org/copyright/en</a> and all linked pages to get more information.</p>
<p>PS: If you want to display own objects or markers on your printed maps, give us more information in detail as a <em>comment</em></p>

<p>I am in the same situation: I use OpenStreetMap as the background for my scientific results.</p>
<p>Based on the existing literature, the best I found is</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Map data © OpenStreetMap contributors, CC BY-SA</p>
</blockquote>
<p>(Fig. 1 in Vladimir Vukadinovic, Fabian Dreier, Stefan Mangold, <a href="http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/j.adhoc.2012.05.011">Impact of human mobility on wireless ad hoc networking in entertainment parks</a>, Ad Hoc Networks, Volume 12, January 2014, Pages 17-34, ISSN 1570-8705.) for an image with only OSM data.</p>
<p>So when there are GPS points, or any other information on the map, I would go for:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>My GPS points (background: © OpenStreetMap contributors, CC BY-SA)</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Possibly with the link to OSM.</p>
