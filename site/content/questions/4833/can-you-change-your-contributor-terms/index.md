<td><div class="item-right">
<div class="answer-body">
<p>You can't go back and change the contributor terms and the "I consider my edits public domain" for old data. Once you have declared some data PD, you can't revoke this license on this existing data, as someone might have already built upon that data under the terms you have given originally.</p>
<p>One could theoretically change the license for future data, although the system doesn't currently allow that either. So the best/only way to proceed in this case is to create a new account where you don't select the "I declare my data PD" flag and contribute future data under this new account.</p>
<p>It might also be worth pointing out, that it seems some what consensus that this PD-flag has no actual legal status, but is more a general hint about how you feel on this topic. It certainly doesn't have any practical implications at the moment, as all data downloaded from OSM is currently CC-BY-SA licensed and in future will be ODbL, irrespective of what the user has selected as their preference.</p>

<p><strong>Answer</strong></p>
<p>You cannot change your <em>Contributor Terms</em> choice any more. Because the <em>OSM community</em> prefers avoiding people switching back to <em>'Declined the new Contributor Terms'</em>.</p>
<p><u>But do not mind:</u> the choice you selected (<em>Public Domain</em>) is not applied to your contributions. Because your vote is just for statistics as <a href="http://www.osmfoundation.org/wiki/License/We_Are_Changing_The_License#What_Are_The_Choices.3F">explained on OSM Foundation</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Why_would_I_want_my_contributions_to_be_public_domain">OSM wiki gives more details</a>: <em>If you declare your contributions to be in the Public Domain, you are thereby making a statement only. You will not actually be changing the license or what people can do with your data, because database law still overrides the protection of individual items</em>.</p>
<p>Therefore, all your contributions use and will always use the same license as the whole database. Currently it is the CC-BY-SA. And in the near future, your contributions will be re-licensed to ODbL.</p>
<p><strong>Thanks</strong></p>
<p>My answer is from what <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/user/Zverik">Zverik</a> and <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/user/chillly">chillly</a> answered from a similar question I posted on my OSM diary : in <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/user/0liver/diary/17086">english</a> and in <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/user/0liver/diary/17087">french</a>. As you can see, the other three answers say all my contributions will stay in <em>Public Domain</em> (PD), and I have to create a new account: this in not true.</p>

<p>It depends on the jurisdiction—and in particular, whether any rights are infringed by extracting information from the database (either "database rights" per se, or other rights involved in a compilation of data).</p>
<p>As an extreme example, if someone were to map an entire city single-handed - such that the only contribution of "OSM" itself were renumbering the negative IDs (on upload) to positive ones (in the database) - then I believe a public domain dedication would hold.</p>
<p>It's a very complex issue, much more so than a simple "you will not be changing what people can do with your data" admits.</p>
