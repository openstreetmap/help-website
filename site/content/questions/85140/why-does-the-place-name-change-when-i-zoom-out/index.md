<p>So basically Barnstable is two things: <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barnstable,_Massachusetts">a large town</a> with both rural and urban areas and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barnstable_(village),_Massachusetts">a village</a> within the town. <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyannis,_Massachusetts">Hyannis</a> is another village in the town of Barnstable, and is both more important than the village of Barnstable and contains the majority of the Barnstable town offices. The town is incorporated and has well-defined borders, but the villages do not. However, the villages are probably better known than the towns; in fact the Barnstable Town Hall has an address of Hyannis, MA.</p>
<p>It seems that someone wants Barnstable (town) to render at the admin center of Hyannis. In an urban city or town, this may make sense; for example, Downtown Boston becomes Boston when you zoom out, despite the centroid of Boston being far to the south. But in a rural town where the admin center has a different name, this is misleading.</p>
