<p>If you mean the osm.org website, then ...</p>
<p>You should/could add a bug report to <a href="https://trac.openstreetmap.org/">https://trac.openstreetmap.org/</a> ("OpenStreetMap.org Website"). You can login there with your www.osm.org login.</p>
<p>Workaround: To avoid switching on the "notes" layer at the receiver of your message/link switch off the "notes" layer on your side before creating a link. Or remove the "<code>&amp;layers=N</code>" part of the produced link. The notes layer is per default off, as <span><span>@scai</span></span> said in the comment here.</p>

<p><span></span><span>@SomeoneElse</span>: yes. Currently, the user's state of the "notes" layer seems to be saved in the <code>_osm_location</code> cookie (an <code>N</code> added at the end: <code>8.67995023727417%7C50.016743371076984%7C15%7CMN</code> instead of <code>8.679993152618408%7C50.01693640159293%7C15%7CM</code>) which is valid for 10 years per default.</p>
<p>Since the state of the notes layer is (silently) changed if you open a <code>layers=N</code> link and it is saved in a long-time cookie you can infect (somehow) people with the notes layer. It will spread if those people send themselves map links. Yeay, a semi-manual worm! ;-) "Notes" will rule the world soon. <a href="https://trac.openstreetmap.org/ticket/5027">Ticket added</a>.</p>
