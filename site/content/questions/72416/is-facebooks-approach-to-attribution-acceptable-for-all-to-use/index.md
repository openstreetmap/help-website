<div class="question-body">
<p>The OpenStreetMap licence page states:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>We require that you use the credit “© OpenStreetMap contributors”.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Facebook uses OpenStreetmap for all maps on their site, but they do not provide the above attribution anywhere that I can tell. They put an "i" information icon in the lower right corner of the map that, when clicked, exposes a link to the OpenStreetMap licence page. Nowhere on Facebook do they mention OpenStreetMap contributors. The link is simply labeled "© OpenStreetMap"</p>
<p>Is this OK? Facebook has been a gold-level sponsor of OpenStreetMap. Does that confer special privileges for a foreshortened attribution, or is this a practice everyone may adopt?</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>Regarding the question of "is this ok", this site is not the right place to answer the question because there is no clear and objective answer - it depends on whether you mean "legally ok" or "morally ok", and if the former, which jurisdiction you re talking about, and so on. The matter regularly pops up on the "talk" and "osmf-talk" mailing lists (e.g. <a href="https://lists.openstreetmap.org/pipermail/talk/2019-June/082653.html)">https://lists.openstreetmap.org/pipermail/talk/2019-June/082653.html)</a> and has also been much debated on Twitter in the last couple of days (e.g. use <a href="https://twitter.com/grischard/status/1214197202643509249">https://twitter.com/grischard/status/1214197202643509249</a> as a starting point then check up and down the thread).</p>
<p>Regarding "does any amount of goodwill towards OSMF absolve you of license requirements", the answer is a resounding no, because the OSMF cannot make exceptions from the license even if it wanted to.</p>
