<p>I'm not sure what the dilemma is supposed to be, an "Improve this map" link is nice and could be combined with appropriate attribution, but on its own is clearly not a replacement.</p>
<p>See <a href="https://wiki.osmfoundation.org/wiki/Licence/Licence_and_Legal_FAQ#How_should_I_attribute_you.3F">Licence FAQ</a> and <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/copyright">OSM copyright page</a>.</p>

<p>Simon, thanks for the quick reply. Your supposition is not quite correct. The phrase is a usual coverage for adding/mixing in privat data to OSM data (note, not derived data) and has nothing to do with the mentioned link. The mentioned private symbol is like in this real case here <a href="https://goo.gl/HDdvNS">https://goo.gl/HDdvNS</a> the "i" in the bottom right corner. Clicking on that we get a new privatsymbol like here <a href="https://goo.gl/7JaiUA">https://goo.gl/7JaiUA</a> and so on. Eventually, after some clicks we may get a list of notes containing a line indicating the OSM copyright. So, an authoritative yes/no answer to the commitment dilemma is, obviously, necessary and essential for many OSM data users.</p>
