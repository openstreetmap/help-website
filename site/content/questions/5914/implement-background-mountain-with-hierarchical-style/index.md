<div class="answer-body">
<p>We can only guess, from <a href="http://www.openstreetmap.org/user/alltopp/edits">your edits page</a>, what you are trying to do. You are trying to simulate a kind of "hill shading" with different kinds of green for a mountain.</p>
<p>We do not usually collect terrain height information in OpenStreetMap. Instead, if you look at a terrain-shaded map like <a href="http://opencyclemap.org/?zoom=14&amp;lat=30.9106&amp;lon=103.56403&amp;layers=B0">OpenCycleMap</a>, the shading comes from a different data source (usually some kind of SRTM-derived data set).</p>
<p>In addition to that, OpenStreetMap tries to be a collection of facts, facts which are interpreted differently by different programs that make maps. Your editor has such a map drawing component (we call it a "rendering engine"), but various web services will have their own renderers that work to different rules. Just because one renderer has chosen to draw a leisure=park area in a translucent green (effecting darker shades if many of them are stacked), this doesn't mean that other renderers do the same. Also, stacking multiple landuse=park areas is a misrepresentation of the facts (unless perhaps you indeed have parks with multiple stories).</p>

<p>You can have a look at the wiki to see what is <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/SRTM">SRTM</a> and how you could use it e.g. as <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Hillshading_with_Mapnik">hillshading with Mapnik</a>, or by generating <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Contours">contour lines</a>. The linked sites explain various methods and tools to transform and render the SRTM data. Alternatively you could use prerendered tiles from someone else. There is also <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Srtm2osm">srtm2osm</a> to produce osm-ways from srtm-data, but please <strong>do not upload those to OpenStreetMap but keep this data separate and use it at rendering time</strong> to combine it with osm-data in the tiles.</p>
