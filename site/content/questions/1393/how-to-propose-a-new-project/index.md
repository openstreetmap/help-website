<div class="answer-body">
<p>In the OpenStreetMap project you are welcome to map anything you are interested in mapping. There's very few restrictions, only guidelines such as <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Verifiability">verifiability</a> and other <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Good_practice">good practices</a>, as documented on the wiki.</p>
<p>If you want to make golf courses, there are already many people doing this. There is some basic documentation on the wiki at <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:leisure%3Dgolf_course">leisure=golf_course</a>, or perhaps it's better to examine how existing golf courses are mapped, like this one:</p>
<p><a href="http://www.openstreetmap.org/?lat=10.9112&amp;lon=77.02036&amp;zoom=17&amp;layers=M"></a><a href="http://www.openstreetmap.org/?lat=10.9112&amp;lon=77.02036&amp;zoom=17&amp;layers=M"></a><a href="http://www.openstreetmap.org/?lat=10.9112&amp;lon=77.02036&amp;zoom=17&amp;layers=M">http://www.openstreetmap.org/?lat=10.9112&amp;lon=77.02036&amp;zoom=17&amp;layers=M</a></p>
<p>Feel free to improve the documentation on the wiki too, when you have experience of mapping different courses and examining how other people map them.</p>
<p>Finally, if you want to raise the profile of golf course mapping, you may wish to propose it as a "project of the week", which would get it featured on the <a href="http://opengeodata.org">opengeodata.org</a> blog and maybe inspire other people. You can find information on the wiki for <a href="http://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Project_of_the_week/Proposals">proposed projects of the week</a></p>
