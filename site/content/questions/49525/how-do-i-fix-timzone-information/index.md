<p>During a visit to Page in Arizona recently I used Openstreetmap to get around. As usual, I noted down things where map and reality differed, and am now trying to correct the map.</p>
<p>But one thing that beats me is the timezone of Page itself. The timezone of Arizona in general is MST, without any daylight savings time. But Navajo Nation in Arizona does use daylight savings time, so it followed MDT during our visit.</p>
<p>The city of Page is not inside Navajo Nation, but jost on the border. But my GPS showed the time as if it did use daylight savings time. When we crossed the Glen Canyon Dam bridge to the west, the time changed in the GPS.</p>
<p>I've tried to figure out how to fix this. But I can't find out where it is set. I read on the wiki about timezone attributes, but I can't find which object has the attribute so I can change it.</p>
<p>Can anyone help me correct the map. (I'm using the Potlatch 2 editor.)</p>

<p>The timezone information is usually built in to your GPS device, and isn't something that comes from either mkgmap nor from OpenStreetMap. You can test this by removing the map from your device, and see that it will still display timezone information.</p>
