<div class="answer-body">
<p>How very sad. My commiserations.</p>
<p>I am not sure that OSM has a fully described procedure. In a couple of cases where I have known the deceased contributor this has been handled by close family members or friends. I can put you in touch with some of these people if you contact me off this forum (see my OSM Wiki page).</p>
<p>I think the key things are:</p>
<ul>
<li>Inform someone in the sysadmin team.</li>
<li>If you have access to Wiki &amp; OSM accounts, add a short notice advising people not to try &amp; contact the user.</li>
<li>Post notices to relevant mailing lists &amp;/or forum, but only if an active contributor.</li>
<li>Possibly create a automated reply for messages emanating from OSM-based systems to handle the likely case that people don't read profiles.</li>
<li>If possible, change email accounts for the wiki &amp; OSM.</li>
</ul>
<p>Sysadmins may desire more additional information: it is not unknown for online identities to be compromised through people claiming third-parties have died.</p>
