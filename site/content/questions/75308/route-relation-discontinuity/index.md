<div class="answer-body">
<p>If the route is not continuous in the real world, then it's OK to have a gap. Might be useful to mention that as note on the relation. I think this is rare for hiking and cycling routes, but as you wrote this can happen for relations representing roads. Where I map it is not common to create a relation for road-refs, I am only aware for relations for E- motorways. refs like you mention (ref=100) are just mapped as tag on the way itself.</p>
