<div class="question-body">
<p>It used to be possible, on the main site, to right click on the map, and select <code>View Image</code> (in Firefox, or similar in other browsers), and view the tile you'd clicked on. By appending <code>/dirty</code> to the end of the URL, the tile would be put in queue for re-rendering.</p>
<p>This was very handy, though I understand that it could place excess load on the servers if overused.</p>
<p>It has not been possible to do this since early 2017 or late 2016, around the time the nice java-script right click menu appeared (<code>Directions from here</code>; <code>Directions to here</code>; etc). The right click menu can be disabled by shift+right-clicking, but the <code>View Image</code> option is no longer available. Someone, Tom Hughes I think, developed an alternative to the standard java-script right click menu, that had an additional item, "View tile info" (or similar) and even had a demo working (on github, I think). It was a wonderful addition to the java-script right click menu. I'm wondering if anyone knows what's happened to it? Is it being considered for inclusion on the main site? Is that demo still running?</p>
<p>If it's not an option, is there another way to mark tiles as dirty?</p>
