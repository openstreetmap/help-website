<div class="answer-body">
<p>The general tag is <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:leisure%3Dbowling_alley"><code>leisure=bowling_alley</code></a>. You should also tag it with <code>sport=10-pin</code> for US style 10 pin bowls, or <code>sport=9-pin</code> for various different European styles (English skittles, Kegelsport). You can browse places mapped with this tag <a href="http://overpass-turbo.eu/?w=%22leisure%22%3D%22bowling_alley%22+global&amp;R">here</a>. (It may be that many bowling alleys are tagged with <code>leisure=sports_centre</code>, but the more specific tag is to be preferred).</p>
<p>At least in Europe facilities range from large standalone places with 10s of lanes, through small places with perhaps only 8-12 lanes, and then down to single bowling alleys located in pubs. One of the larger ones in Zurich is located in the basement of an Italian restaurant.</p>
<p>I dont think that other forms of bowls (petanque, crown-green bowls etc) in indoor centres are tagged with this tag.</p>
<p>It does not look as if anyone is tagging details of number of lanes etc, but this could be done, something like bowling_alley:lanes=## would be a reasonably clear way of doing this.</p>
<p>Don't forget that many bowling alleys may offer other facilities, such as a bar or restaurant. These can be mapped too.</p>
