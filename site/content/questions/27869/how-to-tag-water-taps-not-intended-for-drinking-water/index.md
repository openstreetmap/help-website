<div class="question-body">
<p>E.g. on graveyards there are water-taps that are used for filling waterings-cans. This water is then used for pouring on flowers etc.</p>
<p>That means, this water does not need to be drinkable (It probable is, but no guarantee).</p>
<p>Currently, I'm using the general "man_made=water_well" for these taps, but that is still a bit off (no well, just a tap). Is there a better way to tag ?</p>
<p>"amenity=drinking water" and "amenity=watering place" also don't fit for this purpose.</p>

<p>I have just proposed a new tag: <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Proposed_features/water_tap"><del>man_made</del>amenity = water_tap</a>. Used together with drinking_water = no|yes|conditional or without it, if the water quality is unknown or unspecified, it would be the answer to the original question. Please consider discussing it at the <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Talk:Proposed_features/water_tap">discussion page</a> or voting for/against it once the voting period starts (in about 2 weeks from now).</p>
