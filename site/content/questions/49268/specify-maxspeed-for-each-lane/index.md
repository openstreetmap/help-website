<div class="answer-body">
<p>Just add the <span>tags</span> as suggested in our docu wiki at <span>Key:maxspeed#Lanes</span> – with any editor. In addition, the standard street form template in JOSM has pre-configured fields for <code>maxspeed:forward</code> and <code>maxspeed:backward</code> (but not for the single lanes).</p>
<p>Bonus: For QA and ease of tagging there are two maxspeed addon map styles available for JOSM, see <a href="https://josm.openstreetmap.de/wiki/Styles#Availablestyleslist">https://josm.openstreetmap.de/wiki/Styles#Availablestyleslist</a> and search for maxspeed. You can install them directly from JOSM's options dialogue.</p>

<div class="answer-body">
<p>The above answer is the simplest. If you will frequently use that tag in JOSM, there are lane templates that I believe have places to enter the speed limit in each lane. <a href="https://www.mapbox.com/blog/turn-lanes-mapping/">Mapbox guide to mapping turn lanes</a></p>
<p>In JOSM, go to Edit &gt; Preferences.<br />
Then in preferences click on the Map Settings tab (The grid with a circle).<br />
In the Map Paint Style button, check the Lane and Roads attributes.<br />
Then click the Tagging Preset button and check the Lane attributes.<br />
</p>
<p>While editing a street just highlight it and go to Presets &gt; Lanes and I think one of those has the maxspeed:lanes preset. Happy Tagging!</p>
