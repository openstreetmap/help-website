<div class="answer-body">
<p>From their website <a href="http://we-travel.biz/wetravel/">We Travel</a> appears to be a proprietary application using OpenStreetMap data. Downloads of data in their proprietary format are therefore entirely at their discretion. According to the OSM wiki page <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/We-Travel">reverse engineering</a> of the application is not permitted in the license. Broadly speaking this question is not really germane to this site.</p>
<p>You may wish to consider other applications which do allow you to create your own sets of map data. There is a wide choice outlined on the wiki <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Category:Java_midlet">here</a>. The normal process for creating your own maps from OSM data involved downloading either the <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Planet">planet file</a> or extracts from the planet hosted by providers such as <a href="http://www.geofabrik.de/data/download.html">Geofabrik</a> or <a href="http://downloads.cloudmade.com/">Cloudmade</a>.</p>
