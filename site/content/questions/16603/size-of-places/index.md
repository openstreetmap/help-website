<p>seems like a "barrio" is either place=suburb or place=neighbourhood.</p>
<p>Is it an administrative unit with well-defined borders? That would be pro-suburb. Is it non-administrative, but noticed as "something on its own", eg. there is a functional centre / sub-centre? that also smells like "suburb".</p>
<p>But if it is more a statistical unit and the barrios do not have any other significance (names are not used / widely known by the people etc.), better make it an admin-level and not a place.</p>
<p>But the small size of only a couple of city blocks sounds more like place=neighbourhood.</p>
<p>I guess, a barrio can be many different things, so it depends on the case.</p>

<p>Districts (boroughs) like "arrondisments" of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arrondissements_of_Paris">Paris</a> or "Bezirke" of German/Austrian towns/cities (like <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boroughs_and_localities_of_Berlin">Berlin</a> or <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Districts_of_Vienna">Vienna</a>) are place=suburb.</p>
<p>Smaller areas are place=neighbourhood.</p>
<p>Both are parts of towns (cities). Those never are "countryside" villages.</p>
