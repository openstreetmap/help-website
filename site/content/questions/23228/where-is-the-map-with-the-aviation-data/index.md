<p>There's sometimes a bit of confusion between "map" and "data".</p>
<p>OSM (despite the name) isn't really a map at all, rather it's a large database of things and their positions. The map that you see by default on the OSM.org website is just one example, rendering only a subset of the data in that database.</p>
<p>There are many other maps that use OSM data - it's quite possible that your iPad app takes data from OSM and renders it itself (i.e. the "map" doesn't exist anywhere else; it's created on the fly on your iPad).</p>

<p>Look in the menu of your app for attribution/map info ("about"/"credits"/…). Nothing mentioned there? Then it may be a own creation by the app authors with OSM data.</p>
<p>The <span>Things We Map</span> section means that only those features should be included into the <em>OSM</em> database. All the other stuff (airspace, …) should not be entered into OSM database. That also means that, as mentioned before, everbody can create a map which is highlighting/showing airports and navaids <em>out of OSM data</em> - <a href="http://overpass-turbo.eu/s/l3">example</a>.</p>
