<p>Simple scenario: a person creates a custom Google map][1] of all playgrounds in the area. The same person finds out about OSM. Can he/she use the KML export to move their work into OSM either by importing it into Potlatch/JOSM or by submitting to an importing tool/work group?</p>
<p>When I say "can", I am primarily concerned with the technical side, although legal side is also relevant, of course. I assume that if a person created a map in the first place, the person is free to export/import that data as they see fit.</p>
<p>Naturally, KML export/import has much broader applications and legal ramifications than the above example. Feel free to point me to relevant wiki, help or forum topics rather than rehashing what may have been argued.</p>

<p>Not necessarily. If you used Google Maps (or other map) to draw from, then your map is a derived work of Google Maps.</p>
<p>In your case, though, if there is only a few playgrounds then maybe the person could just manually add them to OSM from memory? That might be faster and less error-prone than trying to import a KML.</p>
