<p>For disputed territories please refer to the official statement by the OSMF: <a href="https://wiki.osmfoundation.org/w/images/d/d8/DisputedTerritoriesInformation.pdf">https://wiki.osmfoundation.org/w/images/d/d8/DisputedTerritoriesInformation.pdf</a></p>

<p>The names to be rendered are not defined by the administrative borders. Instead they are defined by the name tag(s) on each individual object. Looks like there have been various questionable edits on these cities in the past month and years. Look for example at the history of these two cities and how the name tag has been changed multiple times from Ukrainian to Russian and back: <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/252176252/history">https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/252176252/history</a> and <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/337695202/history">https://www.openstreetmap.org/node/337695202/history</a></p>

<p>The rendering will only change if you change the name tag. This will however likely result in other mappers reverting your changes. This edit war can only be solved by the <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Data_working_group">Data Working Group</a>, if at all. Discussing this here is out of the scope of this help system. You can try to get into contact with the DWG or discuss this issue at the <a href="https://lists.openstreetmap.org/listinfo/talk">talk</a> mailing list. There won't be a solution that makes both the Ukrainian and Russian community happy. This is a political issue OSM can't solve.</p>
