<p>I suggest you user your own judgement. You cannot do a lot wrong.</p>
<p>Personally, I usually draw the bridge part of the highway or rail (<a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:bridge"><code>bridge=yes</code></a>) only along the stretch that is not connected to solid ground. The actual structure (<a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Tag:man_made%3Dbridge"><code>man_made=bridge</code></a>) can be bigger of course and comprise all supporting elements.</p>

<p>Last year we discussed <a href="https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Talk:Tag:man_made=bridge#Extent_of_bridge"><code>https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Talk:Tag:man_made=bridge#Extent_of_bridge</code></a> --- From the example screenshots I thought it is interpreted as for the main span between expansion joints. This is more visible to trace, and a more verifiable definition to follow.</p>
<p>Support structures have <code>bridge:support=abutment</code>, and <code>bridge:support=pier</code>.</p>
