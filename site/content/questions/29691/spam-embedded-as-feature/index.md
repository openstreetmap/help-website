<div class="answer-body">
<p>It is generally ok to delete spam on sight. However if you're inexperienced it can't hurt to enlist the help of a second pair of eyes to make sure the right thing is done. In this particular case, the following happened:</p>
<ul>
<li>an inexperienced other user wanted to add their business to the map</li>
<li>instead of simply creating a node that described their business, they added their business information to an existing object describing a residential area, and removed its "residential area" attribute</li>
<li>then you came along, identified the former residential area as spam, and removed it.</li>
</ul>
<p>A look a the object's history would have told you that it used to be a residential area, and you could have restored the object to its former glory instead of deleting it outright.</p>
<p>I have done that now.</p>
